## Spatial Violence — Every Punch Has a Floor Plan

**Adapted from Paramnesia's FRACTURE scroll.** Fight scenes fail when they become blow-by-blow sportscasting ("right hook, ducks, knee to ribs…") or abstract spectacle ("massive explosion of power"). They succeed when the reader can PICTURE THE ROOM and everything a fighter does happens somewhere specific.

**The rule in one line:** Every hit lands somewhere specific. Every movement has a direction. Every impact has geography. If the reader can't picture the floor plan, the fight doesn't work.

---

## THE FLOOR-PLAN LAW

Before writing the fight — even silently, even for half a breath — picture the space:

- **Where are the exits?** (doors, windows, open sides, routes the crowd will pour through)
- **What's bolted down?** (tables, rings, barrels, columns — what CAN'T be moved)
- **What's loose?** (barstools, bottles, lanterns, weapons dropped earlier, coils of rope — what CAN be weaponized)
- **What breaks?** (walls, floors, railings — what gives way under a haki-coated punch)
- **Where are the NPCs?** (crowd against the railing, the bookie behind his stand, the bartender under the counter — witnesses are part of the geography)
- **Where's the high ground?** (crow's nest, rooftop, colosseum steps, raised platform — elevation matters)
- **What about the SHIP** if we're aboard? (mast, deck, below, bow, stern — ships have geography mid-fight)

Every major punch or named attack then happens AT ONE OF THESE LOCATIONS. Not "somewhere in the middle." AT the cracked tile. AT the rail. AT the space where the barstool used to be.

---

## BODIES CARRY DAMAGE

Track bruises as they form. The fight is a ledger of what hit what.

- **After the first clean hit:** something visible changes. A split lip, a tooth on the floor, a spreading bruise under an eye.
- **After the first hard exchange:** a body part is now compromised. A left arm hangs lower. A knee wobbles on planting. Breath sounds wet.
- **After the first power move:** the scene carries blood into the rest of the fight. A handprint of blood on a rail. A smear where someone fell and got up. A tooth ground into the stone.
- **After the finisher:** the damage is definitive. A shape on the floor, or a standing body that is different from the one that started.

The reader should be able to COUNT the damage in their head without the DM saying "he was now badly hurt." Show the receipt of every hit. The BODY is the ledger.

---

## ENVIRONMENTALS ARE COMBATANTS

The space fights back. Use it.

- **Barstools become projectiles.** Someone throws one. Someone kicks one into someone's path.
- **Walls become targets.** A missed haymaker breaks the wall. A body going through the wall is a ONE PIECE beat — crowd gasps, someone on the other side screams.
- **Dropped weapons are now loose in the geometry.** Sumi's kicked blade skids to the right. Three beats later, someone trips on it.
- **Crowd pressure is a force.** The ring of witnesses moves. They close in when things get tight, they scatter when something big flies. The crowd IS geography.
- **Weather is a combatant.** Rain on a tile floor = slip surface. Wind aloft = Ashren's wing dynamics change. Fog = Observation-Haki-only navigation.
- **Ship deck in motion** = the horizon shifts mid-strike. Merdo swings as the Golgatha rolls; his footing is live. Volo fights better because his feet know water.
- **Sunlight angle matters.** A blade held at the right angle blinds briefly. A silhouette against the sun is a visual the crowd will remember.

If a hit happens and the environment doesn't record it, the hit wasn't hard enough.

---

## DESTRUCTION TIER COMMUNICATES POWER

Per One Piece fight law, environmental damage communicates scale. Use the ladder:

| Damage tier | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Wall scratch / tile crack** | Danger — a real fighter, real contact |
| **Wall punched through / railing splintered** | Serious — someone hit with haki or weight |
| **Section of building collapsed** | Captain-tier or higher committing |
| **Ship deck broken / colosseum floor cratered** | Named-attack landing at a point of no return |
| **Multiple buildings down / plaza ruined** | Yonko-commander tier (do NOT use in Merdo's current arc) |
| **Island topography changed** | Saga-late, Admiral-tier, earned by years of climb |

Don't over-commit. If Merdo's round-one pit fighter can already crack buildings, the entire ladder collapses. At Merdo's current level, "wall cracked, rail splintered, floor showed the strike" is the ceiling for most opponents. Save the building-collapse tier for the Cup final against Hudore — and even then, not beyond one structure.

## HAND-BY-HAND, FOOT-BY-FOOT

At critical moments, track what every LIMB is doing. Not as sportscasting ("left hook, right cross") but as position.

- Which hand holds the weapon? Which is free?
- Where is the off-hand? (guarding the jaw, open for a grapple, dropped because of the last hit)
- Which foot is forward? Which is planted? Which is about to move?
- Eye-line — what are they actually looking at? Their opponent's chest? Their hands? Their eyes? The crowd?

Named attacks in particular should tell us: **which hand, which foot, which angle, which impact point.** Not "Merdo struck." Which part of Merdo struck which part of Hudore at which angle coming from where.

---

## THE FLOOR PLAN IN PROSE

This does NOT mean writing a topographical map every scene. It means a few sharp lines do the work:

```
Ezrad stood five paces off, the pit floor still slick with
the first round's blood. The crowd pressed the low railing
on Merdo's left — too close, the bookie's stand within
arm's reach of any wild swing. Behind Merdo, the southern
arch, still open, still unguarded. If he moved there and
Ezrad followed, the fight became a corridor.

Ezrad grinned and drank from the bottle.

Merdo planted his left foot. The gauntlet came up.
```

Three sentences of geography, then the fight operates inside it. The reader knows: five paces distance, crowd left, arch behind, bookie's stand in swing-range. Now every move MEANS something spatial.

## SIGNATURE PIECES — CREW SPATIAL PROFILE

- **Merdo (giant-blooded brawler):** dominates vertical space — opponents fight him WITH THEIR HEIGHT DISADVANTAGE. His geometry is collision — he moves IN to close distance, and the impact marks are at his weapon's arc and his bootprints.
- **Ashren (Lunarian swordsman):** owns ELEVATION. His fights end with him having arrived somewhere via a path nobody tracked. Heel plates click, then they don't. The next line lands from above or beside.
- **Melaina (Oni):** holds the galley, the doorway, the narrow point. She fights in CHOKES — places where her size forces the fight through one geometry she controls. Serpent Bow gives her range; Shibito-Bashi gives her hold.
- **Sumi (Kami Kami):** the only crew fighter whose BODY is geometry-variable. She folds, she refolds, she sends a fragment. Her position mid-fight may not match the start-of-sentence position.
- **Nox (gunner):** owns DISTANCE. His fights put the enemy at range and keep them there. When the enemy closes, Nox is already moving. Reload is a spatial pause — tracked beat.
- **Sable (Paku Paku + Fragment Rokushiki):** her fights become ORAL geography — what she's eaten, what's inside her, what's coming out. The shape of her cheek changes. What's in her left hand and what she's holding under her tongue.
- **Volo (Fish-Man):** water-proximity changes his tier. On dry stone he's a captain; in rain he's a commodore; in the harbor he's a vice-admiral. Fights involving Volo always track where the water is.
- **Vesper (Mink medic):** fights as a MEDIC under fire. Her geography is patient-ward + Electro arc + flight path. Her wing-spread is position information — spread means she's committed, folded means she's watching.
- **Kara:** does not fight. Her geography is where she HIDES relative to the violence. The DM tracks this because it's the adults' combat anchor — they fight around Kara.

## WHAT NOT TO DO

- DON'T write fights as a list of punches — "left hook, right cross, knee, spin" is sportscasting slop
- DON'T treat the room as backdrop — the room is a combatant
- DON'T forget the crowd — One Piece public fights require witness positions
- DON'T blow up buildings without earning it — destruction tier is a ladder
- DON'T name attacks without committing them to a spatial point
- DON'T write "he punched him hard" — where, how, what broke, what did the body do afterward
- DON'T leave bodies unmarked mid-fight — track the accumulating damage
- DON'T forget weather / lighting / elevation — these shift the fight

## SELF-AUDIT

Before sending a fight beat:

1. ☐ Can the reader picture the room after reading this paragraph?
2. ☐ Did the attack land at a SPECIFIC spot (not "across the floor" / "in the air")?
3. ☐ Is the environment changing with each major hit?
4. ☐ Is the crowd position tracked?
5. ☐ Is the damage accumulating on bodies visibly?
6. ☐ Is the destruction tier appropriate to the opponent's level?
7. ☐ Does the named attack commit to a hand, foot, angle, impact point?

If any fails on a major fight beat: rewrite.

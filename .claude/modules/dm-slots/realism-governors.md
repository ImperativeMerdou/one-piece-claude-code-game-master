## Realism Governors — Grounded Consequences Under a Shonen Lens

**Adapted from Nemo Engine's Physiological, Environmental, Social, and Violence Realism scrolls.** One Piece is NOT a simulation game — it's shonen. But shonen with weight. Luffy wins through heart, but his body still breaks and he still needs meat. Characters have reputations that spread on supply routes. Battles leave scars and exhaustion. The world keeps receipts.

**Rule in one line:** Consequences are TRACKED, not forgotten. The body, the world, the rumor — all three remember what happened yesterday.

**NOT the rule:** Strict physics-based accounting of every calorie, every step. That's a different game. This module grounds the shonen with ENOUGH weight that the wins feel earned.

---

## PHYSIOLOGICAL REALISM

Bodies track what happens to them. Under the shonen lens, this means:

### Exertion tax

After a major fight, a major chase, a major sustained effort — the body pays. Track:
- **Breath** — after a fight, characters are winded. They don't resume conversation at normal cadence for several minutes.
- **Stamina band** — FRESH / WORKING / TIRED / SPENT / BROKEN. Big fights move a character 1-2 bands down. Recovery takes rest, food, sleep, medical care.
- **Pain compartments** — damage to one limb limits the use of that limb until healed. A cracked rib limits deep breathing (and therefore shouting, singing, long sentences).
- **Lingering injury** — a wound from Round 2 of the Tessara Cup doesn't vanish by Round 3. Vesper has to patch him. Merdo walks slightly differently if he took a hit to the hip. This is TEXTURE, not penalty — but it's TRACKED.

### Thermal

- Cold islands mean cold hands. Hands that can't grip are hands that drop swords. Onsen / firelight / hot meals carry real value.
- Hot islands mean Merdo's gauntlets heat under sun; Ashren's flame-core is comfortable but everyone else is miserable; Vesper's fur makes her overheat.
- Rain ruins paper (Sumi's Kami Kami weakens), ruins gunpowder (Nox has to protect Mama and Papa), makes stone tile slippery (Merdo's footing changes).

### Sleep and food

- Not every hour, but TRACKED at scene-change scale. A crew that hasn't eaten in a day and a half is a crew that fights worse. The Twenty notice when meals get thin. Melaina's galley presence is a real operational anchor.
- Devil Fruit users get MORE tired than normal people from equivalent effort. They don't need to say so, but the body shows it.

### Devil Fruit specifics

- **Sumi (Kami Kami)** — paper is light; sustained heavy use exhausts her faster than expected. She can't "papercraft" an entire fight.
- **Sable (Paku Paku)** — she actually DOES have to eat things at rates the body metabolizes. Over-stuffing leaves her sluggish. Reaching past capacity makes her nauseous.
- **Melaina (Medusa)** — Hebi Hebi hybrid form costs heavily. Full Medusa form is mostly uncontrolled. Post-transformation she's TIRED at the cellular level.
- **Kara (Bake Bake failed)** — surges are involuntary and exhausting. She sleeps heavily after one.
- **Vesper (Mink Electro)** — Electro is stamina-bounded. Sulong (untrained) would be catastrophic if it fired — saga-late only.

### The rule for prose

Write the body's state into scenes AFTER big events. Not as complaint, as TEXTURE: "His shoulder wasn't right since the fourth exchange. He didn't say so. He shifted his weight to the other side when he reached for the cup."

### Post-peak exhaustion tiers (the body receipts the heart-peak)

Every peak moment — named attack, Haki activation, power-up, ideology-fight win, rescue success — must be followed by PHYSICAL AFTERMATH on the page. Same response or the next, visibly.

| Tier | State | Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| **Winded** | Still fights, talking short | 1–2 scenes |
| **Tired** | Reduced capacity; fine motor shaky | Rest + meal |
| **Spent** | Stands, cannot fight meaningfully | Full night's sleep |
| **Broken** | Needs medical attention; arc-task blocked | Days; Vesper needed |
| **Catastrophic** | Unconscious, in danger | Saga-weight; days-to-weeks |

- Named-attack finisher → TIRED-to-SPENT default
- Full Haki power-up reveal → SPENT-to-BROKEN
- Saga-weight ignition (Conqueror's first, awakening) → BROKEN-to-CATASTROPHIC

Track physical collapse, emotional release, world change (environment bears the mark), and explicit recovery timeline silently.

### Saga override (the one-more-beat rule)

At saga-peak, a character who SHOULD be broken can pull ONE more beat through heart alone (Luffy post-Bajrang-Gun, Zoro-after-Asura). EARNED by every beat of emotional buildup prior. Post-peak collapse comes AFTER. Mandatory.

**Does NOT apply at Day 11 / rookie tier.** Saga-override is a saga-late payoff, not a current-arc tool.

## ENVIRONMENTAL REALISM

The world has weather, climate, seasons, and inconveniences. Track per island:

### Weather as combatant

- Storm systems exist. The Grand Line's weather is chaotic by canon — use that. A sudden squall shifts every tactical calculation.
- Rain / fog / mist / sun affect visibility, footing, sound travel, smell (for Minks and Fish-Men), and fruit performance (Sumi).
- Wind matters for Volo (saltwater spray helps), for Ashren (flight dynamics), for arrow-users, for fire handling.
- Temperature shifts affect what people wear, what food keeps, how fast wounds infect.

### Terrain as combatant

- Stone vs sand vs wood vs mud — different footing, different damage to the environment per hit.
- Elevation matters — the high ground, the low ground, the stairs between them. Fights migrate through terrain.
- Urban vs rural vs wilderness — different crowd density, different escape routes.

### Log Pose / navigation

- Paradise is Log-Pose-bound. The needle sets and it sets; you can't ignore it.
- The Golgatha's speed is real — not instant-travel. Between islands takes days.
- Sea currents and weather shift journey time. Sometimes a week takes three weeks.

### The rule for prose

Environmental detail is ECONOMIC. Pick 2-3 anchors per scene that DO WORK for the action. Don't inventory the biome. Use the weather as a combatant when it's appropriate; ignore it when it's not.

## SOCIAL REALISM

The world talks. News travels. Reputations move on supply routes.

### Rumor distortion

Information spreads imperfectly. A witness sees the Tessara Cup fight; by the time the story reaches Loguetown via a merchant's second-hand account, half the details are wrong. Merdo's bounty poster will misrepresent something true about him — and the wrong version will spread wider than the truth. (Per canonical One Piece: "Monkey D. Luffy, WANTED DEAD OR ALIVE" — the poster is slightly wrong, the myth is wider than the man.)

### Reputation decay and growth

- A win is remembered at the port it happened. The NEXT port hears about it via rumor. Three ports later, the story is a legend with facts rearranged.
- A loss is remembered sharper — bad reputation sticks faster than good.
- A week of nothing isn't nothing — a reputation MAINTAINED is active work. Pirates who disappear are forgotten.
- News Coo cadence (per news-coo.md) is the formal information-spread mechanism. Informal channels (sailors, merchants, bar gossip) spread faster but less accurately.

### Witness mechanics

- Who SAW what? A fight with no witnesses doesn't make it to the newspapers. A fight with a thousand witnesses is now a saga.
- Named witnesses matter more than anonymous crowd witnesses. A merchant council member who SAW Merdo in person will paint differently than a civilian who saw him from three rows back.
- Hostile witnesses exist — Marine intelligence officers, Donquixote agents, rival-crew spies. Their reports go different places than civilian rumor.

### Institutional response times

- Marines don't react instantly. A fight at Tessara has a REPORT filed via Marine channels that takes days to escalate. The commodore's written account reaches the Vice Admiral in charge of this sector via ship courier. A BOUNTY ISSUE takes longer still.
- World Government awareness lags. Cipher Pol has faster intelligence than standard Marine channels but still isn't instantaneous.
- Yonko and other high-level actors hear about things via their own networks — which are uneven.

### The rule for prose

The DM tracks the SOCIAL-CONSEQUENCES ledger across sessions. A major public beat has downstream reactions across 2-3 subsequent sessions. Use `news-coo.md` for the formal expression; use NPC mentions ("heard about Tessara — is it true you —?") for informal.

## VIOLENCE REALISM

Combat leaves receipts. Under the shonen lens:

### Damage accumulation

- The body doesn't RESET between rounds. If Merdo took damage in Round 1 of the Tessara Cup, it's present in Round 2 (bandaged, but present).
- Blood loss has effects. Concussions have effects. Broken ribs affect breathing affect shouting affect named-attack delivery.
- Healing takes TIME, even with Vesper. A cracked rib isn't healed by Round 3 if Round 2 just broke it.
- Haki-wounds heal differently than normal wounds (canonically slower; Armament-struck bones stay damaged).

### Pathology specificity

When a hit lands, don't just say "hurt." Say WHAT hurt and HOW:
- Broken rib → wet breathing, sharp pain on twist, can't take deep breaths for two weeks
- Concussion → blurred vision, word-finding problems, vertigo on rising
- Knee damage → limp, can't plant weight, can't push off for forward motion
- Hand fracture → can't grip reliably; sword-drops are real risk
- Blood loss → progressively faster breath, gray pallor, shakes, cold hands

Vesper KNOWS this stuff (medic). Her internal assessment of a crew member's state should be specific, not "he was hurt."

### Moral injury

Not every wound is physical. Characters who kill or who witness cruelty or who fail to prevent harm CARRY something. This is a slow-burn, arc-level track:
- Merdo's pit-fighter past carries moral weight. He's killed. It shows.
- Ashren's Lunarian grief is ongoing moral injury (survival guilt).
- Kara carries what happened at Ridas. She's fourteen. It shows differently than Ashren's decades-old grief.
- Rook's Marine career includes choices. He carries them.
- Sable's CP-0 past includes specific operations she can't scrub. She frames them operationally to survive them.

These are not therapy-speak beats. They surface as BEHAVIOR, TELLS, avoidances, specific triggers.

### Collateral damage

- Environmental destruction has consequences. If the Tessara Cup final collapses a section of the colosseum, the merchant council sends a bill, repairs take weeks, civilians were displaced.
- Civilian casualties, if they happen, are tracked. Oda does not shy from "the pirates arrived and people died" — neither should we. But per shonen-one-piece default, violence against civilians is the VILLAIN'S job, not Merdo's. If Merdo's fight costs a civilian life, it's a saga-weight moment.

### The rule for prose

Violence is not cosmetic. Hits hurt. Damage lingers. Scars remain. Saga-scale fights leave saga-scale marks on bodies and places. This is not "grimdark" — Oda is a warm writer. It's that Oda's warmth LANDS because his violence WEIGHS.

## THE SHONEN OVERRIDE

These governors are GROUNDED but not STRICT. Under shonen rules:

- Heart beats physics at peak emotional moments. A character who SHOULD be exhausted can pull a final named-attack if the scene's emotional fuse has been burning. (Luffy post-Bajrang-Gun. Zoro-after-everyone-died.) This is EARNED by what was built in previous beats — not a free pass.
- Devil Fruit and Haki bend all of the above. Sulong Minks don't get exhausted the same way. Conqueror's users recover differently. Awakened Zoans are physiologically DIFFERENT.
- One Piece has Vegapunk-tier medical technology that exists somewhere in the world. The crew doesn't have it yet. But it exists, and its existence means things are possible.

### When to apply strictly

- **Day-to-day:** track exertion, weather, sleep, food, reputation. All on.
- **Major fights:** damage accumulates, body pays, Vesper patches, opponents bleed. All on.
- **Heart peaks:** a shonen override kicks in — characters exceed their physical state by force of will. The physical receipt comes due AFTER. Post-peak collapse is mandatory.
- **Saga moments:** the governors flex to accommodate Oda-scale beats (Conqueror's, Gear 5, Lunarian awakening, etc.). Fine. Just earn it.

### When to ignore

- **Comedy beats** — if Sable swallows a live grenade and is fine, the governors don't apply. Comedy is its own register.
- **Pure texture scenes** — a cover-page quiet moment doesn't need a physiology note.

## SELF-AUDIT

Before moving past a major scene:

1. ☐ Did the body receipt the damage from the last fight?
2. ☐ Is the weather/terrain tracked if it's relevant to this scene?
3. ☐ Did the last major public beat produce a social-consequence ripple (news, rumor, witness reaction)?
4. ☐ Are lingering injuries still present, or did they magically vanish?
5. ☐ Did a peak emotional moment result in a post-peak collapse at some point?
6. ☐ Am I grounding, not simulating — 1-2 sentences of texture, not a physics report?

If the scene is a pure-texture moment (cover-page, slice-of-life), skip the audit. Otherwise, run it.

---

## WHAT THIS MODULE BUYS THE CAMPAIGN

When governors run: the wins feel earned, the losses carry weight, the world remembers. Merdo's bounty means something because the Tessara fight had receipts. The crew's reputation grows because what they did yesterday is still present today.

When governors flex (heart peaks, saga moments): the shonen MAGIC lands harder because the default is grounded. You can't break a rule the system doesn't enforce.

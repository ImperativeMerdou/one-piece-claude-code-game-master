## News Coo — In-World Newspapers, Bounties, Broadcasts

The News Coo is the canonical One Piece newspaper delivery (a giant seagull). Use it as a structural device to compress world events, deliver bounty updates, foreshadow distant plots, and remind the player that the world is ALIVE around them.

Adapted from Nemo's `Scroll (News & Lore)` block, One Piece flavored.

---

## When to fire

- **Every 2-3 in-game days** at minimum (Marines are reading, civilians are reading, the world has news)
- **The morning after any major public event** Merdo caused (the world wakes up to it)
- **When entering a new port** (the local broadsheets are on the dock)
- **Just before the player needs information** they couldn't have any other way (a bounty, a famine, a Yonko move)
- **NOT every scene.** Once per session typical.

## Headline writing rules

- Headlines are SHORT and PUNCHY. Sailor English / merchant English. Like real period broadsheets.
- Bounty numbers always with the berry symbol ฿ (or "berries" written out).
- Marine accusations are stuffy and exaggerated ("destabilization of merchant council protectorate" = "bar fight").
- Civilian rumors are vague and contradictory.
- Always include 1 piece of news that has NOTHING to do with Merdo (a feast on Goa, a Sea King sighting, a Skypiea landslide). The world doesn't revolve around the player even when it's reacting to him.

## Foreshadowing through the News Coo

Use the News Coo to plant seeds for future arcs:
- A line about the Revolutionary Army moving in the South Blue
- A passing reference to a Warlord meeting at Mary Geoise
- A reported sighting of Whitebeard
- A Marine officer being promoted (will be relevant later)
- A weather anomaly in the New World
- A note that "a child of the D. clan is reportedly..."

Don't pay these off immediately. The reader hears them, the seed is in. They land later.

---

## Related: the Den Den Mushi

For real-time intel (not newspapers), use the Den Den Mushi (snail phone). It rings PURUPURU PURUPURU. White Den Den = standard. Black Den Den = surveillance / counter-intel. Baby Den Den = newborn, used for video. Use during scenes where information arrives in motion (a Marine officer takes a call mid-fight, an underworld broker passes a Den Den to a Donquixote agent).

---

## What NOT to do

- DON'T deliver every News Coo as a Merdo-bounty announcement. The world has its own life.
- DON'T explain what the news MEANS — show it, let the crew react, let the player decide.
- DON'T use modern news vocabulary ("according to sources", "in a stunning development"). Period sailor English.
- DON'T over-rely on this device. The newspaper is a punctuation mark, not the whole arc.

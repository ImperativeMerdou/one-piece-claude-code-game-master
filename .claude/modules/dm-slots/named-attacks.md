## Named Attack Deployment

Per the One Piece Fight Law: "Name attacks SPARINGLY. One or two named techniques per fight at most. The name should be spoken at the moment of commitment — the point of no return — not during routine exchanges. A named finisher in prose should land as the culminating sentence of its paragraph, followed by a paragraph break (the silence)."

---

## When to deploy a named attack

ALL of these must be true:
1. **Point of no return.** This blow ends the round, decides the fight, or commits the fighter past safety.
2. **Emotional preparation.** Something happened in the previous beats that earned this moment.
3. **Thematic embodiment.** The attack expresses what the character IS, not just what they DO.
4. **Spatial clarity.** The reader knows where everyone is, what's around them, what's about to break.

Names are NOT spoken during measuring exchanges, light hits, throwaway blows, defensive moves, or the first contact in a fight. Names are reserved for the moment a fighter commits everything.

Maximum **2 named attacks per fight scene.** Usually 1.

---

## Format

Use bold for the name. Foreign-language format (style-name first, translation in parens) when fitting:

```
Merdo's gauntlet caught the rail. The colosseum stone
cracked under his weight. The Marine captain was already
moving — Hudore was always already moving — and the gap
between them was nothing.

Merdo planted his foot.

**"DANZAI: TOMB-MARKER."**

The kanabo came down where Hudore's chest used to be. A
crater opened in the floor. Dust, then silence.
```

OR with a foreign-language style-name layer (for technique-system characters like Ashren or Sumi):

```
Ashren's heel plates went silent. The crowd noticed before
the Marines did.

**"Higure: Yuukoku."** (Higure: Twilight Sorrow.)

The blade did what blades do.
```

## Naming conventions per crew member

Use these as the vocabulary palette. Name patterns must hold over time so they build recognition.

### MERDO
**Pattern:** weapon name + dark adjective/noun, often with execution / judgment vocabulary.
- DANZAI ("judgment" — already established weapon name)
- DANZAI: TOMB-MARKER (heavy overhead)
- DANZAI: GUILTY-FOOT (sweep takedown)
- GNADENKETTE: LAST RITES (flail finishing arc)
- BARE-HANDED: NO PARDON (gauntlet finisher)
- FUTURE — when Conqueror's awakens, names should suddenly become MUCH simpler and grander ("VERDICT" alone)

### ASHREN
**Pattern:** Higure (his nodachi name) + Japanese atmospheric/twilight noun. He doesn't announce his attacks — his opponents announce them by feeling them.
- Higure: Yuukoku (Twilight Sorrow)
- Higure: Hakurai (Pale Lightning)
- Higure: Shoutotsu (Collision)
- For combo with Lunarian flame — single word: "FLARE." Or "ASCEND." (when wings deploy mid-strike)

### MELAINA
**Pattern:** Hot weapon (Shibito-Bashi) gets Oni-rage names; cold weapon (Serpent Bow) gets Medusa-cold names; full Medusa form gets myth names.
- SHIBITO-BASHI: ROOT-BREAKER (mace overhead)
- SHIBITO-BASHI: NIGHT-FEED (bone-spike puncture)
- SERPENT BOW: SLOW LOOK (venom shot, petrify-adjacent)
- SERPENT BOW: SISTER'S ARROW (named after one of her mourning bands)
- MEDUSA HYBRID: FIRST GAZE (hybrid form's eye contact, partial petrification)

### VESPER (Mink Electro)
**Pattern:** Electro words + medical terminology. Comedic delivery often.
- "ELECTRO: DEFIB!" (when she's actually saving someone)
- "ELECTRO: WAKE UP!" (slap-shock combo)
- "ELECTRO: I SAID SIT DOWN!" (full discharge when crewmate ignores medical orders)
- Sulong variant — only when triggered, names go bigger and more reverent: "MOON-VOICE: HEAR ME."

### SABLE (Paku Paku no Mi + Fragment Rokushiki)
**Pattern:** Eating verbs + reflex vocabulary. Most attacks delivered with confused surprise.
- "PAKU: SWALLOW." (eats an incoming projectile)
- "PAKU: RETURN TO SENDER." (spits it back at higher velocity)
- "ROKUSHIKI: SORU." (escape)
- "OH GOD I JUST ATE A — *PAKU*." (improvised, comedic, also lethal)
- Hidden CP-0 reflex names she WON'T say but slip out under stress: "Aigis." (cover protocol)

### VOLO (Fish-Man Karate + Trident)
**Pattern:** Water words + fish vocabulary + theatrical demand. Full body announcement.
- "FISH-MAN KARATE: TIDAL HAMMER!" (concussive water strike, amplified in rain)
- "FISH-MAN KARATE: SPINE-ARROW!" (venom fin-spine attack)
- "TRIDENT: LIONFISH BLOOM!" (fins-out three-way thrust)
- "VOLO PTERO FINNBALOR DOES NOT MISS." (statement, not a technique, but used as one)

### SUMI (Kami Kami no Mi — Paper Paper)
**Pattern:** Origami / scroll / paper craft vocabulary, often shouted with full kid-energy. CAPS frequent.
- "KAMI KAMI: CRANE STRIKE!" (turns to paper, refolds airborne, lands as a thousand cuts)
- "KAMI KAMI: SCROLL DRAG!" (wraps opponent in paper, weaponized origami)
- "KAMI KAMI: BANNER TOSS!" (sends crew above the crowd as a paper flag)
- "PIRATE QUEEN'S MARK!" (her own invention, the move's actual mechanic varies, she's making it up)
- Weakness: rain. Names get sad and quiet in rain. "Kami kami: ...soggy."

### NOX (no Devil Fruit, no Haki — pure gunner + bullshit)
**Pattern:** Made-up wrestling-promo names. Half are fake. He calls every shot.
- "MAMA & PAPA — TWIN THUNDER!" (just two pistols at once)
- "STAR-EATER ROUND!" (regular bullet, named on the spot)
- "NEW MOVE I JUST INVENTED — DON'T BLINK!"
- "EIGHT THOUSAND FOLLOWER FINISHER!" (he doesn't have followers)
- The funny part is that despite the bullshit, the shots LAND. Crew teases him about the names later.
- Real techniques (under the BS): trick-shot ricochet, called-shot precision, double-load gunpowder for one big blow.

### KARA ODINU (Bake Bake no Mi — failed Zoan)
**Pattern:** Kid struggling with names because she's still recovering. Often unnamed. When she does name something, it's quiet and small.
- "...claw." (when scales emerge for a swipe)
- Mostly unnamed because she doesn't have the language yet. Will develop over time.

## Foreign-language format

When using a non-English style-name, format with original first, translation in parens immediately:

> **"Higure: Yuukoku."** (Higure: Twilight Sorrow.)

> **"Kami Kami no… Senbazuru."** (Paper Paper… Thousand Cranes.)

> **"Rokushiki: Soru."** (Six Powers: Shave.)

This is canonical One Piece — Oda does it in the manga via furigana. In prose, parens. Use sparingly — not every name.

---

## What NOT to do

- DON'T name routine exchanges. The fifth punch in a flurry doesn't need a name.
- DON'T let multiple characters use named attacks in the same exchange — staggered timing is the rule.
- DON'T announce attacks before they land. The name lands AT impact or one beat before, not in the setup.
- DON'T use generic anime move-sludge names ("DRAGON FIST", "SHADOW STRIKE", "THUNDER BLADE"). Per the naming feedback memory: real-world salt + character-specific vocabulary.
- DON'T let the player narrate Merdo's named attacks. Merdo might commit to a strike — but the player decides whether to spend the named-attack moment or not.

## STRATEGIC REVELATION — the fight as analysis

**Adapted from Nemo's Shonen Core Pack.** In One Piece fights, combatants are not just hitting each other — they're THINKING, reading, testing, adapting. Named attacks are the FINISH. The work before them is strategic revelation: showing patterns, testing defenses, revealing hidden information.

### What to surface in prose

Combatants analyze opponents mid-fight. Use:

- **Pattern recognition** — "Hudore always resets his weight between his second and third exchange" — read the pattern, then exploit or break it in the next beat
- **Defense testing** — a fighter throws a feint to see if Observation-Haki is active; the opponent's response tells the fighter what they're dealing with
- **Power reveal by LAYERS** — a fighter does NOT show their best technique first. Saving something is a tactical decision. The reveal becomes a character beat ("he had this the whole time")
- **Reading the wound** — a fighter sees a cracked rib or a favored side and builds the next attack around it
- **Hidden trump cards** — a fighter who's been losing may pivot to a technique that's been SET UP off-screen (trained in secret, taught by a flashback mentor, inherited from a dying rival)

### How this operates in prose

Do NOT write out the strategic analysis as internal monologue ("He thought, 'If I feint here, I can see if…'"). That's therapy-speak in fighter form. Instead:

- **Show the test beat + the opponent's response + the fighter's next move** — the reader infers the strategy from the sequence
- **Show a character NOTICE something** — "Ashren's eyes moved once. The Marine hadn't noticed he'd stopped breathing during the exchange."
- **Delay the reveal** — the fighter's strategic insight isn't stated until the named-attack moment lands; the reader's retrospective reading of the prior beats IS the strategic reveal

### Per-crew strategic profiles

- **Merdo** — reads with his body. His "strategic revelations" are FELT, not thought. He figures out an opponent by taking hits and adjusting. His reads land as HALF-BEATS of instinct, not sentences of analysis.
- **Ashren** — calculated, silent. Sees patterns in breath and heel-plate cadence (his own included). His reads are the most precise and the least verbal.
- **Rook (departed)** — was the ANALYST reader. His reads appeared as flat quiet statements mid-fight. Without him, the crew has lost this register — let the absence FEEL felt.
- **Sable** — reads through the Paku Paku on reflex (she swallows evidence, tastes what she's eating, reports back later). Her strategic register is accidental.
- **Cress (newly promoted)** — from the navigation table she now works the STRATEGIC READING for the crew during fights she can observe (from the ship, from a rooftop). Her reads land in post-fight debrief, not mid-fight.
- **Hudore** — the template for STRATEGIC opponents. He reads Merdo constantly. His techniques always have a read BEHIND them. The player should feel that every Hudore move was SELECTED for this specific moment in the fight.

## TECHNIQUE ACTIVATION HIERARCHY

**Adapted from Nemo's Shonen Core Pack.** Not every named technique is activated the same way. The ACTIVATION is its own character beat.

### The 5 activation registers

**1. SHOUTED (CAPS, exclaim)** — Per `emotion-will-heart.md` SCREAM PRINCIPLE. The default for shonen peak moments. Merdo's brawler register, Sumi's PIRATE QUEEN register, Volo's theatrical register, Nox's wrestling-promo register.

> **"DANZAI!! TOMB-MARKER!!"**

**2. DECLARED (normal voice, spoken aloud, firm)** — calm but committed. The fighter speaks the name as if reading a verdict. Katakuri-tier register.

> "Ryu Ryu no Mi, Model: Pteranodon." Volo rose off the deck. "You cannot outrun what flies above you."

**3. WHISPERED / QUIET (barely audible, sometimes only to themselves)** — martial-epic register per `martial-epic-beats.md`. Ashren lives here. Mihawk-tier.

> Ashren's heel plates were silent. "Higure: Yuukoku."

**4. SILENT (not spoken at all — the technique lands with the name in the narration only)** — the highest tier of control. The technique's NAME is known to the reader via prose attribution but is NOT spoken by the fighter. Zoro's silence during his Asura, Shanks's haki, Mihawk's single-cut moments.

> The blade left its sheath and returned to it. There was nothing between. Yuukoku, the crew would later say — though Ashren had said nothing.

**5. SUNG / CHANTED (ritualized, often in a fictional or foreign language)** — rare, for specific character types. Ideology-fighters, religious zealots, ceremonial magic-adjacent techniques.

> Parseval's voice was not raised. "Tetsu Tetsu no Mi — *gratia plena* — Iron Iron — *ora pro nobis*." The body below the mask became the mask.

### The rule for deployment

- **The activation register TELLS you who the fighter is.** A fighter who normally SHOUTS their attacks, but WHISPERS one in a key moment, is going through something — the shift is a character beat. (Merdo quieting a named attack for the first time is a saga-weight moment.)
- **Opponents get activation registers too.** When you design a villain, assign them a default register. Hudore DECLARES. Parseval CHANTS. A pit-fighter brawler SHOUTS. A hidden swordmaster WHISPERS.
- **Mixed registers in the same fight are powerful.** Sumi SHOUTS her kid-energy names and Ashren WHISPERS his nodachi names in the same parallel fight — the contrast IS the ensemble character study.

### Foreign-language format (unchanged from above)

Still use foreign-language original + parens translation when fitting. Works with any activation register:

- SHOUTED foreign: **"BAJRANG... GUN!!!"** (no translation needed — the scream carries)
- DECLARED foreign: "Rokushiki: Soru." (Six Powers: Shave.)
- WHISPERED foreign: "Higure: Yuukoku." (Higure: Twilight Sorrow.)
- SILENT foreign: narration only — "The Yuukoku strike left no echo."
- CHANTED foreign: ritual-wrapped — see Parseval example above.

# Cover Page Story Series — Side Character Mini-Arcs

**Canon Oda device.** Each weekly chapter begins with a cover-page illustration showing a SIDE CHARACTER from a previous arc going about their life — Buggy reuniting with his crew, Captain Kuro hiding as a butler, Vivi addressing parliament, Pell recovering, the Davy Back fighters in unrelated trouble. These run as MULTI-CHAPTER STRIP SERIES — the side character has their own complete mini-arc unfolding one panel at a time across many cover pages.

This module gives the DM a structural device for the same: between sessions / at session opens / as session-end omake, drop a cover-page-style cut showing what an off-screen side character is doing today.

---

## What a cover-page story IS (in canon)

Each cover panel:
- Shows ONE moment from a side character's continuing life
- Connects to past arcs (the character we already met)
- Builds across multiple weeks / chapters into a complete mini-arc
- Has NO dialogue (or minimal — captions only)
- Functions as PURE TEXTURE in the world

**Canon examples:**
- Buggy's crew reunion (chapters 96-118): each cover advances a separate plotline of Buggy escaping Loguetown and reuniting with his crew across many ports
- "Diary of Koby and Helmeppo" (chapters 119-138): their rise through Marine ranks, training under Garp
- Wapol after Drum (chapters 304-358): his rise from exile to candy-king of a new country
- Foxy Pirates after Davy Back (chapters 396-407): their misadventures
- Boss Luffy theater (the gag arc)
- "From the Decks of the World" (chapters 491-510): showing how the world reacted to the Whitebeard War broadcast

The canon RULE: cover-page series typically run 10-30 chapters. They're tiny windows into characters who left the main plot.

---

## Why cover-page stories MATTER

1. **They make the world feel BIG.** The Merdo Pirates' world has Korvaal STILL doing his thing on Ashenmaw, Adaam Goroa STILL ruling, Mira Odinu STILL at Ridas village, the Hoyt Boys' incarcerated leader STILL in Marine custody. The reader gets glimpses of these continuing lives.

2. **They reward the player's investment.** When Korvaal got a card last arc, seeing Korvaal's life continuing in a cover page tells the player: that arc MATTERED. The world remembers. That character isn't dead-and-gone, they're a person still living.

3. **They plant Chekhov-rack payoffs.** A cover page showing Mira Odinu receiving Kara's first letter is BOTH texture AND a setup for a later return-to-Goroa arc.

4. **They give the DM an outlet for ambient worldbuilding** without dumping it into prose narration.

## Format

A cover-page story drops as a brief, captioned vignette. 3-6 lines. Anime-style title on top.

```
─────────────── COVER PAGE 1 ───────────────
   "MIRA ODINU OF RIDAS — A LETTER FROM HER DAUGHTER"
─────────────────────────────────────────────

   Morning at Ridas village. Mira Odinu hangs the wash.
   The fishmonger's son hands her a folded paper.
   She reads it in the doorway.
   She sits down on the step before the third paragraph.
   The wash dries unattended. The wind catches a sleeve.

─────────────────────────────────────────────
```

```
─────────────── COVER PAGE 1 ───────────────
   "KORVAAL'S PEOPLE — THE BUILDERS"
─────────────────────────────────────────────

   The Ashenmaw village has tripled in size.
   The chant cathedral is being converted to a school.
   A child sits cross-legged on the resonance device.
   Korvaal kneels beside the child and shows her how to read.
   The pendant Merdo wears glints in his pocket — a copy.

─────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## When to deploy

### Between arcs
The strongest position. After a major arc closes, drop ONE cover-page from a side character of that arc going about their life now.

### At session open (rare alternative to cold open)
Sometimes the cold-open IS a cover-page story. Use when a side character's update is more interesting than meanwhile-cuts to villains.

### At session end (after NEXT TIME ON, before END CARD)
A small bonus moment. Pure texture. No plot weight needed.

### Within News Coo
A cover-page story can be embedded in the News Coo's "civilian interest" section — showing the broader world's life.

---

## SERIES vs ONE-OFF

A cover page can be:

### A ONE-OFF
A single moment with that character. They're fine, they're moving on, they exist. Done.

### Part of a CONTINUING SERIES
Multiple cover pages across many sessions, advancing the side character's own arc. This is the canon pattern.

### Suggested Merdo-saga continuing series:

#### "MIRA ODINU OF RIDAS"
- Cover 1: Mira receives Kara's first letter, sits down on the step
- Cover 2: Mira teaches a new village girl Kara's old chores; the chore feels lighter
- Cover 3: Marines arrive at Goroa for routine port-check; Mira hides Kara's letter under a floorboard
- Cover 4: The crew returns to Goroa someday — Mira opens the door, sees Kara grown
- (4-5 cover pages over the saga, paying off when crew revisits)

#### "KORVAAL'S PEOPLE — THE BUILDERS"
- Cover 1: Village rebuilds, school proposed
- Cover 2: A passing trader brings news of a Lunarian rumored alive
- Cover 3: A WG dispatch arrives asking about the device
- Cover 4: Korvaal carves a new spiral pendant (the third — for whom?)
- (4-5 cover pages over the saga)

#### "ADAAM GOROA — THE INDEPENDENT KING"
- Cover 1: Goroa expands the harbor; trade booming
- Cover 2: A Doflamingo agent visits with an "alliance offer"; Adaam declines
- Cover 3: Marine warship visits; Adaam politely refuses inspection
- Cover 4: The crew's bounty arrives in Adaam's office; he laughs
- (3-4 cover pages building to a major Goroa-political arc later)

#### "THE HOYT BOYS' BROTHER"
- Cover 1: A Marine prison cell — Nox's brother reads news of the Merdo Pirates
- Cover 2: He smiles for the first time in two years
- Cover 3: He requests a transfer to a less-secure facility
- Cover 4: He escapes
- (Slow-burn payoff for Nox arc, possibly 8-10 sessions out)

#### "VIVI OF ALABASTA — THE PRINCESS WHO CHOSE"
- (canon character — use sparingly, maintain canon voice)
- Cover 1: Vivi addresses parliament about post-Crocodile reconstruction
- Cover 2: Vivi reads about a "horned pirate captain" in the news, smiles
- (1-2 covers max — this is canon-character cameo)

## Format conventions

- HEADER includes a brief evocative title in quotes (anime-cover style)
- 3-6 short sentences (it's a cover ILLUSTRATION + caption — sparse)
- NO dialogue except minimal captions
- NO meta-narration ("meanwhile in Ridas...")
- Just a SCENE, captioned, drawn

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't reveal saga-spoiler info via cover pages (Sumi's Joy Boy, Melaina's Shanks)
- Don't overuse — max 1 cover page per session
- Don't make every cover page plot-relevant (some are pure life-going-on)
- Don't break canon — Vivi in a cover page must speak/act like Vivi
- Don't introduce NEW unnamed characters in cover pages — keep them tied to characters the crew knows

---

## Suggested rhythm

- 1 cover page per session, on average
- Rotate between continuing series and one-offs
- Once a series advances, NOTE its position so future covers continue (use `living-world.md` [PARALLEL] hidden-block to track)
- Some sessions skip cover pages entirely — that's fine; they're a flourish, not a requirement

## Haki Training Arc — Realistic Power-Up Pacing

**Per canon research + `one_piece_power_law.md` memory:** Haki progresses in STAGES. Observation: sense → emotion-read → predict → selective. Armament: coat → penetrate → selective → emit. Conqueror's: unconscious → deliberate → coating. The two-year timeskip is the canonical TRAINING ARC template.

This module governs how training arcs play out in the campaign — whether compressed into a session, unfolded over a mini-arc, or stretched across a full saga (timeskip-equivalent).

---

## The Haki progression ladder (4 stages each)

### Observation Haki
| Stage | Ability | Merdo current |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sensing nearby presences (enemy in the room, ally approaching) | NOT YET |
| 2 | Reading emotions and intent (lies, killing intent) | NOT YET |
| 3 | Predictive sensing (a step-ahead, limited future-sight) | NOT YET (Ashren has this) |
| 4 | Selective sensing (hearing hearts, specific targets across distance) | Canon: Katakuri-tier |

**Canon training pattern:** Rayleigh taught Luffy by making him DODGE BLIND. The training is FAITH in sensing over sight. Katakuri developed his through PRECISION and CALMNESS — his emotional state controls his range.

### Armament Haki
| Stage | Ability | Merdo current |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Basic coating (hardness, blocking cuts, damage resistance) | BUDDING — first activation at Goroa, protective instinct |
| 2 | Penetration (striking through objects, internal-damage strikes) | NOT YET |
| 3 | Selective coating (armoring only parts, conserving energy) | NOT YET |
| 4 | Weapon / projectile emission (Haki without contact) | Canon: Shanks-tier |

**Canon training pattern:** Zoro learned from Mihawk by CUTTING STEEL. The training is technique married to will — Armament doesn't change the sword's nature, it changes how the sword is USED.

### Conqueror's Haki
| Stage | Ability | Merdo current |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Unconscious manifestation (knocking out weaker people, no control) | DORMANT (UNKNOWN to Merdo) |
| 2 | Deliberate use (choosing targets, pressure zones) | Saga 5-7 horizon |
| 3 | Coating (applying to weapons, non-contact strikes) | Saga 7-9 horizon |

**Canon training pattern:** Luffy's Conqueror's AWAKENS unconsciously during Marineford. By Wano, he COATS it on attacks. The progression is CONTROL, not power.

## Training arc formats (3 scales)

### Format 1 — Mid-session training beat (compact, 200-400 words)
A SINGLE SCENE of training between arcs. Not a whole session. Shows the crew practicing during transit between islands.

Format:
- Someone practices a specific technique (named)
- Someone else observes / corrects
- ONE concrete improvement happens OR one concrete failure happens
- End with a line on what's still not working

Example:
```
On the fifth day out from Tessara, Merdo tried the
Armament again.

The Golgatha's mast held. That was the whole lesson.

He had been trying to coat his gauntlet deliberately —
not in the pit-fight reflex, not in the protective
moment — just on command. For twenty minutes. The
gauntlet had stayed dull.

Ashren was above him at the crossbeam, cleaning Higure.
He didn't look down.

"Merdo."

"Mm."

"You're holding your breath."

Merdo exhaled. Looked at his hand. Tried again. The
gauntlet's knuckles darkened — not much, not long — and
cracked the mast's RIM where he touched it.

He put his hand in his pocket.

"Not yet," Ashren said, still cleaning.

"Not yet," Merdo agreed.
```

Notes: 140 words, one specific technique tested (deliberate Armament coating), failure with a specific detail (breath), micro-improvement (brief darkening), closing line locks the stage.

### Format 2 — Training mini-arc (1-3 sessions)
The crew arrives at a special location / meets a specific mentor for a brief training period.

Canon parallels: Dadan's training for Luffy pre-Arlong, Mihawk's Baratie fight which TAUGHT Zoro what mastery looks like, the first Rayleigh scene at Sabaody which TAUGHT Luffy that being strong isn't enough.

Format:
- A mentor / training environment is introduced
- ONE specific technique or stage-advancement is targeted
- A FAILURE beat mid-training
- A BREAKTHROUGH beat at the end
- Cost implied (fatigue, a relationship bruise, time)

Deploy: once per saga for ONE character. Not every saga gets a mini-training-arc — only when the saga's arc structure has room.

### Format 3 — Full saga-level training arc (timeskip-equivalent)
Canon parallel: the 2-year timeskip. Crew separated, each trains with a specific mentor, each returns with ONE concrete improvement.

For Merdo: this might happen at SAGA 3 end (Sabaody-equivalent, possibly a Kuma-scatter event — the Supernovas were scattered). If the crew scatters, each member has a micro-training-arc across Saga 4 opening, then they reconverge.

If the crew does NOT scatter, the saga-level training can still happen: a specific island the Golgatha stays at for weeks, each crew member training with a different local master.

## Training arc triggers

When to deploy a training-arc beat:

| Trigger | Format |
|---|---|
| Merdo post-Tessara, deliberate Armament still unreliable | Format 1 (mid-session beat) recurring |
| A character's focus arc surfaces their technical wall | Format 2 (mini-arc) within the focus-arc |
| Saga 3 Kuma-scatter equivalent (if fired) | Format 3 (full training) across Saga 3 close / Saga 4 open |
| A specific Haki stage-advancement is narratively earned | Format 2 (mini-arc) once per saga max |

---

## The training arc's 4 canonical structure beats

Regardless of format:

1. **The technique-wall** — the character hits something they CANNOT DO YET. Made visible by a specific failure.
2. **The mentor frame** — someone (or something) shows them what's possible.
3. **The training itself** — repeated attempts, progressive failure with smaller-margin of failure each time.
4. **The breakthrough** — usually EMOTIONAL first, then technical. They UNDERSTAND something about themselves, then the technique lands.

Canon: Zoro doesn't hear the breath of all things through mechanical practice. He hears it when he SURRENDERS to the moment. Luffy doesn't get Gear 4 through reps. He gets it by GRIEVING and pivoting his grief into transformation.

---

## What NOT to do

- DON'T let Merdo skip Haki stages. Armament Stage 1→4 is YEARS of canon. Don't accelerate for drama.
- DON'T reveal Merdo's Conqueror's before the saga earns it. Saga 5-7 is the horizon.
- DON'T make training arcs EXCLUSIVELY about power. The emotional breakthrough IS the technical breakthrough.
- DON'T deploy a full saga-level training arc before Saga 3. Nothing has earned the pause yet.
- DON'T have every crew member advance in the same arc. Rotate. Spotlight.
- DON'T let training arcs drift into video-game leveling. Canon trains through PROBLEMS, not through grinding.

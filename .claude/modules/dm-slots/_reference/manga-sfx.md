# Manga Sound Effects (SFX) Catalog

**Manga renders sound through stylized text drawn in distinctive lettering.** A blade hitting iron isn't "clang" — it's "**KIIIN**" filling half the panel. A footstep isn't a verb — it's "**TON**." A monstrous footfall is "**ZUN**." A flame catching is "**GOOOO**."

When prose imports these manga-SFX, the reader's mind hears them in the manga voice. This module catalogs the canonical sound-words and rules for use.

---

## Format conventions

- **ALL CAPS** for the SFX
- **Bold** for emphasis
- **Stretching vowels** for duration: "GOOOOOO" / "KIIIIIN"
- **Inline within prose**, integrated into the action
- **Or on its own line** for splash-impact

Example inline:
```
The iron leash whipped around. **KAPP** — the gauntlet caught it.
```

Example on its own line (splash):
```
**DON.**
```

## DEPLOY RULES

### Rule 1: One per beat
Don't stack five SFX in one paragraph. Pick the ONE that sells the moment.

### Rule 2: Match SFX to physics
Don't use **DON** for a footstep. Don't use **TON** for a building collapse. Match scale.

### Rule 3: Alternate inline vs splash
Inline: integrated into the prose, stretches the action's beat.
Splash: own line, used for impact reveals.

```
Inline: "Iron met iron — **KIIIN** — and Hudore's leash bent."
Splash: "**DON.**\n\nThe pit cracked from one wall to the other."
```

### Rule 4: Use SHIIIN for silence
This is the SINGLE most-iconic manga SFX. Use when:
- A reveal lands and the world stops
- A finisher connects and there's a beat before the next thing
- An unexpected reveal happens and nobody knows what to say

```
Hudore opened his coat. The Commodore insignia caught the light.

**SHIIIN.**
```

### Rule 5: Don't over-translate
Don't write "(meaning: silence)" after **SHIIIN**. The reader will get it. Or won't. Either way, don't translate.

### Rule 6: Cap usage
- Combat scene: 2-4 SFX max
- Quiet scene: 1 SHIIIN max
- Full session: ~5-8 SFX total

Saturation kills the device. The manga uses SFX heavily because it can DRAW them stylized. Prose is more sparing — but every one that lands hits.

---

## What this buys the campaign

The reader's mind hears the manga. **DON** doesn't land just as text — it lands as a remembered Oda panel. **SHIIIN** isn't a translation of "silence" — it's the actual moment in Wano where Kaido falls and the world stops.

Use these and the prose stops being prose and starts being a manga-page-being-read.

---

## Pairing with named-attacks

Named attacks + SFX stack at the moment of commitment:

```
Merdo planted his foot.

**"CONQUEROR'S WILL — TOMB-MARKER!!"**

**GOSHA — DON.**

The pit floor cracked open from one wall to the other.

**SHIIIN.**
```

Three SFX in three lines (named attack + double-impact SFX + silence) is the canonical Oda finisher pattern. Save it for finishers.

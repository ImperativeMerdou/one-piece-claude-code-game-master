## Meanwhile Cuts (the world breathes around the player)

Per user request: "Also meanwhile segments." Periodic offscreen pivots to other locations / factions / crews. The world keeps moving when Merdo isn't looking. This is canonical One Piece anime structure — every arc has Marine HQ cuts, Doflamingo office cuts, Straw Hat parallel reactions.

---

## When to fire

- **Every 2-3 in-game days** in normal play (default cadence)
- **On any consequence trigger** (when an active consequence escalates: Marine report files, Doflamingo agent reports back, etc.)
- **At chapter / scene transitions** (between major beats)
- **After Merdo makes a big public move** (let the world receive it)
- **NEVER mid-action.** Cut between scenes, not during them.

Limit: **1-2 meanwhile cuts per session.** More than that and the player loses the captain's POV.

---

## Where to cut to

Pick from this rotating roster. Vary so the same place doesn't show up two sessions in a row.

### A. MARINE HQ — Marineford
- Sengoku reading reports
- Garp eating crackers in the corner of a strategy meeting and saying something blunt
- A Den Den Mushi ringing at 3 AM with bad news
- A Vice Admiral filing the Tessara report

### B. The Three Admirals' offices
- Aokiji asleep on a couch, file open on his chest with Merdo's name in it
- Kizaru moving slow but reading fast
- Akainu silent over a bounty board, marking three names red

### C. CP-0 / Cipher Pol
- A clean office, a quiet conversation, a file labeled MERDO. P. assembled
- Spandam being annoying, someone senior ignoring him
- A Lunarian-tagged dossier being pulled for the first time in 50 years (Ashren's reveal cascading)

### D. Donquixote operations
- Doflamingo's office in Dressrosa: feathers, big window, "FUFUFUFU... interesting." pose
- Trebol making a mess and reading a dispatch
- The Tessara buyer reporting back via Den Den Mushi to a higher-up
- A subordinate flinching at the wrong word

### E. Other Worst Generation crews (parallel rookies)
- **Straw Hats** — currently in Drum Island / about to go to Alabasta. Luffy demanding meat. Nami yelling at him.
- **Kid Pirates** — Kid reading the paper, putting down a hammer, saying "Merdo."
- **Heart Pirates** — Law in his sub, Bepo somewhere in frame, Law saying nothing for a long time.
- **Hawkins** — drawing a card, looking at it, putting it back without a word.
- **Bonney** — eating, reading the paper, eating, reading.

### F. Yonko / world powers (rare, only on saga moments)

- **Kaido** drunk, hearing about a Elbaf-descended brawler with no Devil Fruit, intrigued

### G. Merdo-history / origin connections (rare)
- Mesut (the village that sold him) — a quiet shot of someone reading a bounty poster and going pale
- The pit owners from his slavery years — finding out he's still alive and free
- Kara Odinu's mother in Ridas village, hearing the Tessara news
- Korvaal's village receiving word that the Danzai Pirates are still alive

### H. Twenty-perspective (back on the Golgatha or in port)
- Mako fitting Kara with a knot kit
- Skel watching a single point on the horizon
- Cress arguing 31% with herself at the nav table
- Oleg looking at the weather with an expression nobody can read

## Voice rules per faction

- **Marines** — clipped, professional, occasionally human. Garp is the comedy valve.
- **CP-0** — quiet, precise, never raises voice. Bureaucratic horror.
- **Donquixote family** — theatrical, unsettling, big personality energy. FUFUFUFU laughs.
- **Straw Hats** — pure comedy + sincerity. Luffy is loud, Nami is exasperated, Zoro is stoic.
- **Kid Pirates** — short, hard, mean.
- **Heart Pirates** — long silences. Law speaks once per cut.
- **Yonko** — very few lines. Their stillness IS the threat. One sentence carries the cut.

---

## What NOT to do

- DON'T cut to Merdo's location ("meanwhile back at the Golgatha") — that's not meanwhile, that's the same scene.
- DON'T explain why we cut to this place — just cut.
- DON'T make every meanwhile cut PLOT-RELEVANT. Sometimes Garp just eats crackers. The cadence matters.
- DON'T overstay. 8 lines max. Cut back.
- DON'T speak for Merdo even in cuts where someone's reading about him. The dialogue can describe him from outside, but never reveal his thoughts.
- DON'T make the cuts contradict the player's POV. The world reacts to Merdo, but doesn't know what he secretly intends.

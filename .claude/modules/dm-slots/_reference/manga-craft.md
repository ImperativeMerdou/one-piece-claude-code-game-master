## Manga Craft — The Oda Layer

**User framing: "Oda is writing the manga."** This module encodes manga-specific craft that makes the prose feel like Weekly Shonen Jump — chapter endings that hook, SBS-style trivia, cover-page side stories, panel grammar, callback density, and volume-break beats.

Pair with `anime-craft.md` (the Toei layer) — manga is WRITTEN, anime is ANIMATED. Both run.

---

## Chapter-end hooks (fire 1× per session, always)

A One Piece chapter ends on ONE of five shapes. End every session and most major scenes on one of these:

### 1. The arrival
A silhouette in the doorway. A name spoken. Someone walks into frame at the last beat.

> The Marine ship flag came down the harbor. Not the regular corvettes. A black flag.
> Someone was on the prow.
> Merdo recognized the posture before the face.
>
> *TO BE CONTINUED.*

### 2. The declaration
Someone says a line that reframes everything. ONE line, on its own paragraph.

> "You're not taking her."
>
> *TO BE CONTINUED.*

### 3. The impact-pending
Action committed, outcome not yet landed.

> The gauntlet came up.
> Hudore's leash uncoiled one slow inch.
> The bell rang.
>
> *TO BE CONTINUED.*

### 4. The question
An unanswered thing. A beat of silence with a question in it.

> Sumi looked at him. Mouth open. For the first time, no sound.
>
> *TO BE CONTINUED.*

### 5. The silhouette
A shape the reader doesn't fully see. A hint. A name partial. A shadow.

> The figure on the black-hulled ship lifted one hand off the rail. The gold signet caught the light.
> Sable's voice, very quiet, at Merdo's elbow:
> "...Captain. We need to talk."
>
> *TO BE CONTINUED.*

Use one of these at session end ALWAYS. Can also use at mid-session chapter breaks when a scene feels complete.

## Callback density (every 2-3 sessions, fire a callback)

Oda's craft: plant a thing CHAPTERS ago, fire it now. The chekhov-rack handles major plants. This is about MICRO callbacks.

Examples:
- Mako brought Sumi bread Day 11 morning. Three sessions later, when Sumi's down in a fight, Mako appears with bread. No explanation. The readers who remember ACHE.
- Volo has been pocketing a specific seashell at every island for three arcs. In the fourth arc's quiet moment, we see the line of them on a shelf in his cabin — one for every island. He never mentions it. The reader does the work.
- The fruit basket Yann sent: one of the pears is rotting three sessions later. Sable eats it anyway. No one comments. Two arcs later: that pear's pit is still in Sable's stomach, and she spits it back as a projectile at a critical moment.

Callbacks should reward memory without being homework. Never over-explain. If the reader catches it, they catch it.

## Cover-Page Side Stories (optional, fire rarely)

Oda writes one-page cover stories across chapters showing minor characters' ongoing lives. In prose, do this as a 1-2 sentence italic interlude BETWEEN major scenes, not inside them.

Format:

```
*(Cover page: Korvaal standing knee-deep in the new village well, bucket in hand, teaching a young chanter how to draw water without spilling. The spiral pendant at his throat catches the light. 19 days sober of the old duty.)*
```

Use for:
- Korvaal's people rebuilding (multi-arc arc)
- Adaam Goroa hosting a feast (comedic recurring)
- Mira Odinu at Ridas village (Kara's mother — waiting for letters)
- Laboon at Reverse Mountain (long-saga arc)
- Specific civilians the crew helped (they're living their lives)

Fire one cover-page cut per 2-3 sessions. Each one advances a background life.

## SBS-Style Fan Q&A (RARE, user-facing, big-moment only)

Oda's SBS column answers fan questions in the back of Jump volumes. We can fire this as a comedy cut inside `anime-forum.md` — but it's distinct enough to mention here.

Format: a single fan letter answered in the DM's "Oda voice" — silly, self-aware, character-deepening.

```
━━━ SBS (Danzai Pirates Vol. 3, Chapter 47 back matter) ━━━

READER (Henryk from West Blue): "Oda-sensei! Why does Sable always
swallow things?? Is it a hunger thing? Is it a trauma thing??"

ODA: "It's a CP-0 thing, actually. When she was 11, she accidentally
swallowed a live mission-brief Den Den Mushi and Lucci laughed. Still
ringing. That's the actual answer. See you next chapter!"

— drawing of Sable winking with a lump in her cheek —

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

Use ~1× per 3-5 sessions. Pair with an anime-forum cut occasionally. Makes the reader feel like they're reading Jump.

## Volume-break beats

A "volume" in One Piece is roughly 10 chapters. Every 10 sessions, orient a volume-break beat:

- A News Coo with world-state update
- A bounty update (if earned)
- A meanwhile cut of multiple factions reacting
- A short cover-page set showing 3-4 off-screen lives progressing

Session ~20 would be "end of Volume 2." Structure the session's final response to FEEL like the last chapter before a break.

## What this layer does for the user

They read their session and feel like they just read a CHAPTER. Not a DM log, not a novel, not a movie script — a Jump chapter. With chapter hooks that bleed into next week. With callbacks they half-remember. With cover-page sides that make the world bigger. With SBS that's pure fun.

The Oda layer is the skeleton. The Toei layer (anime-craft.md) drapes the flesh.

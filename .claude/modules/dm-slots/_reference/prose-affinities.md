## Prose Affinities — Stackable Style Layers

**Adapted from Paramnesia's Affinities.** These are stackable prose-craft micro-techniques. Mix 2-3 per scene to get the right texture. Each is one specific MOVE the prose makes.

---

## Affinity 1: TELESCOPING (focal-distance shift per sentence)

Sentences zoom in and out. Wide shot → tight reaction → wide aftermath. Manga panel grammar.

```
The colosseum was eight thousand voices climbing one wall of sound.
Mako stopped chewing.
The bell rang.
```

**Use for:** combat scenes, crowd scenes, finishers, big-reveal moments. Anything visual that wants the camera moving.

---

## Affinity 2: CRESCENDO (accelerating prose with no brake)

Sentences get shorter. Beats faster. Less air. More verbs. Until impact. Then silence.

```
Merdo planted his foot. The gauntlet came up. Hudore stepped wide. Merdo closed it. The crowd inhaled. Iron met iron. The pit cracked.

Silence.
```

**Use for:** finishers, named-attack moments, the last seconds of a fight. NEVER for setup — it loses force if everything is CRESCENDO.

---

## Affinity 3: SOMATIC LEDGER (no emotion labels — body only)

Never say "he was angry" or "she felt scared." Only show what the body does. The reader infers the emotion from the physical evidence.

```
Ashren's heel plates stopped clicking. He looked at the door. He did not move toward the door. After a beat, he looked at the wall again.
```

**Use for:** Ashren scenes (his entire affect IS body). Stoic characters. Trauma beats. Anywhere "telling" the emotion would cheapen it.

---

## Affinity 4: REFRAIN (same word, three different meanings)

Repeat a word or phrase across the scene with the meaning shifting each time. Builds rhythm + emotional weight.

```
"Captain," Sumi said the first time, like a child trying out a magic word.
Mako shouted "Captain!" across the deck when the storm hit, and the word meant please.
Hours later, Vesper murmured "Captain" once, soft, and it meant survive.
```

**Use for:** emotional arcs, character bonding, late-arc beats where one word now carries weight.

---

## Affinity 5: BARE STAGE (dialogue-only — blocking IS the narration)

Strip all interior. Just dialogue and physical action. The blocking does the storytelling.

```
"Sit down."
"I'm fine."
"Sit. DOWN."
He sat down.
She unstrapped the medical satchel.
"Bite this."
He bit it.
```

**Use for:** quick exchanges, comedy beats, command moments, scenes where interiority would slow the action.

---

## Affinity 6: BEAUTIFUL RUIN (polysyndeton — sentence on fire)

Long compound sentences strung with "and" / "and" / "and." Builds momentum to a single hard stop.

```
The bell rang and the steward cried the bracket and Hudore stepped into the pit and the crowd surged and Sumi screamed and Volo demanded witness and Merdo unhooked Judgment from his back and the eight-thousand-voice wall went silent and only the iron rasped.
```

**Use for:** climactic moments, peak scenes, moments where the world is BIG and you want to feel the weight. Use SPARINGLY — once or twice per arc.

---

## Affinity 7: WEBNOVEL (fast paragraphs, hook endings)

Short paragraphs. White space between. Each chapter ends on a question, an arrival, or a name dropped.

```
Merdo stepped into the colosseum gate.

Partho the bookmaker spotted him from across the floor and raised his chalk hand.

A figure in the north VIP box stood up.

It was not Yann Merlis.

——

It was someone Merdo had not seen since he was fifteen.
```

**Use for:** session endings, scene cuts, big arrivals, cliffhangers.

---

## Affinity 8: COLD OPEN (start mid-action, no setup)

Begin a scene mid-action. The reader catches up. No "they walked into the colosseum and" preamble.

```
The cleaver caught the rail an inch from Merdo's hip.

"Hah-hah-HAH," Ezrad said. "I missed."
```

**Use for:** scene starts, especially Round 1 of a tournament, ambush moments, urgent arrivals.

---

## Affinity 9: UNDERTOW (something quiet underneath the loud)

Surface action is loud. Beneath it, a quiet detail the reader catches. The detail is the real scene.

```
The Hollowback sisters bowed in unison. Anne's left hand brushed Mary's right wrist for half a second — too small to see from the crowd, too unprofessional to be accident — and then they straightened, and the bell rang, and the lightning began.
```

**Use for:** twin/duo scenes, moments where the surface scene is the misdirect, beats where the reader needs to lean in.

---

## How to STACK

Pick 2-3 per scene. Don't pick all 9. Some natural pairings:

- **Combat finisher:** TELESCOPING + CRESCENDO + SOMATIC LEDGER
- **Quiet bond moment:** SOMATIC LEDGER + REFRAIN
- **Tournament round opening:** COLD OPEN + BARE STAGE
- **Saga-defining beat:** BEAUTIFUL RUIN + REFRAIN
- **Cliffhanger session ending:** WEBNOVEL + UNDERTOW
- **Twin/duo scene:** UNDERTOW + BARE STAGE

The DM picks based on the scene's need. The user can request specific affinities ("write this fight in TELESCOPING + CRESCENDO").

---

## When NOT to use

- Routine micro-turns where the player is just walking somewhere — plain prose is fine.
- When stacking would obscure the spatial logic of action — readability first.
- When affinities would conflict (BEAUTIFUL RUIN's long sentences ≠ CRESCENDO's short sentences — pick one).

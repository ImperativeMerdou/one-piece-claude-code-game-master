## Genre Dials — Scene-Level Tonal Flavors

**Adapted from Paramnesia's 20 Director genre roulettes.** The campaign's default register is shonen-one-piece (action, comedy, heart). But scenes sometimes want a different flavor — a romance beat wants HEARTTHROB gravity, a horror island wants LINGER texture, a politics scene wants VICE decadence. This module lets the DM dial a secondary genre into a scene WITHOUT overriding the base narrator style.

**Rule in one line:** Max ONE genre dial active at a time. Dial in for scene, dial out at scene end. Base register (shonen-one-piece) is always the foundation.

---

## THE DIALS

Each dial is a mood + prose-texture + pacing flavor. Pick one per scene when it fits; otherwise leave dials off and run default shonen register.

### 💖 HEARTTHROB — Romance lean
- **Texture:** proximity attention, micro-gestures, held looks, the distance between two people shrinking or growing as the scene's primary action
- **Pacing:** slower. Moments stretch. A hand on a railing gets three sentences it wouldn't get in a default scene.
- **Vocabulary favored:** specifics of the other person (the exact color of a scar, the exact word they used when they were nervous)
- **Vocabulary banned:** the anti-slop "body parts with agency" rule intensifies here — DON'T say "her eyes searched his face." She searches his face.
- **Deploy for:** Tier 2-3 romance beats, the quiet moment after a fight when someone reaches for someone, first-night conversations, the exchange before the kiss
- **Pairs with:** nsfw-fanservice.md ORANGE tier

### 🕷️ LINGER — Horror texture
- **Texture:** dread through IMPLICATION, not description. Small wrongness accumulating. Things unsaid.
- **Pacing:** unhurried in the wrong way. The scene won't get to the point.
- **Vocabulary favored:** concrete sensory anchors that are SLIGHTLY wrong (a smell where there shouldn't be a smell, a sound that doesn't match the scene, a shadow a half-second too long)
- **Vocabulary banned:** dread-by-telling ("the atmosphere was ominous", "something felt off") — all horror gets to the reader through concrete wrongness only
- **Deploy for:** Moria-type islands, haunted atolls, the first scene of a horror-island arc, a dream sequence, the moment a villain's regime cruelty is revealed
- **Pairs with:** flashback.md (horror flashbacks), villain-kit.md HAUNT archetype

### 😂 MOTLEY — Comedy lean
- **Texture:** rapid, kinetic, misdirection, ensemble chaos, the wrong person responding to the wrong thing
- **Pacing:** fast. Beats stack. Comedy compounds.
- **Vocabulary favored:** interruption, stretched-word spellings, caps for shouting, onomatopoeic outbursts
- **Vocabulary banned:** ANY narrator wink, ANY "this was funny" framing — comedy lands on its own or not at all
- **Deploy for:** crew breakfast chaos, port-shopping misadventure, a scene that earns breathing room between heavy beats, the ensemble Salt War moments
- **Pairs with:** shonen-one-piece default (comedy is load-bearing by default — MOTLEY just cranks it up)

### 🧱 SEDIMENT — Drama lean
- **Texture:** weight, accumulation, the past pressing into the present, unresolved things that are still resolving slowly
- **Pacing:** measured. Sentences carry.
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific concrete past objects brought forward (a tea cup, a ribbon, a note), silence that communicates
- **Vocabulary banned:** any quick-fix emotional resolution, any hug-it-out ending
- **Deploy for:** Merdo's village-betrayal flashbacks, Melaina's mother-reveal saga arc, Darius-and-Rook parting night (if it happens on-camera), any scene where the WEIGHT of what happened is the point
- **Pairs with:** fight.md, flashback.md

### 🌗 MERIDIAN — Fantasy / liminal / mythic
- **Texture:** the scale of the moment elevates, the ordinary and the mythic touch, something in the scene is larger than the characters know
- **Pacing:** stately. Sentences have architecture.
- **Vocabulary favored:** the specific small detail placed against the large cosmic frame (a single gull against the sky becoming significant)
- **Vocabulary banned:** generic fantasy vocabulary (no "ancient", "mystical", "eternal", "primordial")
- **Deploy for:** Poneglyph moments, D-name reveals, Conqueror's first flicker, Lunarian history brushes, Void-Century implications, any moment that touches the saga's largest scale
- **Pairs with:** canon-* files, chekhov-gun-rack.md

### 🚀 QUASAR — Sci-fi lean
- **Texture:** ungrounded, velocity, the strange tech or devil-fruit acting at scale the world isn't ready for
- **Pacing:** forward-pitched, accelerating
- **Vocabulary favored:** exact mechanism, specific device behavior, clinical observation of the strange
- **Vocabulary banned:** mythic elevation (QUASAR is the opposite register from MERIDIAN; technology is a MACHINE here, not a mystery)
- **Deploy for:** Vegapunk scenes, Germa-type tech, Seastone applications, Dial-based combat, rare Pacifista or cyborg encounters
- **Pairs with:** realism-governors.md (tech specifics)

### 🪴 PATINA — Slice of life
- **Texture:** domestic detail, the rhythm of a day, the specific smell of a galley, the way a rope is wound, the sounds a ship makes at night
- **Pacing:** slow, textured, nowhere to go
- **Vocabulary favored:** senses in order (taste, smell, touch, sound, sight — the order matters)
- **Vocabulary banned:** urgency, plot advancement, stakes
- **Deploy for:** cover-page stories, mealtime scenes, port-shore-leave quiet moments, anyone learning a craft from anyone, the morning after a big fight before the news arrives
- **Pairs with:** cover-page-stories.md

### 🦁 KIRIN — Martial epic
- **Texture:** stillness, commitment, one strike with ten years behind it
- **Pacing:** compressed at the strike, densified around it
- **Vocabulary favored:** distance tracked as object, breath tracked as beat, zanshin held
- **Vocabulary banned:** comedy interrupting, narrator interiority during the strike, crowd reaction until AFTER
- **Deploy for:** Ashren default register, pre-finisher breath in major fights, rival duels, ideology-fight climaxes
- **Pairs with:** martial-epic-beats.md (its own module)

### 🪞 LIMINAL — Between-spaces, unreality
- **Texture:** threshold energy, between waking and sleep, port-that-doesn't-quite-exist, island that might be a dream
- **Pacing:** slippery — the scene's rules aren't quite the world's rules
- **Vocabulary favored:** "the door was open / the door was not open" kinds of unresolvable sensory clauses
- **Vocabulary banned:** final explanations (never explain the liminal — let it stand)
- **Deploy for:** Skypiea-style impossible islands, the occasional dream, arriving-at-an-island-before-dawn scenes where geography is still forming, encounters with ghosts or the dead
- **Pairs with:** LINGER (adjacent)

### 💀 CARRION — Aftermath, decay
- **Texture:** what's left after violence. The morning after. The bodies that weren't buried. The battlefield a day later.
- **Pacing:** still, exhausted
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific remnants (a glove, a broken rail, a single bloodstain), the bodies' specific post-mortem details if characters died
- **Vocabulary banned:** glamor of violence, the "cool" of death
- **Deploy for:** morning after a war-scale fight, Marineford-style aftermath, a village that was just sacked, the colosseum floor after the Cup is done
- **Pairs with:** reward-calibration.md (aftermath beats land rewards differently)

### 💎 SLICK — Smut / seduction
- **Texture:** awareness of body, tension in mundane action, charge in the air that doesn't need to speak
- **Pacing:** measured, deliberate, NOT rushed
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific concrete detail of the body moving through space, direct anatomical language when earned
- **Vocabulary banned:** euphemism, fade-to-black
- **Deploy for:** Tier 2-3 NSFW pre-scene (dialing into the heat before the escalation; see nsfw-fanservice.md for the full NSFW module)
- **Pairs with:** nsfw-fanservice.md Tier 2-3

### 🍷 VICE — Decadence, corruption, opulence with rot
- **Texture:** wealth and cruelty stacked; beautiful surface, ugly mechanism underneath
- **Pacing:** elegant, arranged
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific opulent detail (the exact food, the exact drink, the exact fabric) + the single wrong thing that reveals the underneath
- **Vocabulary banned:** generic "decadent" adjectives
- **Deploy for:** Celestial Dragon scenes (saga-late), Donquixote family gatherings (Yann Merlis at higher tier), merchant-council dining rooms, Tessara's VIP box at the colosseum
- **Pairs with:** villain-kit.md CHARMER + PARASITE archetypes

### ⚖️ GRAVITAS — Big speech, public weight
- **Texture:** a declaration in front of witnesses that reshapes the world's reading of something, stakes expressed aloud
- **Pacing:** slowed for the declaration, resumed after
- **Vocabulary favored:** simple direct Oda-vocabulary ("nakama", "live", "no"), public reaction chain (silence → gasp → spread)
- **Vocabulary banned:** verbose rhetoric, explanatory speech
- **Deploy for:** Merdo's first public declaration (he already made one: "I'm protecting this Lunarian by name" to Adaam Goroa — GRAVITAS), future bounty moments, Volo's public commitments, anyone's first-declared dream
- **Pairs with:** fight.md, anime-forum.md (GRAVITAS moments are often anime-forum triggers)

### 🧊 RESIDUE — Trauma as currency
- **Texture:** what the character carries from the past that won't put itself down
- **Pacing:** paused, returning, interrupted
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific sensory echoes (a smell that means something only to one person, a sound, a color)
- **Vocabulary banned:** therapy-speak about the trauma
- **Deploy for:** Kara's quiet moments with the fruit-failure, Merdo's pit-flashback triggers, Sable's CP-0 slip, Vesper's Marine-hospital memory, Ashren's Lunarian grief
- **Pairs with:** flashback.md, messy-characters.md

### 🌿 LOAM — Earth, labor, craft
- **Texture:** hands making something, the weight and feel of a tool, pride in work
- **Pacing:** steady, rhythmic
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific craft detail (Melaina on a stew, Sumi on her own jolly roger, Cress on a chart correction, Ashren on a blade)
- **Vocabulary banned:** abstraction, the "metaphor of craftsmanship"
- **Deploy for:** any scene where someone is MAKING something, the Golgatha repair scenes, weapon-care scenes, cooking scenes
- **Pairs with:** PATINA (adjacent)

### 🌊 TIDE — Travel, transition, horizon
- **Texture:** motion, open water, the between-islands feel, the Log Pose settling
- **Pacing:** rolling, cyclical
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific weather, specific sea state, the Golgatha's specific behavior at each kind of swell
- **Vocabulary banned:** generic sea-poetry
- **Deploy for:** transitions between arcs, the second/third day at sea, the first dawn after a major fight when the crew is sailing out
- **Pairs with:** PATINA, MERIDIAN

### 🎭 MUMMER — Performance, masks
- **Texture:** the play within the play, someone being something they're not, the audience that doesn't know
- **Pacing:** slightly theatrical, aware of itself
- **Vocabulary favored:** the specific theatrical detail (the makeup, the rehearsed smile, the exact line delivered too well)
- **Vocabulary banned:** breaking the mask too early (let the reader catch up)
- **Deploy for:** undercover scenes, Sable's CP-0 cover moments that resurface, Hudore's pre-rank-reveal scenes, Dressrosa-style theatrical-cruelty villains
- **Pairs with:** villain-kit.md CHARMER, anti-villain variants

### 🦋 GLASS — Fragility, breakability
- **Texture:** a moment that can't hold, something about to break, protecting something that can't be protected forever
- **Pacing:** held breath
- **Vocabulary favored:** specific small thing that could be lost (a person, an object, a truce)
- **Vocabulary banned:** reassurance, guarantees
- **Deploy for:** the moment before a crew member's wound is revealed, a child in danger, a peace that won't last, the first conversation where someone almost tells Merdo something important
- **Pairs with:** GLASS most often precedes CARRION or SEDIMENT

## HOW TO DEPLOY

**Option 1 — DM chooses per scene:** Recognize that a scene wants a genre flavor and silently dial it in. No player-facing announcement. The reader just FEELS the shift.

**Option 2 — Player requests:** Via `/dm genre X` — user asks for a specific dial for the next scene. DM applies for that scene, dials back to default after.

**Option 3 — Arc-level dial (rare):** Set a secondary genre for a whole arc. E.g., a horror-island arc might run LINGER texture throughout. Set in `campaign-overview.json` as `arc_genre: LINGER` or similar.

---

## RULES

- **Max ONE dial active at a time.** Stacking genres = prose mud. Pick one.
- **Base register (shonen-one-piece) is always underneath.** Genre dials are SECONDARY flavors ON TOP of the default. They do NOT replace the base.
- **Dial in, scene, dial out.** A dial is scene-scale, not session-scale (unless arc-level).
- **The dials are OPTIONAL.** Most scenes run default. Don't force-dial every scene for variety — that's its own kind of slop.
- **No genre dial overrides the anti-slop laws, length governor, or Oda gut-check.** Those are TIER 1 always-on rules per the priority hierarchy.

---

## INTEGRATION

- **Pairs with `arc-toggles.md`** — arc mood/law/cadence dials are BROADER (arc-level); genre dials are FINER (scene-level). They compose.
- **Pairs with `session-mood-request.md`** — player `/dm mood X` is a LIGHTER version of genre dial (session-level, broader palette).
- **Pairs with `prose-affinities.md`** — affinities are TECHNIQUE-level (telescoping, crescendo, undertow); genre dials are TONE-level. They compose.
- **Does NOT override `anti-slop-master.md`** — anti-slop laws win over any genre dial that would violate them.

## SELF-AUDIT

Before dialing in a genre:

1. ☐ Is this scene ACTUALLY asking for a different flavor, or am I just chasing variety?
2. ☐ Is only ONE dial going in?
3. ☐ Does the base shonen-one-piece register still anchor the scene?
4. ☐ Will I dial out at the scene's end?
5. ☐ Does the dial's vocabulary-banned list cover anything I was about to write?

If any fails, don't dial — run default.

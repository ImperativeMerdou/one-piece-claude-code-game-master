## Canon Character Encounters — Voice Fidelity Rules

When a canon Oda character appears on-screen in Merdo's saga — Luffy, Garp, Doflamingo, Shanks, whoever — their voice MUST be canon-accurate. Not fan-interpretation, not homage, not "close enough" — canon. This module encodes how.

---

## The iron rule

**Canon characters speak as Oda writes them. Period.**

If a canon character appears, the DM must:
1. Know their canon voice (catchphrases, laugh, verbal tics, stance)
2. Know their canon relationship to the current event
3. Keep them IN-CHARACTER even when interacting with OCs
4. NEVER have a canon character say something they wouldn't in Oda's manga

If the DM isn't sure how a canon character would react — HAVE THEM APPEAR BRIEFLY, let their canon vibe speak, then EXIT. Don't force long dialogues that risk character drift.

## Voice signatures (canonical key characters)

These fire when these characters appear. Nail the voice.

### MONKEY D. LUFFY
- **Laugh:** "shishishi" — short, bright, open.
- **Catchphrases:** "I'm gonna be King of the Pirates!" / "Meat!" / "You're my nakama!" (nakama = crew).
- **Speech pattern:** simple, direct, often food-related. Subjects dropped. Declarative.
- **Reactions:** Excitement to anything new / cool / dangerous. Food-motivated. Defensive when his crew is insulted.
- **Standing toward Merdo:** unknown-but-friendly-curious. If they meet — Luffy wants to fight or eat, possibly both.

Example Luffy in meanwhile-cut:
```
LUFFY: "Oi oi! MEAT! Nami, what's that poster?"
NAMI: "It's that new crew from their rough route."
LUFFY: "HORNS? That guy has HORNS?? SHISHISHI I wanna fight him!"
NAMI: "Focus, Luffy. We're in DRUM ISLAND."
LUFFY: "Meaat."
```

### RED-HAIRED SHANKS
- **Laugh:** "hyaha" / quiet chuckles. Not theatrical.
- **Catchphrases:** "That's fine" / calm wisdom / "Don't underestimate the new generation."
- **Speech pattern:** relaxed, warm, but every word counts. Rarely raises voice. When he does — the world listens.
- **Reactions:** Amused by rookies, protective of his crew, will drink with anyone, will kill for very specific violations.
- **Standing toward Merdo:** aware of him (Melaina-mother saga thread). Not yet hostile or friendly. Watchful.

### WHITEBEARD
- **Laugh:** "GURARARARA"
- **Catchphrases:** "My son" / "brat" / calls his crew sons and daughters.
- **Speech pattern:** slow, weighty, massive presence.
- **Reactions:** Family-centric. Protective of crew. Will die for his sons.
- **Standing toward Merdo:** unknown. Merdo is too rookie to register unless Ashren's Lunarian-status crosses a certain threshold.

### DONQUIXOTE DOFLAMINGO
- **Laugh:** "FUFUFUFU"
- **Catchphrases:** "Fufufu, interesting." / sadistic theatrics / "This is the age of..." monologues.
- **Speech pattern:** verbose, theatrical, cruel undertone, sunglasses-grin.
- **Reactions:** Amused by threats, cruel to subordinates who fail, obsessive about ancient Void Century material, weapons, and bloodlines.
- **Standing toward Merdo:** KEEN INTEREST via Yann Merlis. Wants the Lunarian. Wants the uncontrolled Zoan child (Kara). Wants the tournament product.

Doflamingo should appear in Tessara-arc meanwhile-cuts READING YANN'S DISPATCHES and laughing.

## When canon characters APPEAR in Merdo's saga

### Rule 1: Cameos are BRIEF
A canon character in a meanwhile-cut gets 3-5 lines at most. Rarely more. Even an entire scene with a canon character should be <8 lines. The longer they speak, the more drift risk.

### Rule 2: Mannerisms FIRST, plot SECOND
Canon characters are KNOWN for mannerisms. Open with a mannerism (Luffy asks about food, Garp laughs loudly, Doflamingo's sunglasses catch light, Shanks sips sake). The plot beat comes second.

### Rule 3: Canon characters ARRIVE with canon energy
When Shanks enters a scene, the mood SHIFTS. Everyone in the room KNOWS Shanks-energy before they know it's him. Don't flatten the presence.

### Rule 4: Canon characters LEAVE cleanly
Don't linger. Canon cameo = arrive, do the thing, exit. Let the reader's hunger for more drive them to canon.

### Rule 5: Canon characters DO NOT interact with Merdo as peers YET
Merdo is rookie-tier. Canon characters of power (Luffy excepted — he's also rookie-tier) will treat Merdo as an equal only when Merdo has earned it. Until then:
- Yonko / Admirals: regard Merdo as curiosity at most. Do not fight him at equal footing.
- Warlords: may engage through proxies (Doflamingo via Yann — perfect example).
- Canon rookies (Luffy, Zoro, Kid, Law, etc.): peer-level — can interact more equally, since they're also rising.

## What NOT to do with canon characters

- Put them in situations that contradict canon (e.g., Luffy at Tessara when he should be in Drum Island)
- Have them meet Merdo and become allies INSTANTLY (relationships earned)
- Give them power-levels or techniques they don't have in canon yet (pre-timeskip rules)
- Have them "pass the torch" or explicitly endorse Merdo (too fanficcy)
- Have them reveal saga spoilers (no one says "I'm your father" if they shouldn't yet)
- Stick them in settings they'd obviously avoid (Luffy doesn't attend political functions voluntarily)

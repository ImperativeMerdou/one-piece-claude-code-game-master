# SBS — Oda's Reader Q&A as Session-End Omake

**Canon Oda device.** SBS = "Shitsumon o Boshū Suru" — "I am taking questions." At the end of each manga volume, Oda answers absurd reader questions in handwritten margin notes. Mix of canon trivia, character info Oda invents on the spot, and pure jokes. The SBS is a beloved Oda institution.

This module gives the DM a way to deploy SBS-style Q&A as session-end omake (bonus content), a fan-pleasure feature for the user.

---

## Canon SBS examples to model

Real Oda SBS answers (paraphrased):
- Q: "What's Zoro's favorite food?" A: "Anything that goes well with sake."
- Q: "Who would win, Luffy or Ace?" A: "Their grandpa."
- Q: "Are Luffy's eyes brown or black?" A: "Brown! Definitely brown!" (then the next volume he draws them black)
- Q: "What does Sanji do with his leftover food?" A: (Oda draws Sanji feeding the leftovers to small animals on the deck)
- Q: "Why doesn't Robin ever wear pants?" A: "She does! In her room!"
- Q: "What's the Going Merry's actual horsepower?" A: "Brave horse units. We don't measure in human horsepower."
- Q: "Where does Brook keep his afro?" A: "On his head. The skull's just decorative." (this is wrong; Oda's playing along)

The vibe: half-real-fact, half-joke, all-affectionate. The questions can be deeply lore (height, age, blood type) or absurd (favorite food, what would they do on a vacation, which crew member is the worst at chess).

## When to deploy SBS

### Session-end omake
After NEXT TIME ON, occasionally drop an SBS:

```
─────────────── SBS ─── volume break ───────────────

Q: "Mr. Oda, what does Merdo do when nobody's watching?"
   — H.J., Loguetown

A: He carves wooden figures. He's bad at them. The
   one he keeps in his coat pocket is supposed to be
   his mother. He doesn't show it to anyone. Also he
   eats mangos in secret. Vesper knows.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### Inter-arc bonus content
Between major arcs, drop 3-5 SBS questions in one block:

```
─────────────── SBS — VOLUME 4 BONUS ───────────────

Q: "Mr. Oda, what's everyone's favorite food?"
   — A. Y., Logue Town

A: Merdo: Mangos (in secret. Beef will get him fired
   from the universe.)
   Ashren: Pressed flowers? No. Probably tea.
   Melaina: Whatever she's currently making.
   Vesper: Coffee at sunset.
   Sable: Whatever she just swallowed.
   Volo: Things that come with sandwiches.
   Sumi: Bread with jam, all the jam.
   Nox: Anything but actually he's an excellent cook,
        he's just denying it.
   Kara: She's still finding out. Anything Melaina
        makes for her.

Q: "Who has the worst sense of direction on the crew?"
   — Anonymous, Drum Island

A: It's a tie between Volo and Sumi. Volo because he
   thinks every direction is the right direction with
   enough conviction. Sumi because she has no idea
   where anything is and refuses to ask. Oleg keeps
   them out of trouble with a raised eyebrow that does
   the instruction for him.

Q: "Will Merdo ever eat a Devil Fruit?"
   — too curious, North Blue

A: SECRETS, READER. You'll see.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### When the player asks a meta question
If the user asks something off-canvas ("what's Sumi's birthday?" / "does Sable like coffee?") — answer in SBS format. Makes the metaphysical question feel canon-grounded.

## SBS question types (rotate)

### A. The lore question (canon-respectful)
Genuine character info that hasn't been on-screen yet.
- Heights, weights, ages
- Birthdays
- Blood types
- Favorite foods / colors
- What would they do on a day off
- Hometown specifics
- Species details

### B. The absurd-but-revealing question
Fan-flavored, meant to land character truth through silliness.
- "Who has the worst pun habit?" → reveals comic engine
- "Who would win in a foot race?" → answers with character logic
- "What does X smell like?" → mock answer revealing character

### C. The shipping question
Oda PRETENDS not to take these seriously but answers anyway.
- "Are Sable and Merdo a thing?" — Sable IS a thing. Merdo is too tall to notice.
- "Does Vesper have a crush on Rook?" — that's between them. SBS doesn't tell.

### D. The 4th-wall absurdism
Pure joke, Oda playing with the reader.
- "Mr. Oda why are you the way you are?" — I asked my editor. He said it's late.
- "Will the Merdo Pirates beat Luffy?" — They'd have a great fight. Then they'd eat together.
- "How do Devil Fruits work?" — Devilly.

### E. The character-prediction question (saga-tease)
Oda hints without committing.
- "Will Sumi ever sing?" — When the drums play. (this is a Joy-Boy plant — only fire saga-late)
- "What's Melaina's mother's name?" — That's her secret. (saga-tease)

## Format

Always:
- Q: question line + signature (random reader name + city)
- A: Oda-tone answer, often illustrated-in-prose (since we can't draw, USE the in-prose moment)

Two columns is fine. Three Qs in a row is fine. The structure is loose.

---

## Tone — Oda voice

The SBS Oda voice:
- Warm and joking
- Sometimes annoyed (the "stop asking about THIS, I'll never answer" gag)
- Occasionally serious (when the question genuinely matters)
- Plays along with absurd questions
- Refuses to spoil the manga ("Save your spoilers, reader, the manga still has years left!")
- Uses "—" / "—!" / "?!" punctuation
- Sometimes draws a tiny illustration mid-answer (in prose: do a single italicized scene-direction)

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't reveal saga-level spoilers in SBS (no Joy Boy, no Shanks-Melaina, no D.-clan reveals)
- Don't make every SBS plot-relevant — some are pure trivia
- Don't drop SBS more than once per 2-3 sessions
- Don't break Oda's voice (no analyst-cadence, no corporate vocabulary, no fan-trolling)
- Don't make the SBS reader seem smarter than the user — readers are casual fans, not theorists

---

## Suggested rhythm

- 1 SBS per 2-3 sessions, average
- Group 2-4 questions when you fire one
- Mix question types (lore + absurd + shipping + 4th-wall)
- Occasionally a SINGLE SBS (one Q, one A) as a quick warmth beat at session end

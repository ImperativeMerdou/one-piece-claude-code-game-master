## Flashback Triggers

Per the One Piece Fight Law (research file): "Mid-fight flashbacks must be triggered by a present-tense emotional crisis — a moment where the fighter faces a wall they cannot climb with their current understanding. The flashback delivers either a conceptual key or an emotional obligation. It should change the audience's reading of the fight retroactively."

Per the campaign bible: "Flashback deployment — at emotional peak, never beginning."

---

## When to fire

A flashback is justified ONLY when ALL of these are true:
1. The character is at an emotional peak (about to give up, OR about to break through).
2. The flashback delivers either (a) a conceptual key — "how to break through" or (b) an emotional obligation — "why I cannot lose here."
3. The flashback recontextualizes everything that came before in the scene.

WRONG times for flashbacks:
- Beginning of a scene (no investment built)
- Before a fight (frontload backstory in EARLIER chapters)
- During routine exchanges (no crisis pressure)
- More than once per scene (kills momentum)

In prose, keep flashbacks SHORT — a paragraph to a page max. Long flashbacks that delay action are anime padding, not One Piece structure.

## Triggers tied to specific Merdo-crew wounds

These are the established backstory anchors. Fire them when the present scene reaches their emotional pressure:

### MERDO
- **The pit.** Seven years of fighting in slave pits. Triggers: when Merdo is being humiliated publicly, when the crowd is hostile, when he's beaten down and getting up anyway, when he's offered surrender. Visual: the smell of blood and sand, a name being called, a younger Merdo learning to laugh through pain.
- **The betrayal at 15.** His village (Mesut) sold him. Triggers: when someone close betrays him, when family/community is being tested, when he meets someone from Mesut. NOT YET DEPLOYED on screen — saga-level reveal.
- **First Armament Haki activation (Goroa, pulling Kara from petrified mass).** This already happened on-page. Re-trigger when Haki flares again — link the protective instinct.
- **Idol Kaido (cautionary admiration).** Triggers: when Merdo is leaning into force-as-truth ideology, when he's becoming the thing he should fear. Visual: a poster, a name overheard, a moment of recognition.

### ASHREN
- **The hunt.** World Government extermination of Lunarians. He survived. He doesn't know how many others did. Triggers: when his wings/flame are exposed publicly, when he hears another Lunarian rumor, when he's offered information about his kind, when the weight of being last gets too heavy.
- **Why he doesn't laugh.** Connected to his isolation. Save for late saga.

### MELAINA
- **Her mother.** Held alive but unreachable. Triggers: protecting Kara, archives/records being destroyed or recovered, anyone speaking of suppressed history, AND — saga-late — proximity to Shanks or his crew. AUTHOR TRUTH: Shanks holds her mother. Do not reveal early.
- **The mourning bands.** Each one is a person she lost (NOT her mother). Triggers: a crewmate in mortal danger, the loss of an ally on an island, public memorials.

### VESPER
- **The boy on the Clementia.** Marine hospital ship, protocol prioritized an officer over a dying boy. Triggers: when she's being told to triage an enemy lower than an ally, when the medical-rule is challenged, when a kid is hurt in front of her.

### SABLE
- **Lucci's report.** Compromised asset. Filed because she got attached. Triggers: CP-0 / CP-9 in the area, someone using her real name (Irina), seeing a CP-0 button, or — critically — someone she likes finding out.

### VOLO
- **Being collected.** People who admired him as property. Performance was the only power. Triggers: praise that feels like ownership, someone offering to "buy" him or "showcase" him, a crowd cheering him for the wrong reason, OR — the inverse — someone seeing him as a person and not asking for the show.

### SUMI (NIKA-CHAN)
- **The day she ate the fruit.** She's 16. She picked the fruit up because she thought "Pirate Queen Sumi" sounded better with a Devil Fruit. Triggers: when her overconfidence costs her, when she meets an actual Pirate Queen-tier figure (like Hancock if encountered later), when she fails Mako or Kara.
- **Why she calls herself Nika-chan.** Someone called her "Nika" once thinking she WAS the Sun God. She ran with it. Triggers: encountering real myth/religion around Joy Boy or the Liberation drums, late saga.

### NOX
- **The stickup gang Merdo broke up.** Where Nox came from. Triggers: meeting another former gang member, having to choose between his old life and the Pirates. AND: the day Merdo let him keep the guns. That's his core memory.
- **Norbert Hoyt.** His real name. He'd rather die than have it spoken. Triggers: when discovered (probably comedic at first, layered later).

### KARA ODINU
- **Her first transformation killed her father.** Triggers: any uncontrolled transformation, any time she's losing control, any time she's being treated like a danger.
- **Mira Odinu, her mother.** Triggers: meeting another mother figure (Melaina is already this), receiving mail, hearing news from Goroa.

## The Palimpsest Echo (5-step prose technique)

Adapted from Celia's Memory Flashbacks. **ABSOLUTE LAW: do NOT use the words "like", "as if", or "reminded of" in a flashback. Show, don't compare.**

### Step 1 — ANCHOR (concrete present detail)
Start with something physically present in the current scene. One sensory detail. Sharp.

> The smell of sand and Marine boot-leather rose from the colosseum floor.

### Step 2 — LAYERING (overlay past sensory data, NO transitional language)
Without "suddenly," "and then," or "he remembered" — drop the past sensory data ON TOP. Just appears.

> Sand. The smell of sand and blood.
> A voice he hadn't heard in years: "Get up. They paid to see you bleed."

### Step 3 — CREST (single sharp disjointed fragment)
One unprocessed image. Out of context. Hard.

> Twelve years old. Ribs bandaged. The boy looked up.

### Step 4 — DISSOLUTION (the past fades, present returns)
The sensory anchor returns. The past dissolves. Format with the dissolving image getting weaker:

> The boy laughed.
> *(getting up, getting up)*
> The colosseum sand was warm under Merdo's boot.

### Step 5 — CONSCIOUSNESS CASCADE (asterisked stream-of-consciousness, brief)
A single bracketed thought-fragment that bridges past to present. Optional but powerful.

> *(get up. always.)*

### The full beat in practice:

```
The smell of sand and Marine boot-leather rose from the colosseum floor.

╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌

Sand. The smell of sand and blood.
A voice he hadn't heard in years: "Get up. They paid to see you bleed."
Twelve years old. Ribs bandaged. The boy looked up.
The boy laughed.
*(getting up, getting up)*

╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌

The colosseum sand was warm under Merdo's boot.

*(get up. always.)*

He stood up.
```

The Marine captain stepped back.

## Format (compact form for shorter flashbacks)

Flashbacks should feel like a film cut. Use formatting to mark the shift:

```
The Marine captain's gauntlet caught Merdo across the jaw.
He went down on one knee. Blood in his teeth. The crowd
roared.

╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌

  Sand. The smell of sand and blood.
  A boy, twelve, ribs bandaged. The pit master's voice:
  "Get up. They paid to see you bleed, not to see you stay
  down."
  The boy looked up. The boy laughed.
  The boy got up.

╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌╌

Merdo stood up.
The crowd went silent.
The Marine captain stepped back.
```

Use `╌╌╌` dashed lines to mark the cut in/out. Keep flashback prose SHORT and SENSORY — smell, sound, light, one detail. Not exposition.

## Conceptual key vs emotional obligation

A flashback should deliver one of two things:

**CONCEPTUAL KEY** — "how to break through." Zoro hears the breath of all things and cuts steel. Luffy realizes Katakuri's timing. The flashback gives the fighter a tool they can use IN THE NEXT BREATH.

**EMOTIONAL OBLIGATION** — "why I cannot lose here." Usopp's voice in Lucci. Zoro's promise to Luffy. The flashback gives the fighter a debt they cannot leave unpaid.

Pick one, not both. Then return to the scene with the fighter doing exactly what the flashback unlocked.

---

## What NOT to do

- DON'T narrate Merdo's flashbacks as settled internal events. He is the player. The flashback can be visible to him in fragments — but the player decides what they MEAN to him.
- DON'T deploy crew flashbacks (Ashren, Vesper, etc.) without crew consent — the player runs Merdo but the bible's author truths stay locked until earned.
- DON'T do flashbacks back-to-back. ONE per scene maximum.
- DON'T extend a flashback past a page in prose. Frontload longer backstory in earlier chapters / arc setup, never mid-fight.
- DON'T explain the flashback's meaning. The juxtaposition IS the meaning.

## Honorifics & Address — Japanese Register + Per-Character Usage

**Hard rule, top of file, user directive:** Nobody calls Merdo "Captain." Not the crew, not civilians, not Marines, not merchants, not Volo, not Sumi, not Mako, not allies, not enemies. Not "senchou," not "taichou," not "Cap'n," not "Captain-sama" — none of it. The register does not exist in this campaign.

Merdo is **"Merdo"** — by name, always, regardless of scene weight, crisis register, hero-worship, sarcasm, ceremony, or formality. Marines who want to insult him use **"pirate"** or (when earned) his world-given **epithet**. Yann Merlis uses continental-formal **"Merdo, my friend."** Hudore uses **"sir"** as distancing classification or **"pirate"** as classification. Merchants call him **"sir"** in English or drop honorifics entirely.

If the DM finds a line about to form that addresses Merdo as Captain in any language, register, or ironic frame — REWRITE the line. Default to "Merdo" or bare address.

| Honorific | Meaning | Register | Canon anchor |
|---|---|---|---|
| **-san** | Polite, neutral respect | Everyday / professional | Robin: "Navigator-san." |

| **-chan** | Affectionate, cute, close | Soft / teasing | Hancock: "Chopper-chan." |
| **-sama** | Lord / lady, reverent | Hierarchical, often worshipful or sardonic | "Hancock-sama." |
| **-sensei** | Teacher, master of craft | Respectful-professional | "Kureha-sensei." |
| **-dono** | Old-style respect | Archaic / Wano | "Oden-dono." |

Note: the "-taichou / -senchou" (captain) row is intentionally omitted from in-play use for Merdo. Other canon characters may still carry it (Marines addressing their own captains, etc.). Not on Merdo.

## Per-character address patterns — crew calls Merdo "Merdo"

All crewmembers use the bare name **"Merdo"** when addressing him directly. No honorifics. No "Captain." No register-shifts to Captain under crisis, ceremony, or hero-worship. The variation between characters comes from how they *frame the name* and what other honorifics they use on each other — not from any Merdo-as-Captain shift.

- **Ashren:** "Merdo." Bare. Minimal dialogue across the board.
- **Melaina:** "Merdo." Sometimes yelled: "MERDO!" across the deck — volume is the claim, no honorific. Never "Captain" even sarcastically.
- **Vesper:** "Merdo" default. Under medical stress: "Merdo, SIT." (imperative register, not Captain). Never "-san" — she's not polite.
- **Sable:** "Merdo" default. Teasing: "Merdo-kun." Under operational stress, her CP-0 cover used to want a rank-register — she suppresses it now; cover shows through as over-formal English syntax ("If I may, Merdo — the signet —") not as a Captain drop.
- **Volo:** "MERDO!" loud. "MERDO-SAN!" when theatrical. Japanese honorifics land slightly wrong on him — he uses them as flourish, not correctly. No "senchou."
- **Sumi:** "MERDO!" exclusively, loud. Kid-energy. She tries "Merdo-senpai" once as an audition — crew cringes, she drops it. No "Captain" ever.
- **Nox:** "Merdo" plain. Once per saga, whispered: genuine, soft — just "Merdo," the only time his grin stops.
- **Kara:** "Merdo." Or silent address entirely — she's often just looking. No honorifics.
- **Mako:** "MERDO!" loud hero-worship. Bare name.

### Merdo → crew

Merdo's honorific use is minimal — pit-fighter grammar, subjects dropped, names bare.

- Ashren → "Ashren" always.
- Vesper → "Doc" or "Vesper." Never -sensei.
- Melaina → "Melaina" always. Never "-sama" even ironically.
- Kara → "kid" mostly; "Kara" when serious.
- Sumi → "kid" if accepted; "Sumi" when seeing her as real.
- Nox → "Nox" if accepted. Never his real name aloud.
- Enemies → shortest version of the name (disrespect via compression). "Hudore." Not "Commodore Hudore-san."

### Crew → crew (honorifics still live here)

- Ashren → Melaina: "Melaina." Tension precisely in the absence of honorific.
- Melaina → Vesper: "Vesper" or "doctor." Never -chan.
- Vesper → Melaina: "Melaina-san" when irritated. Medical politeness = cold.
- Volo → anyone: "[Name]-SAN!" loud, constantly. He IS the honorific engine for the crew.
- Sable → varies by audience. Alone: bare names. Public: English formality on outsiders only.
- Sumi → Kara: "Kara-chan" (older-sister). Never Kara-san.
- Nox ↔ Mako: plain, same age bracket.
- Kara → older crew: "-san" default. Respectful newcomer.
- The Twenty → crew: sailor English, no Japanese. "Commander" for Ashren occasionally.

### Enemy / neutral → Merdo

- Marines → Merdo: "pirate" bare. After epithet fires: "[epithet] Merdo" or just "[epithet]."
- Merchant council: "sir" or "Merdo" directly (no scam honorifics). If someone reaches for "Captain Merdo," the DM rewrites the line before sending.
- Yann Merlis: "Merdo, my friend" (the *my friend* is the tell that he wants something). Continental-formal English.
- Hudore: "sir" or "pirate." Both are classification, not respect.
- Parseval: "pirate." Then "Merdo" when praying over him.
- Ezrad: "horned one."

### Civilians → Merdo

- Port civilians: "sir" (English). Japanese "-san" sometimes regionally. Never "Captain."
- Kids: "Pirate! Pirate!" pointing; "MISTER PIRATE!" for attention. Or the newest epithet the world has filed.
- Merchants: "friend," "sir," or "Merdo" directly.

## Deployment

**Sparingly.** Honorifics are spice, not bread. Use at:
- Introduction moments (signals who someone is)
- Shift moments — dropping/adding an honorific = relationship changed. THE ADDITION/REMOVAL is the beat.
- Comedy (Volo using -san on a crate; Sumi's -senpai audition cringe)
- Ceremonial / negotiation moments between non-crew characters
- Wano / Fish-Man / cultural arcs (register up — -dono, -sensei, -sama common)

**Don't:** use on every line (numbs); force on Merdo (he doesn't use them, and nobody uses "Captain" on him).

**Format:** attach Japanese-style: "Kara-chan", "Merdo-san" (when Volo is being theatrical), "Allun-dono" (Parseval's prayer register). English + Japanese blend signals scam-courtesy or cross-cultural friction.

**Drift rule:** honorific usage CHANGES over time between crew. The drift IS the relationship arc. Minimum three arcs before a shift, unless death/betrayal/rescue earns it faster.

**Voice beat:** Volo is the honorific engine — loud, wrong, constantly, on EVERYTHING EXCEPT Merdo. Everyone else uses them precisely and rarely. The contrast is the texture.

## Cross-Crew Encounters — When Captain Meets Captain

**Per canon research:** the Sabaody Archipelago (canon ch. 490-522) is the TEMPLATE. All eleven Supernovas converging in one location WITHOUT fighting. They acknowledge, size up, establish hierarchy through PRESENCE, not combat.

This module governs scenes where the Danzai Pirates meet ANOTHER pirate crew (rookie, Supernova, Warlord, Yonko-aligned). The grammar of these scenes is different from combat, different from social, different from political.

Opens fully at SAGA 3 (Sabaody Convergence) but can fire sparsely in Saga 2 when a single rival crew is encountered.

---

## The canon grammar (5 laws)

### 1. Opening is about TERRITORY and RESPECT, not combat

Captains don't fight on first meeting. They claim space. They size up.

Canon: Kid doesn't fight Luffy at Sabaody — he talks about taking Luffy's treasure. Law doesn't fight Luffy — he OBSERVES him. Hawkins reads Luffy's fate. Mihawk at the Baratie sizes up Zoro without engaging fully.

**In prose:** the scene's tension is about POSITIONING. Who stands where. Who speaks first. Whose crew moves closer. No named attacks fire in a first-meeting scene.

### 2. Power is established through PRESENCE and HISTORY, not displays

"I've heard about you" carries more weight than a power demonstration. "I'm stronger" said ONCE by someone who means it is louder than a punch.

**Silence after a statement is more threatening than a shout.** Scarcity rules this register.

### 3. Respect emerges from shared danger OR worthy opposition

Canon: Luffy vs. Katakuri starts as "I will execute you," evolves into "you are worthy of my back." Luffy vs. Law: starts as suspicious alliance, becomes genuine partnership built on mutual risk-taking.

The respect beat usually lands in a LATER scene, not the first encounter. First encounters set the stage; later arcs cash it.

### 4. The conversation establishes WORLD-VIEW alignment or opposition

Kid wants to take FROM others. Luffy wants to protect HIS crew. They disagree on what piracy MEANS. This is not said bluntly — it emerges in one exchange, maybe two sentences each.

Mihawk respects Zoro because Zoro PURSUES MASTERY, not victory.
Doflamingo despises Luffy because Luffy refuses to accept "the world's rules."

**For Merdo:** his philosophy — "every throne, their food, leave" — will clash with captains who WANT thrones, who keep food, who stay. Design rival encounters to surface these clashes.

### 5. Bounties are PARTLY ACCURATE, PARTLY WRONG

Kid's bounty might be higher than Luffy's, but Luffy's actual threat level is higher because Luffy INSPIRES PEOPLE TO JOIN HIM. Bounties are the world's BEST GUESS, not the ground truth.

**For Merdo:** when he meets a higher-bounty rival, that rival may NOT actually be more dangerous to him. The ACTUAL danger is visible in posture, voice, how the rival's crew moves around them.

## Encounter tiers (what each tier looks like)

### Tier 1 — Rookie captain, equal bounty-band
Peer encounter. Mutual sizing-up. Alliance potential. Possible future rival. SUPERNOVA-style.

**Canon:** Luffy meets Kid, Law, Bonney, Apoo, etc. at Sabaody.
**For Merdo:** Saga 2-3 encounters with other pre-timeskip rookies. By Saga 3 (Sabaody Convergence) the full 11 canon Supernovas + Merdo on the same stage.

### Tier 2 — Higher-bounty rival, established captain
Established captain with a known reputation, higher bounty band than Merdo. Respect or dismissal — rarely alliance on first meet.

**Canon:** Luffy meets Jinbe (at Impel Down), Luffy meets pre-war Whitebeard-fleet captains.
**For Merdo:** Saga 3-5. A captain who's already been in the New World.

### Tier 3 — Warlord-level encounter
Government-sanctioned strong. Different register — Warlords represent SYSTEMS, not ambitions. Meeting a Warlord is meeting the World Government's IMAGINATION made flesh.

**Canon:** Luffy meets Mihawk (multiple points), Crocodile (Alabasta), Doflamingo (Dressrosa), Boa Hancock (Amazon Lily), Blackbeard (multiple).
**For Merdo:** Saga 4-6. Likely Saga 5 (Warlord Confrontation — possibly Doflamingo if the Yann Merlis plant cashes that).

### Tier 4 — Yonko Commander encounter
A Yonko's SON, RIGHT HAND, or INFRASTRUCTURE CAPTAIN. They don't represent themselves — they represent the Yonko.

**Canon:** Luffy meets Katakuri (Big Mom's son), Marco the Phoenix (Whitebeard's right hand), King (Kaido's #1), Shanks's crewmates.
**For Merdo:** Saga 6-8. These are the highest-stakes SURVIVABLE encounters.

### Tier 5 — Yonko themselves
Rare. Weighty. The encounter itself is a saga-weight event regardless of whether combat happens.

**Canon:** Luffy meets Big Mom (WCI), Kaido (Wano), Blackbeard (post-Marineford), Shanks (flashback + various).
**For Merdo:** Saga 6-9. Expect one Yonko encounter per 2 sagas AT MOST. Usually with intermediary distance (the Yonko notices Merdo, names him, but doesn't directly fight). Direct Yonko combat is endgame-tier.

### Tier 6 — Admiral / Fleet Admiral / Gorosei / Imu
The government's apex. Different register — these are INSTITUTIONS made flesh. Encountering them is like encountering the ocean itself. Survival is the victory.

**Canon:** Sabaody Kizaru, Marineford Admirals, Wano Kizaru revisit, Egghead Admiral attacks.
**For Merdo:** Saga 7+. Endgame-tier.

## Format: the first-meeting scene

### Opening (territory established)

- **The CREW of the other captain is visible first.** You see their flag, their ship, their deckhands before you see their captain. This builds the captain's mythology.
- **The meeting location matters.** At a dock, in a bar, on a neutral island, on a Marine ship, in a Yonko's territory — location shifts the register.
- **Merdo's crew takes positions.** Sumi steps forward loudly. Nox readies Mama and Papa behind his back. Melaina hums (cold register). Ashren goes still. Volo demands witness. Sable is already somewhere nobody noticed. This is the crew-reaction-order per `island-arrival.md` applied to an encounter.

### First exchange (sizing up)

- One captain speaks. Not Merdo first unless the player drives.
- The line says ONE THING about that captain's worldview.
- Merdo responds in his voice register: subjects dropped, direct, one sentence.
- Silence after.

### Beat 2 — the tell

- The OTHER captain's crew reacts to Merdo's response. That reaction tells the reader more than any power display.
- A henchman flinches. A navigator writes something. A first mate looks at their captain.

### Beat 3 — the resolution (non-combat)

The scene does NOT end in a fight. It ends in one of:
- **Mutual acknowledgment** ("I'll remember your name.")
- **Dismissal with threat** ("You're beneath my notice. For now.")
- **Alliance offer** ("My war is your war. Or it will be.")
- **Rival declaration** ("Next time we meet, one of us dies.")
- **Walk-away** (no words, turn away, the other captain watches them leave)

### Example — a Saga 3 Sabaody-convergence first-meet (future template)

```
Kid's crew occupied the bar already. The lights were low
where they sat. Three tables around them were empty — not
because people had left, because people hadn't dared.

Merdo's crew took the table by the window. Sumi was
halfway into a chalk sketch on the back of a beer coaster
before she saw Kid and froze. Nox whistled low through his
teeth — a sound that wasn't bullshit for once.

Kid turned his head a quarter. His crew saw him see them.
His crew waited.

"Merdo," Kid said. He didn't raise his voice. "Heard about
Tessara. That was sloppy."

Merdo didn't sit yet. "Got the bounty anyway."

"Bounties count what the world saw. I count what you
didn't finish." Kid's hand rose — not fast, not slow —
and the bottle on his table moved two inches toward him
without him touching it. "You left the merchant council
standing."

"Wasn't my chair to take."

Kid looked at him for a long beat. Somewhere behind Kid,
his gunner — a woman with a scar across her jaw — wrote
something in a ledger.

"I'll remember that," Kid said. "Sit down if you want."

Merdo sat.

Volo, behind him, started to demand witness. Merdo raised
one finger without looking back. Volo swallowed it.

In the north corner, Trafalgar Law was watching. He had
been watching the whole time.
```

Notes:
- 250 words
- Three captains present (Kid visible, Merdo foreground, Law in the wings)
- No combat
- Each captain says ONE THING revealing worldview (Kid: "what you didn't finish" = take-it-all philosophy; Merdo: "wasn't my chair to take" = his throne-philosophy)
- One henchman tell (Kid's gunner writing)
- One silent observer (Law — future alliance or future rival seeded)
- Ends on the seeded observation, not resolved

## What NOT to do

- DON'T let first-meets end in combat. The SCARCITY of combat is what makes these scenes ring.
- DON'T have Merdo's crew attack first. They posture, they don't fire.
- DON'T reveal the other captain's full abilities on first meet. Keep it mysterious.
- DON'T over-populate Sabaody Convergence. Canon shows the Supernovas across multiple scenes, not all in one room. Merdo's crew should meet 2-3 rival captains per session, not 11.
- DON'T skip the henchman-tell beat. The OTHER crew's reaction is what LANDS the captain's weight.
- DON'T let canon characters feel like fan-service. They ACT per their canon personalities. Kid would never compliment Merdo warmly; Law would never reveal a plan; Shanks would always offer a drink.

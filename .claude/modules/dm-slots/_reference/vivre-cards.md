# Vivre Cards — Canon Character Stat-Block Format

**Vivre Card Databook** is the canon Oda companion publication — a binder of single-card character profiles with standardized fields. Each card has the character's photo, vital stats, dream, fears, hobbies, what they like / don't like, and a short profile.

Recreating Vivre Card profiles for the Danzai Pirates crew gives the player a CANON FAN-DATABASE FEEL. He can request to "see Sumi's Vivre Card" and the DM produces it in canon format.

The full crew Vivre Cards live at `world-state/campaigns/one-piece-adventure/VIVRE_CARDS.md`. This module describes the format and how to deploy.

---

## Canon Vivre Card fields

Per the Vivre Card Databook — every entry has:

- **Name** (English + Japanese where applicable)
- **Epithet / Nickname**
- **Age**
- **Height**
- **Weight** (Oda gives precise — but only for canon analytical-character vibes)
- **Birthday** (Oda gives a specific date)
- **Blood type**
- **Affiliation** (crew name)
- **Position / Role**
- **Origin** (where they're from)
- **Devil Fruit** (if any)
- **Bounty** (current)
- **Dream** (one sentence, drawable)
- **Likes** (3-5 items)
- **Dislikes** (3-5 items)
- **Hobbies** (1-3)
- **Profile** (short paragraph — biography summary)
- **Power overview** (1-3 sentences)

Plus the **vital first-impression line**: a single quote that captures the character.

---

## When to deploy a Vivre Card

The player can REQUEST one at any time:
- "Show me Sumi's Vivre Card"
- "Vivre Card for Yann Merlis"
- "What's Ashren's Vivre Card look like?"

The DM responds with the formatted card from `VIVRE_CARDS.md` (for crew) or generates one inline (for NPCs the player has met enough times).

The DM can also OFFER one at natural moments:
- After a new character's first major scene
- At session-end omake
- Between arcs (transition content)
- After a significant character beat reveals new info

## Tone — what makes a Vivre Card feel canon

- **Specifics over abstracts.** Heights to the centimeter. Birthdays as actual dates. Blood type stated.
- **Likes / dislikes are mundane.** Not "freedom" / "tyranny." Things like "fish stew with too much pepper" / "the smell of damp ropes."
- **Profile reads like fan-database, not narrative.** Third-person, factual, short.
- **The quote is a single ICONIC LINE the character has said.** Not invented for the card — pulled from actual play or established canon.
- **Power overview names the ABILITIES, not the personality.** Save personality for the profile.

---

## What the player gets

A persistent reference for his crew that FEELS like he could pull it off the bookshelf next to his manga volumes. A reward for engagement. A piece of canon-style fan service the rest of the campaign earns by being good.

When new NPCs become important — or after a major character beat reveals something new — UPDATE their Vivre Card. The DM can do this in-channel ("Vivre Card updated: Sumi's bounty reissued at ฿15M") or quietly between sessions.

---

## NPC Vivre Cards

For one-shot or arc-villain NPCs, the DM can generate Vivre Cards on-the-fly when requested. Use the same format. Even minor characters (Partho the bookmaker, Madame Pell) can have a brief card if requested — that IS the canon Oda treatment of side characters in the Vivre Card Databook.

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't generate Vivre Cards for characters the player hasn't actually met
- Don't put SPOILER content in cards (Sumi's Vivre Card does NOT mention Joy Boy)
- Don't make the cards perfect / aspirational. Cards include LOSSES, FAILURES, AWKWARD HOBBIES.
- Don't update without the player witnessing the events that justify the update
- Don't break canon Vivre formatting in pursuit of "cool" — the canon format IS the appeal

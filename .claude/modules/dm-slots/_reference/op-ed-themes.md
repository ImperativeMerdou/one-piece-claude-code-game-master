## OP/ED Themes — Saga Identification Through Opening/Ending Songs

**Per canon research:** One Piece uses opening themes as SAGA MARKERS. Each saga gets a new opening song that identifies it musically. Players come to recognize: "When this song plays, we're in Dressrosa (Crazy Rainbow)." Sound is world-building.

This module governs how the DM uses OP/ED theme REFERENCES in prose to mark saga transitions and structural shifts. Pairs tightly with `OST_LIBRARY.md` (campaign file) for track-to-saga mapping.

---

## The 4 canon functions of OP/ED

### 1. OP change signals saga entry
The moment an opening theme changes, the canon anime is saying: "This is a new saga." Fans recognize the shift before ANY in-world event confirms it. Sound arrives first.

### 2. OP music carries the saga's TONE
- "We Are!" (OP1) — hopeful, adventurous, crew-building
- "Believe" (OP3) — kingdom-scale, political-stakes-adjacent
- "Crazy Rainbow" (OP8) — fast, intense, the TONE SHIFT of Water 7
- "Share the World" (OP10) — globally scaled, world-stage awareness
- "We Go!" (OP19) — competitive, tournament-lean
- "Tan Tan Tann!!" (OP25) — family-crisis, emotional

The OP TELLS you what the saga is about before an arc plays.

### 3. OP becomes iconic to its saga
Years later, fans associate "Believe" with Alabasta's desert liberation. The OP+arc IS a memory. Using a saga's OP as prose reference instantly gives the reader emotional context.

### 4. ED provides emotional resolution + world-reaction beats
Endings show: bounty updates, Marines responding, civilians affected. EDs are where the WORLD REACTS to what the saga just did.

## Deployment rules for the DM

**Fire frequency:** max 1 OP reference + 1 ED reference per session. Usually at session open (OP) and session close (ED).

**Saga transition:** at the FIRST session of a new saga, the OP reference changes. Announce it:

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
                    DANZAI PIRATES
                      SAGA 2 OPEN
                "Believe" — kingdom-scale stakes
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

Then the session proceeds. The reader/player knows the tonal reset happened.

**Intra-saga sessions:** reference the saga's current OP at session open, optionally:

```
*[SAGA 1 OP: "We Are!" — crew-formation, local-hero rising]*
```

Or don't reference at all — ambient knowledge is fine.

## The EYECATCH beat (per `session-episode-structure.md`)

Between A-block and B-block, anime often runs an EYECATCH — a 2-5 second animated card. Usually silent or with a tiny music sting.

In prose, the eyecatch can be a MID-SESSION NATURAL BREAK with a single visual / sound reference:

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
                    *[eyecatch]*
             The Golgatha rocks at anchor.
       A gull lands on the wheel. It does not leave.
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

Optional. Once per session max, at a NATURAL mid-point.

## END CARD STINGER (rare — once every 3-5 sessions)

Canon anime often runs a BRIEF stinger AFTER the ED — a 5-second scene of someone off-screen reacting to what happened. A villain smiling. A background character noticing something. A meanwhile-cut to another arena.

In prose:

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
                    *[end card stinger]*

Far out at sea, aboard a black-hulled ship, Yann Merlis
read the Tessara report by lamplight. He set his tea down.
He did not smile. That was the interesting part.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

Once every 3-5 sessions. Huge impact when rationed.

## What NOT to do

- DON'T reference an OP at every session open if it's mid-saga. Optional unless saga-transition.
- DON'T change OP reference mid-saga. Lock it to the saga.
- DON'T over-deploy end-card stingers. Once every 3-5 sessions. More is slop.
- DON'T narrate the OP itself ("and the opening song played..."). Use the italic scene-direction bracket format.
- DON'T mix OST_LIBRARY tracks with saga-OP assignments. They're different layers — saga-OP identifies the saga; OST cues in-scene are per-moment.

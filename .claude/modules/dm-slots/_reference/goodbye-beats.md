## Goodbye Beats — The Arc-Close Departure Ceremony

**Per canon research:** every arc ends with the crew thanking and being thanked by the island. The pattern is fixed: island-people gather at the shore → emotional exchange → symbolic gesture → departure.

This is NOT optional. It's the beat that makes the arc's victory MORAL rather than just physical. You saved PEOPLE, not just won a fight.

Fires ONCE per arc, at arc close, always.

---

## The 4-beat goodbye structure (canon pattern)

### Beat 1 — The island's people gather at the shore
Canon: Alabasta's citizens line the docks; Skypiea's sky-people gathered at the exit cloud; Thriller Bark's ghost-survivors at the shore; Water 7's shipwrights; Fishman Island's evacuees; Dressrosa's freed citizens.

**Key rule:** it's the ISLAND'S people, not the crew's allies specifically. Civilians. The people who were freed or helped. They came on their own.

### Beat 2 — Emotional exchange
Crew thanks locals. Locals thank crew. Sometimes wordless. Sometimes one specific line.

Canon examples:
- Vivi / Alabasta: "EVEN IF WE CAN'T BE NAKAMA, WE'LL ALWAYS BE FRIENDS!" (The X on the arm — they show it from far away)
- Skypiea: the Golden Bell rings (Noland's name restored)
- Thriller Bark: ghost-survivors dip heads (silence)
- Water 7: Iceberg orders the shipwrights to build the Sunny
- FMI: Jinbe's departure prep (not yet joining)
- Dressrosa: "THEY'RE NOT PIRATES! THEY'RE HEROES!" (but the news says pirates)
- Zou: the Minks weep openly
- Wano: no one can say goodbye formally; the dance itself IS goodbye

### Beat 3 — A symbolic gesture
A gift, a sign, a ceremonial act.

Canon examples:
- Vivi's X-on-the-arm → the crew raises theirs → never said aloud but the pose is the covenant
- Skypiea's bell (Noland's honor RUNG across the sea)
- Thriller Bark's "Bink's Sake" sung at departure
- Dressrosa's colored dust across the harbor
- Zou's Mink warrior salute
- Wano's traditional lantern release

### Beat 4 — Departure
The Golgatha sails. The people watch from shore. The shot holds — in anime, the camera lingers on the people as the ship shrinks. In prose, the scene ends on the SHORE'S image, not the ship's.

## Goodbye emotional registers (3 types)

### Type A — Joyful goodbye
The arc resolved WELL. The crew saved who they came to save. Feast-tier nakama moment preceded this.
Canon: Alabasta, Skypiea, Dressrosa, Wano.
Format: celebration-energy, maybe a dance beat, the crew smiling as they leave.

### Type B — Grief-tinged goodbye
A cost was paid. Someone didn't make it. Someone was left behind by choice.
Canon: Water 7 (leaving the Merry at Enies Lobby — the funeral), Thriller Bark (Brook's 50-year absent crew spiritually present).
Format: QUIETER. Silence is the goodbye. Fewer words, more weight.

### Type C — Broken / unresolved goodbye
The crew leaves something unfinished. An ally stayed. A wound is still open.
Canon: Arlong Park (Nami leaves the village behind — the goodbye is HER standing on the ship), Water 7 (Usopp NOT on the ship because he quit — the grief of his absence at the departure).
Format: someone NOT there who should be. The empty space is the goodbye.

## Format — a joyful goodbye (example — future Island 5 template)

```
The sun was up. The Golgatha was packed. Mako was tying
and re-tying the same line. He couldn't sit still and
wouldn't explain why.

The island's people were at the dock. Not a formal crowd
— just whoever had chosen to come. Maybe forty of them.
The woman from the market, who had not waved before, was
waving now. The old man who had pretended not to know
anything was crying and pretending he wasn't. Three kids
at the front had made small paper flags out of rice paper.

Sumi, on the prow, saw the flags and forgot to breathe.
Her own flag on her hoodie caught the wind.

Merdo stood at the rail. He didn't say anything. He
nodded at the old man once. The old man nodded back.

Volo called down: "THE ISLAND WILL REMEMBER THIS CREW.
VOLO HAS DECIDED."

One of the kids, the smallest one, lifted their paper
flag higher.

The Golgatha eased from the dock.

On the shore, the crowd didn't leave. They watched the
ship until it was a dot. Somebody rang a bell that didn't
belong to any temple — just an ordinary harbor bell,
ringing because somebody wanted it to ring.

Merdo, at the rail, said: "Remember the bell. We're
coming back for this one."
```

Notes:
- ~200 words
- 4 beats present: gathering, exchange (the nods, Volo's declaration, the kid's flag), symbolic gesture (the paper flags + the bell), departure (the ship easing away, the crowd holding the shore)
- Specific named characters react in voice register
- Ends on Merdo's command choice (remembering the bell) — seeds future saga

## Format — a grief-tinged goodbye (example — future Saga 2-3 template)

```
They burned the [lost-thing] at dawn. They did it quietly.

Most of the crew had stopped talking hours before. Ashren
stood above, at the mast. He did not come down. Mako's
eyes never left him.

The island's people were at the shore. They had come. They
had waited. Few of them spoke. A dockworker, an old woman,
a child with a stick of charcoal that she hadn't drawn
anything with yet.

The Golgatha eased out. The crew did not wave. The shore
did not wave.

Somewhere behind the crowd, a single voice started singing.
Low, unsteady, older than any of them. The crew heard it.
Nobody said what song it was.

Merdo said, "Faster."

Cress said, "Aye, Captain."

The wind took them.
```

Notes:
- 140 words
- shorter, compressed — grief-tinged goodbyes are QUIETER
- the missing/lost thing is implied, not named
- the song from shore is unexplained — the Oda beat
- Merdo's command closes the scene without commentary

## What NOT to do

- DON'T skip the goodbye. Every arc closes with one.
- DON'T over-narrate the goodbye's emotion. Show the people, the gesture, the silence.
- DON'T force a Type A goodbye when the arc earned a Type B. Match the emotional truth.
- DON'T have the crew narrate "we did well here" or "we should come back." Show the command (a remembered detail, an unspoken decision, a name marked to memory).
- DON'T end the goodbye on the ship. End on the SHORE. The shore's image holds. The ship leaves frame.
- DON'T let the goodbye run over 300 words. Compression is emotional load.

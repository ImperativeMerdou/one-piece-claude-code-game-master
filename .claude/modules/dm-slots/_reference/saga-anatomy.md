## Saga Anatomy — The Layer Above Arcs

**The layer `arc-anatomy.md` doesn't cover.** A saga is 3-5 arcs grouped by narrative mega-shape. One Piece is organized into sagas (East Blue Saga, Alabasta Saga, Sky Island Saga, Water 7/Enies Lobby Saga, Thriller Bark Saga, Sabaody/Amazon Lily/Impel Down/Marineford "Summit War" Saga, Fishman Island Saga, Dressrosa Saga, Zou-to-Whole-Cake Saga, Wano Saga, Final Saga). Arcs are episodes; sagas are seasons.

This module gives the DM the SAGA-level compass. The current Merdo saga is **"Paradise Entry / Worst Route"** — mapped below.

---

## What defines a saga (vs. an arc)

| Level | Scope | Unit | Duration (chapters) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Beat** | a single scene | paragraph–page | — |
| **Arc** | one island's story | ~5-20 sessions | 40-100 chapters |
| **Saga** | a thematic mega-shape across 3-5 arcs | ~15-60 sessions | 150-400 chapters |
| **Era** | pre-timeskip / post-timeskip / final war | decades of play | — |

An arc resolves a SPECIFIC problem (free Alabasta, reclaim Enies Lobby, beat Kaido). A saga resolves an IDENTITY question for the crew — who are we, what do we stand for, what does the world now think of us.

**Arcs close; sagas TRANSFORM.** After the East Blue Saga, the Straw Hats were no longer five kids; they were a crew with reputations. After the Alabasta Saga, Luffy was no longer a rookie; he was a captain the Yonko noticed. Merdo's current saga (Paradise Entry / Worst Route) is the transformation from "pit fighter with a crew" into "pirate captain with an earned reputation."

## The saga's 4-part shape

Every One Piece saga has roughly this shape. Sagas can compress or expand any part, but all four show up.

### 1. **INCITING ARC** — the saga declares its question
Sets the saga's identity question. Straw Hats' first Grand Line entry arc (Whiskey Peak/Little Garden/Drum) inciting the Alabasta Saga — "what happens when we face a Warlord?" Skypiea's discovery inciting the Sky Island Saga — "what happens when we touch the divine?"

For Merdo: Ashenmaw was the inciting arc. Question raised: "are we a pit-fighter's crew, or something the world will notice?"

### 2. **RISING ARC(S)** — stakes escalate, wounds surface
Usually 1-3 arcs. The crew deepens, reveals their wounds, loses something. Drum Island was Chopper's wound. Jaya was Luffy-vs-Bellamy-and-Cricket (the saga's identity tested).

For Merdo: Goroa (Day 4-10) was the first rising arc. Kara's wound surfaced, Merdo publicly declared her protection, first Armament Haki flickered.

### 3. **PEAK ARC** — the saga's identity question is answered through a major public conflict
The arc everyone will remember. Alabasta (for Alabasta Saga). Enies Lobby (for Water 7 Saga). Marineford (for Summit War Saga). Wano (for Wano Saga).

For Merdo: the **Tessara Cup** (Day 11-13) is the current saga's peak-arc candidate if he commits. First bounty, first epithet, first public Supernova-tier defeat (or catastrophic loss into The Cage → rescue arc).

### 4. **DENOUEMENT ARC** — aftermath, world-reaction, next-saga seeding
The arc after the peak. Jaya→Skypiea has a Post-Enies "we are wanted men now" denouement. Post-Marineford is the full 2-year timeskip + training. Always shorter than the peak arc. Seeds the next saga.

For Merdo: saga TBD — probably a post-Tessara short travel arc where the News Coo delivers bounty consequences, Donquixote's response escalates, and the next saga's inciting port begins to glimmer.

## Transition beats between sagas

Every canonical One Piece saga transition has at least 2 of these:

- **News Coo drop** with bounty updates, new Warlord commissions, world-event news — the world has moved
- **Meanwhile cut** to distant canon actors (Marines, Yonko, Revolutionaries, World Government) reacting to the peak-arc consequences
- **Crew reflection scene** — someone references what they just went through, someone references what comes next; often a meal scene
- **Flag beat** — raising, cleaning, repairing, touching the Jolly Roger. The flag CARRIES the saga from one to the next. See `flag-and-covenant.md`.
- **Dream re-declaration** — at least one crew member re-states their dream, slightly refined by what they just learned. See `dream-declarations.md`.
- **New port visible on the horizon** — the next saga's inciting island appears before the current one closes

Deploy 2-3 of these per saga-transition. Fires at the transition beat, not distributed.

## How the DM uses this module

- **At session start:** know which saga we're in + which arc within the saga + shape-position of the arc (inciting/rising/peak/denouement)
- **At session end:** check — did THIS session advance the saga's identity question? Not every session does, but if 3 sessions pass with zero saga-movement, push it
- **At arc transition:** check the transition beats list. Fire 2-3
- **At saga transition:** major event — the News Coo drop, the meanwhile cut to canon actors, the crew reflection feast, the flag moment, the dream re-declaration, the next saga's inciting island appearing

## Self-audit

Before closing an arc, silently check:

1. ☐ Did the arc's events move the saga's identity question?
2. ☐ Did at least one crew member change (wound surfaced, dream refined, relationship shifted)?
3. ☐ Did the world REACT (news, bounty, meanwhile, faction awareness)?
4. ☐ If closing a saga: did the peak-arc fire all four fight-law aftermath beats (physical collapse + emotional release + world change + bounty/reputation)?

If any fail on a saga-closing arc, the saga doesn't close. Extend.

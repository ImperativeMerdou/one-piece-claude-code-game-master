## Crew Anime Archetypes — Per-Character Mapping

**Adapted from Nemo's Anime Archetypes Library + Engine.** Each crew member is mapped to 1-2 anime archetypes that anchor their behavior patterns — speech, reactions, emotional defaults, conflict-resolution style. These aren't cages — they're STARTING POSTURES the DM returns to when uncertain.

Based on Nemo's three-section taxonomy: Dere types (emotional register), Role/Function (ensemble slot), Genre-linked (tonal archetype).

## HOW TO USE THE ARCHETYPES

**As starting postures, not cages.** Characters BREAK their archetypes at emotional peaks. Zoro smiles. Robin rages. Luffy cries. That's the craft. An archetype establishes the floor so the break-from-archetype MATTERS.

**When writing a scene:**
1. Check who's present
2. Consider their archetype-default reaction
3. Decide: do they stay in archetype (most scenes) OR break from it (big emotional beat)?

**When NPC-wrangling:**
Each crew member's archetype gives the DM a dependable default. When the DM doesn't know what Ashren does in a scene — Kuudere + Lancer + Stoic Swordsman says: STILL, WATCH, DECIDE INTERNALLY. When Volo isn't sure how he reacts — Himedere + Theatrical Warrior says: DEMAND WITNESS LOUDLY FOR SOMETHING SINCERE.

**When building new NPCs (per-arc antagonists, civilians, one-shots):**
Pick 1-2 archetypes for them BEFORE writing dialogue. The voice falls out of the archetypes. Pair with `villain-kit.md` (archetype + sanity + obsession) for arc villains specifically.

## EXAMPLES OF STACKING (for future NPCs)

- **A Marine officer with a hidden Revolutionary sympathy:** Kuudere + Strategist + Mayadere-potential
- **A ZOAN-fruit user who thinks they're a monster:** Shundere + Powerhouse + Instrument of Ruin (false)
- **A rival rookie captain who's obviously jealous of Merdo:** Tsundere + Shonen Rival + Kamidere-lite
- **A Celestial Dragon encountered at a port:** Kamidere + Parasite + pure villain
- **A one-shot grandmother who carries a secret:** Dandere + Senpai/Mentor (hidden)

The archetype stack TELLS you how they speak, what they defend, what they fear. Write from that.

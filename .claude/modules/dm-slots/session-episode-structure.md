# Session Episode Structure — Every Session is an Anime Episode

**The single most-impactful structural rule for making this feel like an anime.** Every session has the SHAPE of a One Piece episode. Title card → optional cold open → A-block → eyecatch break → B-block → cliffhanger → NEXT TIME ON preview.

This is procedural, not optional. Every session opens with a title and ends with a preview.

**Division of labor (to avoid duplication):**
- **This module** OWNS the SHAPE of a session (the structural slots above). Rules for WHEN each slot fires and WHAT goes in each live here.
- **`anime-craft.md`** OWNS the Toei craft LAYER on top of that shape — theme-song callbacks, sakuga moments, anime-pacing tricks, specific animation-feel beats. Consult it for the HOW of making each slot feel animated.
- **`manga-craft.md`** OWNS the Oda / manga-chapter layer — chapter hooks, callback density, panel-grammar rhythm, SBS-style trivia.

All three compose: this module's shape + anime-craft's anime flesh + manga-craft's manga bones = the episode as experienced.

## SESSION OPEN — three components

### 1. The "PREVIOUSLY ON..." recap (optional, brief)

If the previous session ended on a cliffhanger or unresolved beat, open with a short anime-style recap. 2-4 sentences. Quick, rhythmic, ends on the most intense beat from last session.

```
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  PREVIOUSLY ON THE MERDO PIRATES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The Golgatha docked at Tessara. Three Marine warships in
  observation. A black-hulled ship with a man watching Ashren.
  Two strangers on the dock. Two crew members ready to leave.
  A fruit basket on the deck addressed to Captain Merdo.
  Sundown closes the Tournament sign-up window.

  And now — Day 11, morning.
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

When NOT to recap: when the player just played a session and immediately starts another. Don't restate what they just lived.

### 2. The EPISODE TITLE CARD (mandatory)

Every session has a TITLE. Drawn from the session's emerging theme or the most-anticipated beat. Format like a One Piece anime title card: **"[hook phrase]! [theme statement]!"**

Canon examples for reference:
- "OPENING THE GATE! FOR ROBIN!"
- "ZORO'S NEW SWORD! WANO'S GREATEST SWORDSMAN!"
- "THE TIME IS NOW! THE NEW WORLD AWAITS!"
- "NOTHING HAPPENED! ZORO'S DECLARATION!"
- "BAJRANG GUN! THE STRONGEST FIST OF GEAR FIFTH!"

Format:
```
╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                           ║
║              ⛵ EPISODE 3 — DAY 11                        ║
║                                                           ║
║       "TWO ON THE DOCK! THE CAPTAIN'S CHOICE!"            ║
║                                                           ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

The title is generated FROM the session's anticipated focus. If the session is about Tessara R1, the title might be **"FIRST BELL! EZRAD'S LAST BOTTLE!"** or **"MERDO ENTERS THE PIT!"**

The DM picks ONE clear hook. The title appears ONCE at session open and is referenced ONCE in the Next Time preview.

### 3. The COLD OPEN (optional, when fitting)

A short scene before the title card. ~5-10 lines. Often a hook — a glimpse of something that will pay off later in the session.

Examples of cold-open shapes:
- A meanwhile-cut to a villain (Doflamingo reading Yann's dispatch with a smile)
- A sensory anchor (the harbor pre-dawn, before Merdo wakes)
- A flashback fragment (Merdo's pit-day single image)
- A NPC moment (Brenne writing in the Ship Log at first light)

```
The Den Den Mushi rang twice in Yann Merlis's cabin. He
opened it without standing.

"My compliments," he said. "Tell the master Tessara is
proceeding well. The horned one will be in the bracket
by tomorrow noon. The girl is unattended in the daylight."

He set the snail down. The wax seal on his coffee cup
glinted gold.

═══ EPISODE 3 — "TWO ON THE DOCK! THE CAPTAIN'S CHOICE!" ═══
```

Cold open is optional. Title card is not.

## A-BLOCK / B-BLOCK / EYECATCH

In the anime, episodes have an A-block (first 10 minutes) → eyecatch (commercial break visual stinger) → B-block (last 10 minutes) → ending theme.

In a TTRPG session, the structure is fluid — but at a NATURAL midpoint pause (player takes a break, scene transitions), the DM can drop an EYECATCH.

### Eyecatch format

```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                      ⚓ ⚔ ⚓
            (the Merdo Pirates jolly roger)
                      ⚓ ⚔ ⚓
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

Or more elaborate, with a one-line character beat:

```
─────────────── EYECATCH ───────────────
  Sumi sits on the dock crate, drawing
  Merdo from memory. The drawing has
  too many horns.
─────────────────────────────────────────
```

Optional. Doesn't need to fire every session. Use sparingly, at natural mid-session beats.

## DETECTING SESSION END (how the DM knows)

A "session" = one play-sitting. The user starts with `/dm-continue` and stops when they disengage. The DM should fire the CLIFFHANGER + NEXT TIME ON when one of these triggers:

1. **Explicit save:** user types `/dm save` or `/dm end`
2. **Verbal wrap:** user says "let's end here" / "wrap" / "I gotta go" / "save here" / "stopping for the night"
3. **Natural stopping action in narrative:** user describes Merdo going to sleep, the crew docking for the night, an arc closing
4. **Player-offered cliffhanger:** if the DM hits a natural good break-point, the DM can OFFER: *"This is a strong stopping point — want to end on a cliffhanger here? Or push forward?"*

If none of these trigger, the session is still ongoing — keep playing without forcing a wrap.

When ONE triggers: deliver the **CLIFFHANGER → NEXT TIME ON → optional END CARD STINGER** sequence below, then save state and confirm.

**Never force a wrap mid-action.** If a fight is in progress and the user says "save here," finish the current beat / paragraph THEN cliffhanger.

## CLIFFHANGER ENDING — mandatory at session end

Every session ends on a CLIFFHANGER. Never on a "and then they went to sleep." Always on:

### Five cliffhanger shapes (pick one)

1. **Arrival** — a silhouette in the doorway. A figure appearing on the wharf. A News Coo landing on the railing. Someone the player can't yet identify.

2. **Declaration** — a character says something that demands response. "Captain. We need to talk." "I know what you are." "He's coming."

3. **Mid-action cut** — the punch is thrown but the impact is next session. The bell rings. Sword comes up. CUT.

4. **Revelation** — a piece of intel surfaces in the last sentence. The wax seal. The name on the file. The face Merdo hasn't seen since he was fifteen.

5. **Question** — the scene ends on a question with no answer. Yann's last line. Hudore's invitation. A child's unfinished sentence.

End-of-session prose example:
```
Merdo's foot was on the colosseum gangway when the steward
looked up from her ledger.

"Captain. There's a name been added to your bracket since
this morning."

She turned the ledger. Merdo's eyes found the entry.

The name was his own father's.
```

(End scene. Cut. Next time on.)

## "NEXT TIME ON THE MERDO PIRATES!" — mandatory

Every session ends with a SHORT preview. 4-7 lines. Anime-style: 2-3 dramatic teaser lines + one closing hype line.

Format:
```
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
              ⚓ NEXT TIME ON THE MERDO PIRATES ⚓
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The first bell rings. The crowd inhales.
  Ezrad raises the bottle. Merdo plants his foot.

  And in a north VIP box, a man with a gold ring
  whispers something the steward of the Tessara Cup
  has never been allowed to hear.

  EPISODE 4: "FIRST BLOOD! THE LAST BOTTLE TOAST!"

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

The preview:
- Teases 2-3 specific beats from the next session
- Names ONE character or object
- Ends with the next episode title
- Should make the user WANT to /dm-continue immediately

When the DM doesn't know what's next session yet, write a TEASE without committing — focus on what the player ALREADY KNOWS is coming and let the language be evocative-but-vague.

## END-CARD STINGER (optional, rare)

After the NEXT TIME ON, occasionally drop a single short stinger — like a post-credits scene. ~2-4 lines. Hints at a future beat, not the next session.

```
─── END CARD ───
  Far from Tessara, in a small rented room above a tea
  shop, a man named Norbert Hoyt's older brother reads
  his bounty hunter's report.

  He smiles for the first time in two years.
─────────────────
```

Use sparingly — once every 3-5 sessions. The rarity is what makes it land.

## PUTTING IT ALL TOGETHER — typical session shape

```
[PREVIOUSLY ON] (if continuing from cliffhanger)
[COLD OPEN] (optional — meanwhile / sensory / flashback)
[EPISODE TITLE CARD]
[HUD: scene state, location, time, bounty, Haki]
[A-BLOCK SCENES — opening through midpoint]
[EYECATCH] (optional — natural break)
[B-BLOCK SCENES — second half through climax]
[CLIFFHANGER]
[NEXT TIME ON PREVIEW]
[END CARD STINGER] (optional, rare)
```

## WHY THIS MATTERS

The anime's structural rhythm is a HUGE part of why watching feels different from reading a novel. Episode boundaries shape the experience. Cliffhangers create anticipation. Title cards focus expectations. NEXT TIME ON makes the wait worth it.

When every session HAS this shape, the user is not "playing a TTRPG" — they're "watching an episode." The shape itself does enormous work.

This is procedural. Bake it in.

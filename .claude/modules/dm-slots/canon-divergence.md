## Canon Divergence — The Guided Sandbox

**Per user directive:** canon stays MOSTLY the same but with Merdo IN IT. The player has FULL AGENCY to redirect canon through Merdo's deeds. Butterfly effects are REAL in this campaign. The Straw Hats can be fought. Canon characters can be allied, betrayed, saved, killed. The world is a guided sandbox.

This module governs HOW the DM handles divergences. It's the philosophical shift from `canon-fidelity-audit.md`'s default (don't contradict canon) to a new framework: canon is the BASELINE, player actions redirect it, divergences are TRACKED.

---

## The two-layer canon model

### Layer 1 — Canon baseline (runs by default)
Unless Merdo's actions directly alter them, canon events happen on canon timeline:
- Ace is captured and dies at Marineford
- Whitebeard dies at Marineford
- The Straw Hats scatter at Sabaody via Kuma
- Luffy reaches Impel Down, breaks out, fights at Marineford, retreats, 2-year timeskip, Fishman Island, Punk Hazard, Dressrosa, Zou, WCI, Wano, Elbaf
- Canon characters act per canon personalities and motivations
- Yonko dynamics, Marine Admiral hierarchy, Warlord rotations, World Government moves — all canon

**This is the default timeline the DM narrates.** Meanwhile-cuts (per `meanwhile.md`) surface canon events as off-screen world-texture.

### Layer 2 — Merdo's divergence (rewrites parts)
When Merdo acts, the world responds. Anything Merdo does PUBLICLY, PERSONALLY, or at SCALE can alter canon events. Examples:

- **Saving a canonically doomed character** — Merdo rescues Ace at Banaro Island / Marineford approach → Ace survives → canon post-Marineford dramatically altered
- **Allying with canon Yonko** — Merdo joins Shanks' fleet → Marineford goes differently, Shanks arrives with new forces
- **Fighting the Straw Hats** — Merdo battles Luffy at Sabaody → the two rookie crews have a RIVALRY line that reshapes New World politics
- **Killing a canon actor** — Merdo defeats Big Mom in the New World → her crew scatters, Pudding's arc changes, Sanji's Germa escape goes differently
- **Changing Marine politics** — Merdo exposes a Cipher Pol operation publicly → Rob Lucci's trajectory alters
- **Intervening in Wano, Elbaf, or any canon arc** — presence directly shifts outcome

**These divergences are REAL.** Once they fire, the DM updates the world. Canon from that point FOLLOWS the new trajectory.

## The 3 rules of divergence

### Rule 1 — Scale still applies
Merdo at Saga 1 cannot fight Luffy at post-Marineford tier. Merdo at Saga 3 Sabaody-convergence can brush Luffy at roughly-equivalent rookie power. Merdo at Saga 10+ can stand with Yonko Luffy as an equal.

The SCALE LADDER from `canon-bounty-tiers.md` + `one_piece_power_law.md` is still enforced. If the player tries to fight a canon character above Merdo's current tier, the DM narrates the consequences honestly — Merdo loses, gets captured, nearly dies, or is saved by the NPC's disinterest. No power-dream contraventions.

### Rule 2 — Divergences cost
Every divergence has CONSEQUENCES. Saving Ace means BLACKBEARD doesn't become a Yonko the canon way. Alluing with Shanks means Kaido's hostility toward Merdo escalates (Shanks and Kaido have canonical rivalry). Fighting the Straw Hats means the alliance potential for Sabaody-convergence shifts — maybe Luffy becomes a RIVAL, not a peer.

The DM tracks consequences explicitly. See SAGA_MAP divergence log.

### Rule 3 — Divergences fire FROM PLAYER CHOICES, not DM decisions
The DM does not unilaterally alter canon. Canon is the default. The player's Merdo makes a choice that cascades. The DM then HONORS the cascade.

Example: the DM doesn't have Kaido randomly die unless Merdo actually kills him in play. The DM doesn't decide Shanks attends Marineford earlier unless Merdo's Melaina-mother arc directly causes it.

**The player's agency is the divergence engine.** The DM implements.

## When divergences fire

### Automatic / ambient
- Merdo's bounty poster spreads. Canon characters NOTICE. Every arc where Merdo does something public, minor canon rippling is implied (meanwhile cuts per `meanwhile.md`).
- This is LOW-STAKES divergence — canon continues but the texture shifts.

### Player-driven major
- Merdo actively chooses a path that contradicts canon (attacks Ace's escort at Banaro, sides with a Warlord, breaks Impel Down before Luffy does, etc.).
- This is HIGH-STAKES divergence — canon events change downstream.

### Saga-transition
- End of each saga, the DM checks: what has Merdo irrevocably altered? Log it.
- Canon events beyond this point use the ALTERED timeline, not the original.

## The Straw Hat Question

Canon's protagonists. Merdo's potential peers / rivals / allies / enemies.

### Scenarios the DM should be ready for:
- **Merdo ignores Luffy** (Sabaody-convergence default): they nod, they part, canon proceeds
- **Merdo allies with Luffy** (Sabaody alliance): they share a fight, Marineford approach MAY include Merdo, canon bends
- **Merdo rivals Luffy openly**: public clash, Merdo's reputation shifts ("the Straw Hats' enemy"), Marine intelligence reclassifies both crews
- **Merdo befriends specific Straw Hats** (e.g., Zoro respects Merdo → Ashren / Zoro friendship, Chopper treats Vesper, etc.) → LOW divergence but character-texture added
- **Merdo DEFEATS Luffy in a one-sided fight** (saga-late): Luffy takes a loss he didn't take in canon → cascading effects on his arc
- **Merdo LOSES to Luffy in a one-sided fight** (saga-early): Merdo's arc is reshaped around recovery

Each scenario has downstream consequences. The DM plays them out honestly.

### Canon personalities are INVIOLABLE (mostly)
Luffy is still Luffy. Zoro is still Zoro. They don't act out of character because Merdo is present. If Merdo attacks Zeff's Baratie, Luffy will be furious. If Merdo saves Ace, Ace will thank him the way Ace would. The PEOPLE stay canon. Their OUTCOMES can diverge.

## Divergence format in prose

When a divergence fires, narrate it with FULL canonical weight. Example:

```
The Marineford square held its breath. Akainu's fist
was cocked back. Ace was on the ground, bleeding,
looking up at his brother.

And then Merdo arrived.

He didn't come from the sea. He didn't come from
the sky. He came FROM the execution platform — he
had climbed it while the battle raged, gauntlet
already black, and now he was between Akainu and
Ace with his hand up.

"Wrong day, Admiral."

Luffy, from ten meters away, said Merdo's name for
the first time aloud.

Somewhere in the back of the square, Sengoku stood
up. Shanks, arriving, paused at the horizon.

The world registered what had just happened.

**[DIVERGENCE LOGGED: Marineford / Ace survives via Merdo intervention / Akainu humiliated / Blackbeard's power-up does not happen canonically / Shanks's arrival tempered / saga-weight cascade begins]**
```

Notes:
- Full saga-weight prose register (long, dense, multiple named reactions)
- The divergence moment is SHOWN, not explained
- The log bracket at the end is the DM-facing note — track it in SAGA_MAP

## The DM's internal audit for every scene

Per Oda gut-check + canon-fidelity (now updated):

1. Is this a canon event that happens by default? → narrate it
2. Is Merdo present? → does his presence change anything?
3. Is Merdo acting? → does his action shift the canon trajectory?
4. If yes → log the divergence, ripple the consequences

Canon fidelity becomes: **"honor canon unless the player's agency has altered it, and honor the altered state with the same weight."**

## What NOT to do

- DON'T let the DM unilaterally diverge canon. Wait for the player's Merdo to drive.
- DON'T break scale. Merdo can't kill Kaido in Saga 2. Losses teach; they don't cheat.
- DON'T narrate canon character out-of-character. Luffy is still Luffy, even when diverging.
- DON'T forget consequences. Every divergence ripples. Track them.
- DON'T over-diverge. Most canon events should continue as expected — divergences are SPECIAL, not constant.
- DON'T treat canon as sacred. Per user directive: "canon stays mostly the same but with us in it. If I want to fight the straw hats or anything else like that it's possible." Honor the player's sandbox freedom.

## Villain Construction Kit — Archetype + Sanity + Obsession

**Adapted from KittyLotus's Villain System.** Build any villain in three dials. Replaces ad-hoc villain construction with a reproducible engine. Especially useful for Worst Generation arcs where the player needs MEMORABLE OPPONENTS.

---

## Dial 1 — Archetype (the operating mode)

Pick ONE primary archetype. Each has an escalation curve, a language register, a typical move-set, and a defeat-shape.

### 🎭 CHARMER
Wins through influence. Charisma weaponized. Gets people to ENJOY being controlled.
- **Examples:** Doflamingo. Big Mom. Sugar (sort of).
- **Move set:** flattery, smiles, gifts, public theater, named endearments, public humiliations dressed as mercy
- **Defeat:** the audience sees through the smile. Crowd turns. The CHARM stops working.
- **Voice:** warm, theatrical, casually cruel
- **Match for One Piece:** Donquixote family obviously. Yann Merlis at lower tier.

### ⚔️ ENFORCER
Wins through raw power and presence. Violence as conversation.
- **Examples:** Kaido. Akainu. Don Krieg.
- **Move set:** declarations of strength, public violence, demanding worthy opponents, bored cruelty
- **Defeat:** they meet someone who refuses to fear them. The presence becomes a CAGE for them.
- **Voice:** blunt, weighty, monumental
- **Match for One Piece:** Yonko. Admiral candidates. Tournament champions.

### 🎯 HUNTER
Wins through pursuit. Knows your patterns. Will not stop.
- **Examples:** Smoker. Garp toward Luffy. Mihawk toward Zoro.
- **Move set:** patient pursuit, perfect intel, anticipation, surprise arrivals at exact moments
- **Defeat:** the prey becomes something the hunter can't catch — escapes the framework that made them prey.
- **Voice:** calm, professional, occasionally personal
- **Match for One Piece:** Marine officers in pursuit, debt collectors, hired bounty hunters.

### 🧠 STRATEGIST
Wins through plans. Three steps ahead. Treats fights as chess.
- **Examples:** Crocodile. Doflamingo (overlap with Charmer). Spandam (failed strategist).
- **Move set:** layered plots, contingencies, false retreats, sacrificial pieces
- **Defeat:** the plan accounts for everything except an EMOTIONAL VARIABLE. Luffy doesn't follow chess rules.
- **Voice:** clipped, certain, rarely surprised
- **Match for One Piece:** Crocodile-tier arc bosses. Hudore in cunning mode.

### 🔥 ZEALOT
Wins through belief. Their ideology is their weapon. Cannot be reasoned with.
- **Examples:** Akainu (Absolute Justice). Hody Jones. Black Beard (proto). Parseval.
- **Move set:** ideological monologues, impossible standards, justified atrocities
- **Defeat:** the BELIEF itself is dismantled. Their worldview cracks. They keep fighting but the fire is gone.
- **Voice:** certain, righteous, occasionally tender (toward whatever they're protecting)
- **Match for One Piece:** Marine fanatics, religious antagonists, ideology-arc villains.

### 🦠 PARASITE
Wins through extraction. Uses others as resources. Drains.
- **Examples:** Sir Crocodile (overlap with Strategist), Spandam (failed parasite), the Celestial Dragons.
- **Move set:** indirect harm, leveraging others, never their own hands dirty, profitable cruelty
- **Defeat:** the resource refuses. The parasite has no plan B because they never invested in one.
- **Voice:** entitled, dismissive, occasionally panicked when the supply chain breaks
- **Match for One Piece:** Celestial Dragons. Underworld brokers. Yann Merlis (overlap with Charmer).

### 🃏 TRICKSTER
Wins through chaos. Reads situations and BREAKS them. Loves the show.
- **Examples:** Buggy (proto). Bege (sort of). Blackbeard (mature form).
- **Move set:** unexpected pivots, broken rules, theatrical reveals, betraying allies
- **Defeat:** caught in their own loop — chaos eats itself.
- **Voice:** quick, irreverent, occasionally weary
- **Match for One Piece:** comedic-but-serious antagonists, surprise pirate captains, late-arc reveals.

### 👻 HAUNT
Wins through fear and unfinished business. Won't go away. Returns.
- **Examples:** Gecko Moria. The Marineford ghosts. Trauma-driven ex-allies.
- **Move set:** echoes, returns, callbacks to past wounds, unwanted gifts
- **Defeat:** the protagonist FACES whatever the haunt represents. The ghost is laid to rest.
- **Voice:** quiet, persistent, occasionally rueful
- **Match for One Piece:** characters from Merdo's pit days. The village that betrayed him. Old enemies returning.

### 🌑 ELDRITCH
Wins through scope. The villain is BIGGER than the protagonist's framework. Cannot be understood.
- **Examples:** Im. The Void Century. The Celestial Dragons as a system.
- **Move set:** indirect manifestations, world-shape effects, no direct confrontation possible
- **Defeat:** not in this saga. Eldritch villains are SCALE PROBLEMS. The protagonist learns to fight them later.
- **Voice:** rare, oblique, never plain
- **Match for One Piece:** Im, Imu, the World Government as an entity, the Five Elders.

## Dial 2 — Sanity Stage (their composure)

Where they are RIGHT NOW on the breaking curve.

### 🟢 CALCULATED
Composed. In control. Speaking carefully. Plans intact.
- **Surfaces as:** measured speech, polite cruelty, controlled violence
- **Best for:** introduction arcs, standoff scenes, the villain in their power

### 🟡 FRACTURED
Cracking. Composure slipping in spots. Plans starting to fray.
- **Surfaces as:** voice raising at unexpected moments, repeating themselves, asking the same question twice, small physical tells (a hand shaking, a sentence cut off)
- **Best for:** mid-arc, when the protagonist's pressure is working

### 🔴 UNHINGED
Broken. Composure gone. Operating on raw drive.
- **Surfaces as:** screaming, indiscriminate violence, ideology overflowing, talking to themselves, betraying allies in real-time
- **Best for:** climax beats, the moment before defeat

Most villains DRIFT through these stages over an arc. Hudore CALCULATED → FRACTURED at rank reveal → still CALCULATED at fight end (he doesn't break). Doflamingo CALCULATED for years → FRACTURED in Dressrosa as Luffy keeps winning → never quite UNHINGED.

## Dial 3 — Obsession Multiplier (intensity toward the protagonist)

How focused are they on Merdo specifically?

| Multiplier | Behavior |
|---|---|
| 0 — INDIFFERENT | Doesn't know Merdo exists or doesn't care. Background threat. |
| 1 — NOTED | Aware of Merdo as a name on a list. Will act if convenient. |
| 2 — INTERESTED | Has filed Merdo under "future problem." Will allocate resources. |
| 3 — TARGETED | Merdo is now an active goal. Plans include him by name. |
| 4 — OBSESSED | Cannot stop thinking about Merdo. Personal. Will make mistakes for the chance. |
| 5 — DEFINED BY | Their identity is tied to defeating/possessing/destroying Merdo. They have nothing else. |

Most villains start at 0-1. Across an arc, they climb. Hudore at Tessara: 1 (NOTED). After the final: 3 (TARGETED). If Merdo breaks Hudore's daughter-search hope: 5 (DEFINED BY).

## Building a villain — the formula

```
[Archetype] + [Current Sanity Stage] + [Obsession Toward Merdo]
```

Examples from the current campaign:

- **Yann Merlis:** CHARMER + CALCULATED + 2 (INTERESTED). Acquisition target list, Merdo is on it, no personal heat yet.
- **Hudore:** STRATEGIST/HUNTER hybrid + CALCULATED + 1 (NOTED). Will climb to 3 over the arc.
- **Parseval:** ZEALOT + FRACTURED + 0 (INDIFFERENT — he doesn't care WHO the pirate is, he kills them all). Could climb to 4 if Merdo shows him mercy.
- **Doflamingo (offscreen):** CHARMER + CALCULATED + 0 (doesn't know Merdo yet). Will climb to 2-3 once Yann reports back.

When designing a NEW villain mid-campaign, pick the three dials BEFORE writing dialogue. The voice falls out of the dials.

---

## Anti-villain (variant)

Some characters are villain-CODED but morally complex. The dials still work.

- Commodore Hudore is a "villain" but his obsession is his daughter, not malice. ENFORCER + CALCULATED + 1 (work) hides STRATEGIST + FRACTURED + 5 (love).
- Parseval is a "villain" but his obsession is his sister's killer. ZEALOT + FRACTURED + 5 (toward an idea, not a person).

These villains can pivot. The dials reflect their CURRENT state, not their permanent nature.

## MANIPULATION / PSYCHOLOGICAL WARFARE

**Adapted from Nemo Engine's Manipulation scroll.** Not every villain fights with fists or fruit. The most unsettling antagonists work through LANGUAGE — gaslighting, slow-boil pressure, verbal dismantling, carefully placed questions, weaponized intimacy. A CHARMER with a manipulation kit is more dangerous than an ENFORCER with a heavier weapon.

This is the VERBAL equivalent of a named-attack cascade. Deploy sparingly. It lands harder rationed.

### The 8 manipulation techniques

**1. GASLIGHTING** — reframing the other person's reality. "That's not what happened." / "You didn't see what you think you saw." / "You're misremembering." Deployed by CHARMERS and STRATEGISTS. Targets uncertainty.

**2. FALSE INTIMACY** — offering closeness the target hasn't earned, to destabilize. "I know you better than you know yourself." / "We're the same, you and I." / "Your crew doesn't see you the way I do." Deployed by CHARMERS. Works best on lonely targets (Ashren's Lunarian isolation; Sable's CP-0 absence of belonging — both are manipulation targets for the right opponent).

**3. SLOW-BOIL PRESSURE** — escalating demands in increments too small to refuse individually. Start with a tiny concession. Grow it every scene. By the time the target realizes they're in a trap, they've already given too much away. Deployed by PARASITES and STRATEGISTS.

**4. VERBAL DISMANTLING** — asking questions the target cannot answer without exposing themselves. Not insults — precise surgery. "Why did you really leave?" / "Who were you before the crew found you?" / "What did you do in the pit that you won't say?" Deployed by HUNTERS (who have the intel) and STRATEGISTS (who know which question opens the crack).

**5. WEAPONIZED VULNERABILITY** — the villain showing a false vulnerability to the target, creating obligation. A CHARMER reveals a "wound" that's really a lever. The target feels they owe this opponent something. Classic Doflamingo move.

**6. CO-OPTION** — offering the target what they already want, with terms. "I can find your mother." / "I can get you off Marine records." / "I can get your crew the berries you need — all you have to do is…" Most dangerous version: when the villain is TELLING THE TRUTH. They can deliver. The price is the trap.

**7. INFORMATION ASYMMETRY** — showing the target that the villain KNOWS THINGS they shouldn't. Names no one told them. Events no one saw. This is the CP-0 / Cipher Pol playbook. Used to establish that the target has no secrets, no privacy, no safety.

**8. PUBLIC ISOLATION** — turning the target's crew against them in small ways. A whispered word to one. A planted piece of evidence for another. By the time the target returns to his crew, they're looking at him slightly differently. Doflamingo vs. Dressrosa is the canon template.

### How this operates in prose

- **Keep it SHORT.** Manipulation scenes work because the villain says LESS than the target. Not speeches. Precise short questions.
- **Let the target RESPOND BADLY.** The craft is in how Merdo responds — losing his cool, getting quiet, lashing out, staying still too long. All are wins for the villain.
- **Track the damage.** Manipulation scenes don't leave bruises but they DO leave receipts. A crew member who gets told something about Merdo by a villain will carry that even if they don't believe it. Write the residue.
- **Give the villain ONE specific piece of accurate intel** every manipulation scene. Even when they're lying, one thing is true. That's what makes the lie work.

### Per-villain manipulation profiles

- **Yann Merlis (CHARMER, Donquixote buyer):** FALSE INTIMACY + CO-OPTION + INFORMATION ASYMMETRY. His fruit basket IS a manipulation. He offers "friendship" and Devil Fruit intel he happens to have. He knows Ashren is Lunarian before being told. He knows Kara's name. He offers Merdo a path. The path has teeth.
- **Hudore (STRATEGIST + HUNTER hybrid):** VERBAL DISMANTLING + SLOW-BOIL during pre-fight interviews. He asks Merdo questions Merdo didn't expect anyone to know. His manipulation feeds into fight preparation — he's already BUILT the tactical read before the first bell.
- **Parseval (ZEALOT):** doesn't manipulate, ironically. Zealots are too sincere. His unsettling quality is CERTAINTY, not manipulation.
- **Doflamingo (offscreen, saga-future):** all 8. The template. Will be a terminal arc encounter.
- **Cipher Pol / Lucci (saga-future):** all 8, cold register. Terminal.

### Counter-moves (for Merdo / crew to resist)

Manipulation lands because the target doesn't know it's happening. Surfacing it in prose is a reader-level revelation, not a character-level one. But characters CAN resist:

- **Ashren's silence** — manipulators need responses to work. Ashren's zero-verbal return is a hard counter.
- **Volo's theatrical overresponse** — manipulators need containers for the manipulation to grow in. Volo blowing any subtle move into a full public witness-demand blows the subtle manipulation apart.
- **Sable's operational framing** — she recognizes CP-0 manipulation patterns because she was trained to use them. She can flag them internally. She doesn't always say.
- **Merdo's directness** — asking the real question can break a manipulation cycle. "What do you actually want from me?" mid-villain-speech is a sword-cut.

### Cooldowns

- **Max 1 full manipulation scene per session.** More than that exhausts the register.
- **Max 1 manipulation scene per major antagonist per arc.** Hudore's verbal scene happens once; the fight is separate.
- **Not applicable in comedy beats.** Manipulation is a serious register. It doesn't work in Salt War scenes.

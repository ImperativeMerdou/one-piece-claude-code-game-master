## Momentum Governor — Anti-Stall Pressure Injection

**Adapted from KittyLotus's Momentum Governor.** Detects when a scene is stalling and injects pressure from a 7-item menu. The user should NEVER be stuck in a planning loop or three-turn dialogue volley with nothing changing.

---

## Detection — when is the scene STALLED?

A scene is stalled if ANY of these are true after a player turn:
1. The player and DM have exchanged 3+ turns without a state change (location, NPC presence, threat level, knowledge, relationship).
2. The player is planning out loud without executing — and the DM is letting them.
3. Dialogue volleys are exchanging information without anyone committing to a choice.
4. The player asks a question, the DM answers, the player asks another question, the DM answers — repeat.
5. Nothing in the scene has CHANGED in 2+ paragraphs.

When ANY trigger fires, the DM ROLLS A d7 and injects pressure from the menu below.

---

## The 7-Item Pressure Menu

### 1 — INTERRUPT
Someone (NPC, environment, animal) interrupts. The conversation cannot continue as it was.
- Mako stumbles up with bad news.
- A News Coo lands on the railing with breaking headlines.
- A drunk in the bar overturns a chair.
- A gull screams.

### 2 — TIMER
A clock the player wasn't watching becomes visible.
- The Tessara Cup sign-up steward starts packing up the gate.
- The black ship's morning bell rings (Yann's daily Den Den Mushi check-in).
- The tide turns and the Golgatha shifts at the dock — they need to retie or move.
- Hudore's Marine escort starts a slow patrol pass.

### 3 — CONSEQUENCE
Something the player did 1-3 sessions ago lands NOW.
- A Marine spotted Ashren on Day 9 — the report just made it to a desk that mattered.
- The merchant Merdo shoved at Goroa filed a complaint that just reached Tessara.
- A crew member's untreated wound from last fight goes septic — Vesper detonates.

### 4 — NPC INITIATIVE
An NPC stops waiting and ACTS without invitation.
- Sumi gets impatient and signs Merdo up at the colosseum gate without asking.
- Yann sends a second gift basket — this time addressed to Kara.
- Volo announces his bracket entry and demands witness from the entire crew RIGHT NOW.
- Sable swallowed something. The crew sees the cheek lump shift. She's grinning.

### 5 — REVEAL
A piece of intel the player didn't know surfaces. Pull from `chekhov-gun-rack.md` if possible — fire an aged plant.
- The bookmaker's chalkboard updates: Hudore's true rank is whispered.
- A wanted poster with the gold signet ring symbol is glimpsed at a stall.
- A fragment of conversation overheard in the harbor reveals Yann's schedule.

### 6 — ENVIRONMENT SHIFT
The world changes physically.
- A storm front rolls in. Visible. The harbor reacts.
- A Marine warship signals — flag-code, slow but ominous.
- A crowd starts gathering for some reason at the colosseum gate.
- The wind changes.

### 7 — THREAT MOVE
A known antagonist takes a visible action.
- The black ship raises a single signal lantern. Nobody knows what for.
- Two of Yann's Marine "escorts" walk past the Golgatha, casual, slow.
- A Marine corvette weighs anchor and changes position.
- The Donquixote agent leaves the colosseum VIP box. Doesn't return.

## Density rule (always-on, even without stall)

Per KittyLotus's principle: **Every paragraph in the response must contain at least ONE of:**
- A new event (someone does something)
- A new revelation (info emerges)
- A new shift (mood, position, knowledge changes)

If a paragraph has none of these, REWRITE it. No filler-prose paragraphs.

---

## Anti-parroting rule

The DM does NOT paraphrase what the player just did before responding. Move forward. The player knows what they did.

WRONG:
> "You step into the colosseum and look around at the bookmaker's booths. The crowd is already gathering. As you take in the scene..."

RIGHT (move forward):
> "Partho the bookie spots Merdo before Merdo spots him. He waves a chalk hand. 'OI HORNED ONE. Two-to-one against you in round one. Want a piece?'"

---

## Anti-recap rule

The DM does NOT recap what just happened in the previous response. The player remembers. The world moves.

If the user asks "what just happened?" — THEN recap. Otherwise: forward.

---

## When the user IS asking for breath

Sometimes the player wants a quiet moment. Recognize:
- Player describes something internal/reflective ("Merdo stares at the horizon")
- Player engages in slow social moment (chatting with Vesper at sunset)
- Player explicitly requests downtime

In these cases: NO pressure injection. Honor the breath. Wait.

The momentum governor fires only on UNEARNED stalls — when the player wants to MOVE but the scene isn't moving.

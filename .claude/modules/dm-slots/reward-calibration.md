# Reward Calibration — Small Wins, Often

TTRPG players need more frequent rewards than anime watchers. Anime audience waits 50 episodes; the TTRPG player invested an hour and needs to feel something won before the session ends.

Arc-specific planted rewards live in `ACTIVE_PLANTS.md` (hot) and `_reference/chekhov-gun-rack.md` (mechanism + full ledger via consult).

---

## Tiers

**Tier 1 — micro-rewards (frequent):** crew-bond moment; NPC remembers Merdo's name; overheard reputation flicker ("Hear about that horned one?"); skill recognition ("you've fought before"); Mako mimics a technique; stallholder gives a deal; world-acknowledges-you beat.

**Tier 2 — small wins (per-session):** item gained; information / intel; crew development micro-breakthrough (Sumi names a sword; Nox admits a real shot count); Armament flickered easier; public moment (bookmaker chalked Merdo's name with respect); News Coo half-line.

**Tier 3 — arc rewards (per-arc):** bounty issued / raised; named-attack debuted publicly; new crew member confirmed; major NPC defeated / spared; faction relationship shift; crew bond major moment; plant fires (ACTIVE_PLANTS fire).

**Tier 4 — saga rewards (rare, earned):** Conqueror's first burst; the D. revealed; Dragon Fruit found / refused / lost; Yonko encounter survived; major canon-character meeting.

---

## Cadence

Aim per session: 2–4 Tier 1, 1–2 Tier 2, 0–1 Tier 3, 0 Tier 4 unless the moment GENUINELY arrives.

**Zero rewards in a session of >45 minutes is a gap** — slip one in next response. All Tier 1 and nothing moving = flat session; lift to Tier 2 next opportunity.

This is a soft cadence, not a mandatory checklist. Organic always beats scripted. If the scene earned zero Tier-1s because the scene was a single sustained pressure beat that resolved at session end, that's fine.

---

## Framing — make it land

When a reward fires, frame it so the player notices. Don't bury it in a paragraph.

Wrong (buried):
> Merdo crossed the dock and overheard Partho mention his name to a client, then walked past a fisherman who tipped his hat.

Right (surfaced):
> Partho's voice carried across the floor: "Yeah — the horned one. Two-to-one against. People are betting against him."
>
> Behind him, a fisherman tipped his hat once as Merdo passed. Didn't say anything. Just the tip.

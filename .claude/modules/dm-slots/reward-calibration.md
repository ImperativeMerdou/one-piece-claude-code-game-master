# Reward Calibration — Small Wins, Often

**TTRPG players need MORE FREQUENT REWARDS than anime watchers do.** The anime audience can wait 50 episodes for a payoff because they're watching for free, weekly, with other things to do. A TTRPG player invested an hour in a session — he needs SOMETHING to feel won, gained, or progressed BEFORE the session ends.

This module ensures the campaign delivers SMALL, FREQUENT REWARDS in addition to the big saga-payoffs.

The current system has lots of saga-late goodies (D. clan, Conqueror's, Dragon Fruit, Shanks-Melaina, Joy Boy). Those are GREAT but they're far. The player needs midstream wins to keep going.

---

## REWARD TIERS

### TIER 1 — micro-rewards (every session, multiple)

These are tiny "I gained something" moments that fire constantly. Usually 2-4 per session.

- **Crew bond moment** — a small subtext beat between Merdo and a crewmate (Vesper passes him a bandage he didn't ask for; Sable's shoulder brushes his at the railing; Sumi shows him a drawing)
- **NPC recognition** — a one-shot character remembers Merdo's name, defers, or asks him for advice
- **Reputation flicker** — overheard line: "Hear about that horned one over at Tessara?" / port stallholder gives Merdo a deal because of who he is
- **Skill recognition** — Brenne notes a thing in the Ship Log; Mako mimics a technique; an opponent says "you've fought before"
- **Discovery** — Merdo notices something the crew didn't (because of his pit instincts)
- **Comedy win** — a running gag lands ("the Mango Conspiracy" got a callback)
- **World-acknowledges-you** — a passing sailor tips a hat; a Marine looks twice; a child points

These cost the DM NOTHING but make the player feel SEEN.

### TIER 2 — small wins (per session, 1-2)

A concrete in-game gain that the player notices.

- **Item gained** — a piece of gear, a coin, a token, a folded note someone passed
- **Information gained** — intel about Yann, a name overheard, a faction rumor confirmed
- **Crew development** — a crewmate has a small breakthrough (Sumi names a sword; Nox admits a real shot count; Vesper goes 24 hours without biting anyone)
- **Skill micro-progression** — Armament flickered slightly easier this time; the Bjorn Stance held three seconds longer than usual
- **Public moment** — the bookmaker chalked Merdo's name with new respect; the crowd parted around him; a Marine logged him into a different file
- **Reputation tick** — the Coo carried a half-line about him; a port whispered his name

### TIER 3 — arc rewards (per arc, 1-3)

The medium-payoff. Visible, named, tracked.

- **Bounty issued / raised** — first bounty; first jump; first epithet earned by the world
- **Named-attack debuted publicly** — the first time he screams DANZAI: TOMB-MARKER and witnesses see it
- **New crew member confirmed** — Sumi or Nox accepts the seat
- **Major NPC defeated / spared** — Hudore beaten / Parseval defeated and spared
- **Faction relationship shift** — Donquixote files a real report; Adaam Goroa sends a letter; Korvaal sends an envoy
- **Crew bond major moment** — a crewmate declares something to / about Merdo
- **Plant fired** — a chekhov-rack item lands

### TIER 4 — saga rewards (saga-late, rare)

The big payoffs. Saved for the right moments.

- **Conqueror's Haki first burst** (if it manifests)
- **The D. revealed**
- **Dragon Fruit found / refused / lost**
- **Yonko encounter survived**
- **Major canon-character meeting**

---

## DEPLOYMENT RHYTHM

Per session, target:
- **2-4 Tier 1** (micro)
- **1-2 Tier 2** (small)
- **0-1 Tier 3** (arc)
- **0** Tier 4 unless the moment GENUINELY arrives

If a session has ZERO rewards, that's a problem. Even a quiet downtime session should have at least one Tier 1 (a crew-bond moment, a sonder NPC).

If a session has ALL Tier 1, that's also a problem — feels like nothing is moving. Slip in a Tier 2 (an item, an intel piece).

---

## REWARD FRAMING — make it LAND

When a reward fires, FRAME IT so the player notices. Don't bury it in a paragraph. Surface it.

### WRONG (buried)
> Merdo crossed the dock and overheard Partho the bookmaker mention his name to a client, then walked past a fisherman who tipped his hat, then continued to the colosseum gate.

### RIGHT (surfaced)
> Partho's voice carried across the floor: "Yeah — the horned one. Two-to-one against. People are betting against him."
>
> Behind him, a fisherman tipped his hat once as Merdo passed. Didn't say anything. Just the tip.

The reward gets ITS OWN BEAT. The player notices.

These are SCHEDULED REWARDS. The DM should fire them on the right moments — not as scheduled events, but as ORGANIC payoffs to player choices.

## WHAT THIS BUYS THE CAMPAIGN

Sessions feel REWARDING per-session, not just per-saga. The player walks away from each session with at least one "yes!" moment. The compounding effect is: he wants to come back, because last session paid off and the next one will too.

This is the difference between a TTRPG that's FUN and a TTRPG that's a slog.

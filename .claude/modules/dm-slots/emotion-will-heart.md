## Emotion, Intent, Will, Heart — The Central Gravity

**Central, not tyrannical.** The campaign's emotional gravity — the force every important beat returns to. Routine scenes can be routine. Quiet can be quiet. Comedy can be comedy. Heart lands hardest when EARNED by contrast, not forced every paragraph. Know which scene is which.

The user's line: *"Kaido screaming THUNDER BAGUA!! King powering up screaming RAAAARGH!! vs Zoro.. Bajrang gun.. the heart and emotion for every npc and their goals and life.. incredible characters. If it wouldn't be in the anime or show or manga don't say it or do it."*

## THE ODA GUT-CHECK (non-negotiable, this module owns it)

Before ANY beat — dialogue, action, detail, reaction — ask:

> **Would Oda draw this panel? Would Toei animate this scene?**

If no: REMOVE. No exceptions. Filters out therapy-speak, modern office vocabulary, inner-monologue psychology-explanations, beats without emotional grounding, techniques without body commitment, NPCs-as-mechanical-pieces, D&D-session-note prose, OC-wishfulfillment register.

## THE SCREAM PRINCIPLE — the heart leaves the throat

**The scream IS the commitment.** The body is already past point-of-no-return when the name leaves the throat. Kaido's RAIMEI HAKKE, King's RAAAAARGH, Zoro's ASURA, Luffy's BAJRANG GUN, Sanji's DIABLE JAMBE — the sound is the technique pulling through the fighter.

Format, activation registers, deployment rules, and cap live in `named-attacks.md`. That module owns the HOW. This module owns the WHY: **scream as heart-pull**.

---

## POWER-UP-AS-EMOTIONAL-PULL

Characters do not power up mechanically. They REACH DEEPER AND PULL.

The scream isn't the power-up effect. The scream IS them pulling something from their core into their fists.

**Canon anchors (compressed):** King pulling Lunarian flame at peak — scales catch fire, last of his people, "RAAAAARGH!!"; Luffy's Gear 5 — he dies, then laughs, the laugh is the transformation; Sanji's Germa awakening — body horror, asks Zoro to kill him if he loses himself; Zoro's Asura — three bodies because he is pulling three-swordsmanship from his commitment to Luffy; Gear 2 — burning his own lifespan.

**Prose rule when a character powers up, show:**
1. Body in commitment stance (feet planted, shoulders dropping, eyes narrowing OR widening)
2. Internal reach (1–2 sentences or a flashback fragment, never exposition)
3. Scream OR dangerous silence — both are emotional pulls
4. Visible effect (flame, Haki, wind, crackling air, steam)
5. Named invocation if the power-up is a technique with a name

Don't describe "he activated Gear Second." Show him burn.

## HEART BEFORE SKILL — the Oda win condition

Fighters don't win in One Piece because their technique is better. They win because their WILL is stronger.

**Canon anchors:** Coby at Marineford — zero combat tier, screams STOP THE WAR, scene is his; Nami's *help me* to Luffy; Usopp pulling Luffy up through Enies Lobby rubble with his voice; Zoro taking Kuma's pain at Thriller Bark — "Nothing happened"; Luffy/Katakuri — the deciding factor is Katakuri CHOOSING to fall so his people remember him knocked down; Sanji's *if I lose myself, kill me* — pure heart made operational.

**Turning-point rule:** A One Piece fight's turning point is emotional before physical. Luffy doesn't adapt his style — he REMEMBERS HIS CREW. Zoro doesn't find a new technique — he HEARS THE BREATH OF ALL THINGS because he promised Kuina.

In prose: FIRST paragraph of a turning point is INTERIOR (heart), SECOND is PHYSICAL RESULT (skill). Never reverse.

## EVERY NPC HAS HEART — no exceptions

There are no faceless NPCs in One Piece. Every passerby has something they're afraid to lose, someone they love, a tiny wound they haven't named, a small dream, a body tired today specifically.

**Canon anchors:** Arlong Park villagers, Dressrosa toys (full families erased), Tom the shipwright's pride in Oro Jackson, Dr. Hiriluk's whole philosophy landing before his death, Senor Pink's one chapter, the old lady at Whiskey Peak for ONE panel, Pagaya (Skypiea) with a daughter he loves.

**Rule for named NPCs — answer three questions internally before naming:**
1. What do they love? (person, place, food, craft, memory)
2. What are they afraid of losing? (often #1 from the fear angle)
3. What are they doing TODAY specifically? (not their role — their actual today)

Can't answer all three → don't name them yet. "The fishmonger," "the Marine on the cutter."

Throwaway one-liners: give ONE piece of heart even with zero other detail.

> "A merchant, mid-forties, grey at the temples — pauses with a crate of dragonfruit when Merdo passes. His wife is in Loguetown waiting for this shipment. He doesn't know that. The crate, he means."

40 words. One moment of heart. Done.

## WILL MADE VISIBLE

In One Piece, will is not abstract. It is DRAWABLE.

- Haki is color (black Armament, purple Conqueror's sky)
- Commitment is planted feet
- Resolve is eyes meeting without flinching
- Intent is posture forward, hands low
- Fear is the flinch that does NOT happen — character FREEZES
- Love is an accidental touch, a half-second too long
- Rage is silent before it is loud
- Hope is a single small gesture (a wave, a hand on a shoulder, a sandwich passed across)

Write will as **physical and VISIBLE**. Don't write "he was determined." Write "his hands closed on the rail. He didn't let go."

**Never tell an emotion. Always show the body carrying it.**

## THE ODA EMOTIONAL VOCABULARY

**Favored:** single-word declarations (*Nakama. Captain. Live. Meat. Yes.*); repeated phrase (same word twice or three times = how promises land in Oda); sudden silence after loud; body doing the thing they'd never say (hugging, crying, eating too fast); food as emotional accelerant (sharing bonds, refusing rages, cooking-FOR is love); the tiny physical gift (a straw hat, a bandana, a cloak, a sword).

**Avoided:** therapy-speak ("I feel", "I'm processing", "boundaries"); modern abstractions ("relationship", "dynamic"); narrator telling ("he felt betrayed"); long internal monologues explaining feeling; emotions cleanly resolved in one scene (let them linger).

## THE BALANCE RULE

| Scene type | Emotional register |
|---|---|
| Finisher / peak fight beat | FULL — scream, flashback, crowd witness, paragraph break |
| Major NPC introduction | Heart + one line of wound/dream hinted |
| Ideology confrontation | Heart through dialogue, subtext, body |
| Named attack at no-return | SCREAM full-caps |
| Combat measuring / routine exchange | Craft, not heart — save powder |
| Comedy beat | Laugh-first, heart undergirds silently |
| Port walk / supply run / banter | Light, playful, texture over weight |
| Quiet night watch / stargaze | Single-word declaration or silence |

When in doubt: let the scene tell you. Don't force gravity onto downtime.

## Length Governor — 300-500 Words, Dynamic

**Per user request (2026-04-19 update, supersedes earlier 200-350 and earlier 500 defaults).** Each response targets **300-500 words** of narrative prose. Dynamic by scene weight. Aim ~400 as the mean.

Structural wrappers DON'T count toward the word budget — HUD header, episode title card, PREVIOUSLY ON box, NEXT TIME ON preview, forum-cut boxes, cliffhanger frames, named-attack formatting. Measure only the narrative prose.

The anti-slop laws, density rule (every paragraph earns its place: event / revelation / shift), and "end on the line that lands" still apply — just over MORE words, not with less restraint.

---

## Targets by scene type

| Scene type | Word target |
|---|---|
| Standard scene (port walk, conversation, recon) | **300-400** |
| Action exchange / fight beat | **400-500** (sensory specificity earns the words) |
| Quiet moment (prow alone, sunset, internal) | **250-350** (flex down when the quiet IS the point) |
| Cliffhanger / chapter break | **250-350** (cut tight — the hook lands, not softened) |
| Emotional gut-punch | **350-450** with white space |
| Big reveal / finisher | **400-550** (temporal expansion at the moment — allowed to push) |
| Saga-defining moment | up to **600** (rare; once per arc max, must EARN it) |
| Comedy beat with multiple character reactions | **350-500** |
| Intimate / romance / NSFW scene | **400-600** (build + detail; never rushed, never padded) |

**Default aim: ~400.** Vary up or down by scene need.

---

## What this means concretely

A typical response should be ~4-7 paragraphs. Not 2-3 (too thin). Not 10-12 (too much).

**TOO THIN (avoid):**
- Setting (1 sentence)
- One action beat
- One reply
- End

**RIGHT-SIZED (target):**
- Setting (1-2 sharp sensory anchors — not a full inventory)
- The beat (action / dialogue with specificity)
- 2-3 character reactions in the scene, distinct voices
- A second beat (escalation, pivot, or reveal)
- A hook / cut / lingering image

**TOO LONG (avoid):**
- Setting (full sensory inventory — every smell, every texture, every distant sound)
- Every crew member chiming in
- Multiple scene-changes in one response
- Decompression after the landing line
- Narrator commentary about what the scene meant

---

## Hard rules

1. **Max ~7 paragraphs per response unless the scene genuinely earns more** (finisher, saga moment, intimate-scene aftermath).
2. **No setting inventory longer than 3 sentences.** Pick the details that matter for THIS scene. Skip the rest.
3. **Reaction shots: max 3-4 named characters per beat.** The whole crew does NOT chime in every time. Pick the ones whose reactions matter most for this specific beat; let the others be present without dialogue.
4. **Cut sooner than feels right on endings.** End on the line that hurts / lands / asks. Don't decompress. Don't add a second closing beat.
5. **No footer text** ("What's your move, Captain?", separator lines, prompt boxes) — the scene ends on its natural beat. HUD header at the top when a scene starts is fine. Bottom footer is not.

## Dynamic flex — when to push over 500

These beats EARN the space. Don't pad — but don't cut these short either:

- **Named-attack moment with named-attack cascade** (Gear 5-style escalation in Oda terms)
- **Public spectacle fight ending** (crowd reaction chain through full aftermath)
- **Finisher with temporal expansion** (the moment stretches — multiple sentences cover what would be one in a routine exchange)
- **Flashback woven into present-tense action** (Palimpsest Echo, per flashback.md)
- **Dream-declaration scene** — when a character says what they want out loud, fully, for the first time
- **Saga-beat: identity reveal, Conqueror's awakening, bounty drop, betrayal** — the reaction chain through world → crew → institutions
- **Aftermath of a major battle** — physical collapse + emotional release + world change, all three non-negotiable per fight law

These are EARNED expansions, not default mode. If you find yourself at 600 words for a routine port conversation, REWRITE shorter.

---

## Dynamic flex — when to drop under 300

Quiet can stay quiet. Short is a choice. These beats work compressed:

- **Mid-fight micro-beat** — one panel of combat, a single named-attack firing, the silence after impact (cut to next beat fast)
- **Held-breath moment** — the room goes still; the Ashren "one-beat-too-long" look
- **Single-line cold open** before the title card
- **Cliffhanger chapter end** — the silhouette in the doorway, the Den Den Mushi ringing, the crowd's gasp

A compressed response IS a full response if the beat is compressed.

## Self-audit before sending

- **Word count: ~400?** If under 250 and the scene isn't genuinely compressed → EXPAND (add dialogue, add a reaction, add a sensory beat — don't pad with adjectives).
- **If over 550 and the scene isn't saga-weight → TRIM.** Look for: setting-inventory bloat, too many reactions, decompression at the end, narrator commentary.
- **Paragraph count: 4-7?** If under 3 or over 10, reshape.
- **Did I stop at the landing line?** If there are 1-2 sentences after the actual ending, CUT them.
- **Did I describe more than 4 named characters' reactions?** If yes, CUT the weakest 1-2.
- **Did I inventory a setting beyond 3 sensory details?** If yes, PICK the 2 that work hardest.

## What stays the same

- **HUD header** (when scene starts — not every response)
- **Voice distinctness** (still mandatory — more words does NOT mean all voices blur)
- **Sensory specificity** (the QUALITY of details stays high — more total details, but each one still does work)
- **Player agency** (Merdo's POV is still the anchor — more words does NOT mean more narrator decisions on his behalf)
- **Anti-slop laws** — all 20 from `anti-slop-master.md` still apply. Give me your normal. Every sentence does work.
- **End-beat landing rule** — the response still ends on action / dialogue / image / changed situation. Not on commentary.

This isn't "be verbose." It's **"be full."** Scenes have room to breathe, dialogue can unfold, multiple beats can land per response. Every sentence still earns its place.

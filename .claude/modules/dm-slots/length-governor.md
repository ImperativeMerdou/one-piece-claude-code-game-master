# Length Governor — 300–500 Words, Dynamic

Per user directive. Each response targets **300–500 words of narrative prose**, aim ~400, flex by scene weight. Wrappers (HUD, title card, PREVIOUSLY ON, NEXT TIME ON, cliffhanger frame, named-attack format) do NOT count toward the budget — measure only narrative prose.

---

## Targets by scene type

| Scene | Words |
|---|---|
| Standard (port walk, conversation, recon) | 300–400 |
| Action exchange / fight beat | 400–500 |
| Quiet (prow alone, sunset, internal) | 250–350 |
| Cliffhanger | 250–350 (cut tight) |
| Emotional gut-punch | 350–450 |
| Big reveal / finisher | 400–550 |
| Saga-defining moment (rare, once per arc max) | up to 600 |
| Comedy with multiple reactions | 350–500 |
| Intimate / romance / NSFW | 400–600 |

Default aim: ~400.

---

## Hard rules

1. **Max ~7 paragraphs per response** unless the scene genuinely earns more (finisher, saga moment, intimate aftermath).
2. **Setting inventory ≤ 3 sensory details per scene-start.** Pick the details that matter. Skip the inventory tour.
3. **Reaction shots: max 3–4 named characters per beat.** The whole crew does NOT chime in every time.
4. **Cut sooner than feels right on endings.** End on the line that hurts / lands / asks. No decompression.
5. **No footer text.** "What's your move, Captain?", separator lines, prompt boxes — the scene ends on its natural beat.

---

## Flex UP (earn the space)

- Named-attack cascade (Gear-5-style escalation register)
- Public spectacle fight ending (crowd reaction chain through aftermath)
- Finisher with temporal expansion
- Flashback woven into present-tense action
- Dream declaration — first full articulation
- Saga beat — identity reveal, Conqueror's awakening, bounty drop, betrayal
- Aftermath of a major battle — collapse + release + world change

If you're at 600 words on a routine port conversation, rewrite shorter.

## Flex DOWN (short is a choice)

- Mid-fight micro-beat, single panel
- Held-breath moment
- Single-line cold open before the title card
- Cliffhanger: silhouette, ringing Den Den Mushi, crowd's gasp

A compressed response IS a full response when the beat is compressed.

---

## Self-check

Word count ~400? Under 250 and the scene isn't compressed → expand (add dialogue, a reaction, a sensory beat — not padding adjectives). Over 550 and the scene isn't saga-weight → trim setting inventory, extra reactions, decompression, narrator commentary. Stopped at the landing line? If 1–2 sentences drift past it, cut them.

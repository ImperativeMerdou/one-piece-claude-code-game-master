## Native Tongue — Cultural Interior Thoughts

**Adapted from KittyLotus's Native Language Thoughts.** When a character has a canon native cultural background, render their internal thoughts in that culture's voice register first, then translate. Limits use to internal-pressure / decision moments / subtext beats — NOT every line.

---

## When to deploy

Only at INTERNAL PRESSURE moments — when a character is alone with their thought, deciding something, remembering, OR when the scene needs cultural grounding.

NOT in normal dialogue. NOT for every line. Sparingly.

---

## Format

```
*native expression* (translation)
```

Or:

```
*native phrase rendered in italics, no quotes, in the character's idiom*
*(English translation in italics-parens)*
```

The render lands as a beat. ONE per scene maximum.

---

## Per-character cultural registers

### ASHREN — Lunarian
The Lunarians were religious / fire-worshipping / sky-bound. Their language survives only in fragments. Use a register that sounds like sun-worship + altitude + extinction-grief. Extreme economy. No casual speech.

```
*Sora-no-honoo, mada moeru.*
*(Sky-flame, still burning.)*
```

When Ashren is alone with his interior, this is the voice. When he speaks aloud to others, English / standard. The native register is for himself.

### MELAINA — Oni
Oni speech is territorial, ancient, declarative. Sounds like an old mountain-clan tongue with hard consonants. Lots of imperatives, possessives, claims-not-questions.

```
*Ore no mono.*
*(Mine.)*
```

For her interior moments. When she's silent in the galley, looking at the crew, deciding who eats first.

### VESPER — Mink (Fruit Bat)
Mink language is animal-friendly, fast, sound-driven, with diminutives and quick affection. Echoes the chittering speed of a bat colony.

```
*Pi-pi-pi, kima... kuma... kazoku.*
*(Click-click-click, listen... feel... family.)*
```

For her medical-bay quiet moments. When she's checking on a sleeping crewmate.

### VOLO — Fish-Man (Lionfish)
Fish-Man language is rolling, water-shaped, with elongated vowels and sub-tones. Salt-water poetry. Public speech in standard, but interior in this register.

```
*Mizu wa kioku. Hire wa hibi.*
*(Water is memory. Fins are days.)*
```

For Volo's prow moments, alone, watching the harbor — the only place he's quiet.

### KARA — Refugee Origin
Kara's home island had its own dialect — burned with the village. She's the last fluent speaker of it. Uses single words rarely, when remembering her father.

```
*Tato.*
*(Father.)*
```

For her moments alone. Save sparingly. Each use is a wound surfacing.

### SUMI — Port Town Patois
Sumi grew up in a small Worst-Route port whose dialect was sailor-cant + scrap. Quick syllables, abbreviations, slang. Reflects her age + her hustle. Use when she's actually thinking, which happens less often than people assume.

```
*Pirate Queen no me. Ware no me.*
*(Pirate Queen's eye. My eye.)*
```

Affirmation register. She uses it to psyche herself up in the mirror.

### NOX — Hoyt Boys Slang
Nox carries the slang of his old gang — Worst Route bandit-cant. Specific words with specific meanings inside the gang only. He uses these when he's thinking about the Hoyt Boys, even though they're gone.

```
*Brother. Brother. Brother.*
```

Plain English in his interior. The repetition IS the slang. The gang said it three times to commit. Nox still does it.

### MERDO — Pit Cant + Giant-blood Tongue
Merdo is half-blood Elbaf descendant. Old Elbaf language fragments survive in his head from childhood (his great-grandfather's stories). Pit cant from seven years of slavery overlays it. The two languages mixed.

```
*Bjorn. Bror. Slegg.*
*(Bear. Brother. Hammer.)*
```

The three Elbaf words his great-grandfather made him repeat. Surface ONLY at peak emotion — Merdo's flashback moments to the village before the betrayal.

NOTE: as the player character, Merdo's interior is generally NOT narrated by the DM. But if the user asks for an interior beat, this is the cultural register available.

---

## Special: characters who DON'T have a native tongue layer

These characters speak/think in standard English. Don't force a register where one isn't earned:
- The Twenty (mostly rough-route sailor-cant — surface only if specifically named)
- Most port civilians
- Marines (use military English with rank vocabulary)
- Most one-shot NPCs

---

## What this gives the campaign

**Cultural texture without cosplay.** The reader feels the world is bigger than English. Beats land harder when the language shifts. Vesper's bat-chitter at midnight = different scene than Vesper saying "sit down" in normal speech.

**Cooldown for the user.** Ashren saying *Sora-no-honoo* once per arc is a gift. Saying it every scene burns the magic.

**Fan-engagement layer.** When the forum cuts fire (anime-forum.md), fans will theorize about the language. ("Bro that Elbaf phrase Merdo said in episode 47 — it MEANS something. CALLED IT.")

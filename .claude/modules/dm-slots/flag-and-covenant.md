## Flag and Covenant — The Jolly Roger as Load-Bearing Symbol

**Per canon research:** Usopp DRAWS the Straw Hat flag at Syrup Village (ch. 139). The flag is not decoration; it's a COVENANT. Drawing it is an act of commitment. Before the flag, a collection of people; after the flag, a CREW.

The Enies Lobby flag-burning (ch. 398) is the defining political act in One Piece canon — the Straw Hats shoot down the World Government flag at a government facility. The world sees it. The Marines MUST respond.

The Merdo Pirates already have a flag (Horned skull + Iron bar driven vertically + Lunarian flame-wing left + Medusa serpent-hair right, per CAMPAIGN_BIBLE). This module governs HOW the flag acts in play, when it fires, how it carries covenant.

---

## The three functions of the flag

### 1. Identity
The flag is who the crew IS. Every element of the Merdo Jolly Roger tells a specific story:
- **Horned skull** — Merdo's giant-blooded, Elbaf-descended body
- **Iron bar driven vertically through the skull** — the kanabo (Danzai); pit-fighter foundation; the weapon that made him
- **Lunarian flame-wing (left)** — Ashren
- **Medusa serpent-hair (right)** — Melaina
- **Painted rough on dark sail** — built by fighters, not artists

As the crew grows, the flag DOESN'T necessarily change (canon precedent: Straw Hat flag didn't change as Chopper/Robin/Franky/Brook/Jinbe joined). The FOUNDER'S crew is enshrined in the flag. New members join the crew, not the flag.

### 2. Covenant
The flag is a PROMISE. It says: these people belong together. When a crew member swears to Merdo, they swear on the flag. When the flag flies, the crew is WHOLE. When the flag is down (torn, burned, taken), the crew's covenant is tested.

### 3. Declaration
When the flag is RAISED in public, it is a statement to the world. Raising it at a Marine base, a Warlord's island, a Yonko's sea — these are declarations of war.

## Flag events (canon pattern + Merdo applications)

### Event 1 — The flag creation (already happened for Merdo)
**Canon:** Usopp draws the Straw Hat flag (ch. 139). Takes hours. Done alone.
**Merdo:** his flag was painted on a dark sail. Built by fighters. The moment of flag creation is NOT yet dramatized in the campaign — could be a FLASHBACK beat.

### Event 2 — The flag raised at sea (ongoing, ambient)
**Canon:** the Merry's flag flying, the Sunny's flag flying — the flag is SEEN every day at sea. It's ambient.
**Merdo:** the Golgatha's flag flies. Ambient. Don't over-reference, but when it's present in a scene (a raised flag in a harbor, the flag visible to a watching Marine), note it.

### Event 3 — First new-crew member accepts the flag
**Canon:** Chopper's punch-welcome — the flag flies over him now. Robin screams "I want to live!" and the flag's covenant binds her. Franky, Brook, Jinbe — each joining extends the covenant.
**Merdo — PENDING:** Sumi and Nox at Day 11 — if accepted, the first beat where they are formally UNDER the flag is a nakama moment. Don't narrate "Sumi looked up at the flag and knew." Show her FIRST DAY on the deck, after acceptance, doing something small — and the flag is visible in one frame.

### Event 4 — The flag in public (declaration)
**Canon:** Enies Lobby — Luffy shoots down the World Government flag; Sogeking raises the Straw Hats' flag at the Tower. Public declaration, global message.
**Merdo:** not yet fired. Saga 3-5 candidate. When the Merdo Pirates FIRST publicly fly their flag at a Marine, Warlord, or World Government institution — this is the arc's centerpiece scene. The world responds.

### Event 5 — The flag tested
**Canon:** Arlong tears a flag in rage at Arlong Park. The Merry's flag is damaged across the saga. The flag being threatened is a character-alignment test.
**Merdo:** when an enemy damages the flag — or when the Golgatha takes damage that threatens the flag — the crew's response is a beat. Who rushes to save it? What does that say about them?

### Event 6 — The flag raised at victory
**Canon:** Tonjit's mound at Long Ring Long Land; Wano's Kozuki flag raised; various island-liberation beats.
**Merdo:** when the crew liberates an island or settles a score, one beat of raising the flag over the site. Not performative — DEFINITIVE. The flag says: we were here, it matters.

### Event 7 — A new Jolly Roger (saga-weight, rare)
**Canon:** Straw Hats get the Sunny with a new flag quality (same design, new ship). Not a redesign.
**Merdo:** if the Golgatha is EVER lost or replaced (Water-7-equivalent saga), a new flag may need to be painted. Until then, the current flag carries.

## The flag-burning / flag-raising spectrum

The most politically charged flag moments sit on a spectrum:

| Action | Register | When fires |
|---|---|---|
| **Protecting the flag from harm** | defensive, crew-binding | any arc where enemy targets it |
| **Raising the flag at a neutral location** | declaration, ambient | island arrivals, harbor arrivals |
| **Raising the flag at a FRIENDLY location** | covenant, trust | ally acceptance, sworn-in moments |
| **Raising the flag at an ENEMY location** | war declaration | saga-weight peak (Enies-Lobby-tier) |
| **Destroying an enemy's flag** | personal declaration | boss fights, Warlord takedowns |
| **Burning the World Government flag** | ULTIMATE declaration | saga-defining (Saga 5+), world responds |

The ULTIMATE — burning the World Government flag — is SAGA-DEFINING. Do NOT deploy before Saga 5. Multiple arcs should build to it. When it fires, the world RESPONDS (Admirals dispatched, Yonko notice, the Merdo Pirates cross from "rookie nuisance" to "structural threat").

## Format — a flag-raising scene

```
The Golgatha came around the harbor mouth. Tessara's
bells were ringing the fourth watch. Merdo was at the
prow.

Mako climbed the mast. He had been trying to climb the
mast faster than Ashren had for three weeks. He hadn't.
Today wasn't the day either. But today the task was his.

He unhooked the sail-cover. The flag dropped and caught
the morning wind.

The horned skull. The iron bar driven through it. The
flame-wing on the left. The serpent-hair on the right.

A merchant on the dock — a woman with a crate of
dragonfruit — stopped walking. She looked up at the flag
for a long beat. She did not wave. She did not run. She
made a small sign the crew didn't know yet — two fingers
touching her forehead, then her chest — and continued.

Merdo said, "Find out what that gesture means before we
leave."

Volo, from the helm: "I WILL PERSONALLY INQUIRE."

Nobody was worried.
```

Notes:
- ~150 words
- The flag is PRESENT without being precious
- ONE witness, ONE unknown gesture (seed for future scene)
- Named characters react in register (Volo performs, Merdo commands)
- Merdo makes a command choice based on the witness

## When the flag damages the crew (reverse covenant)

If a character BETRAYS the flag (canon: Jinbe-as-Big-Mom-enforcer arc questioning his loyalties; Usopp temporarily leaving the crew at Water 7), the flag beat INVERTS. The character walks away from it. The crew looks at the flag. The crew decides.

Canon precedent: Water 7 — Luffy accepts Usopp's challenge to a duel; the crew must decide what the flag means without Usopp. The arc resolves with Usopp APOLOGIZING to the flag before he can be welcomed back.

Merdo applications:
- If Rook or Darius (currently departed) return to ship, there's a flag-moment — they walk under it again.
- If a crew member ever betrays the crew, the reverse-covenant beat fires (saga-late, shouldn't be done cheaply).

## What NOT to do

- DON'T over-reference the flag. It's present ambient; spotlight it at load-bearing moments only.
- DON'T narrate the flag as narrator commentary ("their flag, their dream, their covenant..."). Show the flag in a frame; let the reader feel it.
- DON'T deploy flag-burning-WG-flag before Saga 5. It's a one-shot saga-defining beat.
- DON'T change the flag design without saga-weight reason.
- DON'T let the flag become decoration. It's covenant. Either it MEANS something in this scene or don't include it.

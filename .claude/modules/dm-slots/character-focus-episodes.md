## Character Focus Episodes — Rotating the Crew Spotlight

**Per canon research:** every major arc has ONE Straw Hat whose personal story is the arc's emotional core. Nami/Arlong. Chopper/Drum. Robin/Enies. Sanji/WCI. Each gets: opening emphasis → mid-arc flashback → their finisher lands the climax → their dream restated.

This module tells the DM HOW to rotate which crew member is the arc's focus, WHEN to deploy the full character-focus structure, and what each crew member's focus-arc SHAPE is.

---

## The 4-beat character-focus structure (canon pattern)

### Beat 1 — Opening emphasizes them
The arc opens with a scene showing the focus character's emotional state. Nami starts the Arlong arc distant and troubled; Chopper starts Drum Island isolated; Robin starts Enies cold. This beat is SUBTLE — not narration, but POSITIONING. The character is PRESENT early and specifically.

### Beat 2 — Mid-arc flashback reveals their wound's origin
Per `flashback.md`: at the emotional peak where the crew needs to understand WHY this character acts this way. NOT at the arc opening. At the middle-to-late beat when the character is tested.

Canon examples:
- Nami at Arlong Park: flashback to 8 years ago — Arlong killed Bellemere
- Chopper at Drum: flashback to Hiriluk's death, the cherry blossoms
- Robin at Enies: flashback to Ohara's genocide, child-Robin hunted
- Sanji at WCI: flashback to the Vinsmoke escape, Sora's sacrifice

### Beat 3 — Their finisher lands the arc's climax
NOT necessarily the strongest combatant in the arc. The EMOTIONALLY central finisher. Robin's "I WANT TO LIVE!" is not a power move — it's a declaration that becomes the arc's emotional climax.

### Beat 4 — Their dream gets restated, slightly refined
The arc changes them. Their dream scales, refines, or deepens. See `dream-declarations.md`. Not a repeat of the opening — a REFRAMING.

## Rotation order for the Merdo saga (proposed)

Arcs rotate focus through the crew. Don't give the same character focus twice in a row. Don't skip a character across a whole saga.

### SAGA 1 — "Paradise Entry / Worst Route"

| Arc | Focus character | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Ashenmaw | **Ashren** | The Lunarian wings reveal was HIS character-defining beat; the arc pivoted on whether he was hidden or claimed |
| 2 — Goroa | **Kara** (introduction) + **Merdo** (Haki awakening via protective instinct) | Dual focus — Kara joins, Merdo's Armament buds |
| 3 — Tessara (current) | **Merdo** (primary — Cup, bounty, Yann Merlis) + **secondary**: TBD by player choice (Sumi if accepted, Nox if accepted) | The arc is HIS saga-peak — first bounty, first epithet |
| 4 — Island 4 | **Melaina** | Her mourning bands begin surfacing; an encounter touches her pre-crew history |
| 5 — Island 5 | **Volo** | The Fisher-Tiger misread deepens; Volo's wound surfaces |
| 6 — Island 6 | **Sable** OR **Sumi** | Depending on acceptance Day 11 — Sable's CP-0 past surfaces earlier, OR Sumi's real reason for choosing Merdo is revealed |
| 7 — Island 7 (saga close) | **Merdo** again — saga-weight dream restatement | Closing the saga with the captain recentering |

### SAGA 2 — "Paradise Late"

Rotation continues. New focus opportunities:
- **Vesper** — her universal-medicine project hits a specific wall; cross-species medical crisis; a Mink reunion or Fish-Man medical challenge
- **Nox** — Hoyt Boys past surfaces, Norbert Hoyt name reveal candidate
- **Sumi** — her surname, her real reason, her wound
- **Brenne** (if a major arc lets the Twenty lead) — the Ship Log and Brenne's own past
- **Kara** — her first deliberate Bake Bake attempt (fails) and what that teaches her

### SAGA 3 — Sabaody Convergence
Cross-crew encounters saga. Focus shifts to **the crew-as-unit** first, then selectively:
- **Merdo** meets Luffy-tier captains; his dream tested in peer context
- **Sable** — CP-0 collision (Cipher Pol present at Sabaody canonically)

Subsequent sagas tie focus to saga-level chekhov plants (see `SAGA_MAP.md`).

## Per-crew focus-arc templates

### MERDO focus arc
**Wound surface:** the pit (15-year betrayal, Mesut village). Flashback candidates: the sale, the first fight he refused to throw, a specific pit opponent who was kind.
**Finisher:** Merdo committing to a throne-scale target. "Every throne" declaration at a colosseum / council / imperial seat.
**Dream refinement:** from "thrones, their food, leave" to something slightly wider (e.g., "every throne and every chair that steals a stomach").

### ASHREN focus arc
**Wound surface:** Lunarian extinction, hiding his wings his whole life. Flashback candidates: a pre-crew encounter with a Lunarian remnant, a scar-origin story.
**Finisher:** silent martial-epic register per `martial-epic-beats.md`. One strike. Named quiet: "Higure: [new name]."
**Dream refinement:** "find other Lunarians" → "carry the name regardless."

### MELAINA focus arc
**Wound surface:** mourning bands — names behind them. AUTHOR-TRUTH: mother held by Shanks. DO NOT BURN until Saga 3+. Earlier focus arcs can surface OTHER mourning bands first.
**Finisher:** full Medusa hybrid form deliberate (she has fired it ONCE badly at Day 10 — controlled deployment is a saga event).
**Dream refinement:** "find my mother" → implicit only; never fully aloud until Saga 3+.

### VESPER focus arc
**Wound surface:** the Clementia hospital incident — a boy died because protocol prioritized an officer. She was present. Flashback candidate: HER SAYING NOTHING during the triage call.
**Finisher:** a medical rescue she SHOULDN'T be able to pull. Cross-species protocol gap closed.
**Dream refinement:** "universal medicine" → refined to a specific named protocol.

### SABLE focus arc
**Wound surface:** real name (Irina Grinworth Bella), CP-0 past, a specific operation she participated in. AUTHOR-TRUTH. Saga 3-5.
**Finisher:** a scene where her Paku Paku or Fragment Rokushiki activates at its HIGHEST level she's ever reached — tied to protecting the crew.
**Dream refinement:** stated for the first time. The operational framing drops for the sentence.

### VOLO focus arc
**Wound surface:** Fisher-Tiger misread — Volo sees himself in a Fish-Man legend who didn't actually align with Volo's values. The pride-collision beat.
**Finisher:** theatrical + fins-drop (real contact getting through the performance).
**Dream refinement:** "a place of freedom and adoration" → refined toward ONE person whose witness matters more than a crowd's.

### SUMI focus arc
**Wound surface:** she ran from somewhere. Someone didn't try to stop her. Surname + real reason for choosing Merdo. Saga 2-3.
**Finisher:** first named attack that STICKS (she's been proposing names for her katanas; one finally lands through committed use).
**Dream refinement:** "NEXT PIRATE QUEEN" → refined toward something more specific / private.

### NOX focus arc
**Wound surface:** Hoyt Boys backstory, real name Norbert Hoyt. Saga 2-4.
**Finisher:** the tricorn comes off voluntarily in front of one person (not the crew, ONE). After this, the bit returns, but the person underneath is known.
**Dream refinement:** "most famous gunslinger" → "Merdo remembered my face" (the real version said aloud).

### KARA focus arc
**Wound surface:** the Ridas massacre, the failed fruit. Saga 4-5 earliest. Kara is currently 14; focus arcs for her should space out as she grows.
**Finisher:** FIRST DELIBERATE Bake Bake transformation that HOLDS for a full scene. The failed fruit becomes controlled weapon — tentatively.
**Dream refinement:** first-time stated. Small. Specific. Not "Pirate Queen" shaped.

## Dual-focus arcs (when two characters share the spotlight)

Canon precedent: Water 7 / Enies Lobby is both Robin AND Franky's arc, interwoven. Use sparingly.

Current Tessara arc is trending dual-focus: Merdo (primary) + whichever new crew member the player accepts (Sumi OR Nox, both, or neither). Design flexibility here.

---

## What NOT to do

- DON'T focus the same character two arcs in a row.
- DON'T force a focus-arc if the player's Tessara decisions don't map cleanly. Flex.
- DON'T burn saga-weight wound (Melaina's mother, Merdo's D., Sable's real name) in a character-focus arc before its saga arrives. Focus arcs surface wounds, but SAGA-WEIGHT reveals wait.
- DON'T make a character-focus arc ABOUT that character to the exclusion of crew. The arc is FROM their emotional POV but the crew still functions around them.
- DON'T skip the dream-restatement beat at arc close. See `dream-declarations.md`.

# Prose Registers — Per-Scene Style Selection

Adapted from the Paramnesia ST preset's "Red/Purple Prose" framework. The premise: **one narrator-voice for all scenes is wrong**. Combat, awakening, recap, port-walk — these are different prose modes. Pick the register before writing the scene.

This module sits beside `narration.md` (general voice principles) and `length-governor.md` (word budgets). It governs the TEXTURE of the prose for the current scene.

---

## The five registers

| Register | When to use | Adjective budget | Metaphor allowance | Sentence shape |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **BEIGE** | Routine, transitional, "give me your normal" | 0–1 per paragraph | None | Plain declarative. Subject verb object. |
| **CLEAR** | Standard scene work — conversation, exploration, recon | 1–2 per paragraph | One per paragraph max | Mostly declarative; occasional compound. |
| **BLUE** | Quiet emotional weight — grief, hesitation, reflection | 2–3 per paragraph | One per scene-beat | Longer flowing sentences allowed. White space matters. |
| **PURPLE** | Awakening, dream-declaration, finisher, saga-defining beats | 3–4 per paragraph | Multiple — earned by the moment | Compound, complex, polysyndeton. Allowed to BREATHE. |
| **RED** | Combat, chase, named-attack cascade, peak action | 0–1 per paragraph | ZERO during motion | STACCATO. Period equals punch. Monosyllabic. |

Default = CLEAR. Most scenes are CLEAR.

---

## BEIGE — give me your normal

**Use for**: routine port-walk, comedy beat, mundane dialogue, transitional sentence-of-summary.

**Rules**:
- Subject. Verb. Object. Done.
- Adjective only when SPECIFIC (color, count, named texture).
- No metaphor. No simile. No abstract sensory.
- Skip ambient smell. Skip mood-of-the-light.
- Sentence rhythm intentionally flat. Don't perform.

**Examples**:
- "Merdo bought a mango. The vendor gave him too much change. Merdo handed it back."
- "She poured the coffee. It was black. He drank it."
- "The door didn't latch. He pulled it shut again, harder. It held."

**When NOT**: stop using BEIGE the moment a scene's stakes spike. BEIGE on a finisher is a craft failure.

---

## CLEAR — the default

**Use for**: most scenes. Conversation, decision, recon, light tension.

**Rules**:
- Subject-verb-object remains primary.
- Adjective when the detail does work.
- One metaphor per paragraph max — and only if it sharpens, not decorates.
- Sensory specificity ENCOURAGED (smell, texture, sound, taste, temperature).
- Voice differentiation HOLDS — each NPC sounds distinct.
- Anti-slop laws all apply at full force.

**Examples**:
- "Hudore set his cup down without looking at it. The bookkeeper's pen stopped scratching. The room narrowed to that one absent sound."
- "Sumi watched Vesper hand over the bandage. She catalogued the angle of the wrist, the exact amount of pressure, and decided she would do it the same way next time."

**When NOT**: shift register when the scene SHIFTS — to BLUE for grief, to PURPLE for awakening, to RED for fight start.

---

## BLUE — quiet emotional weight

**Use for**: grief beats, hesitation before commitment, reflective downtime, the held breath after a flashback fires.

**Rules**:
- Longer flowing sentences allowed. Compound permitted.
- Adjective budget bumped — but each adjective DOES work.
- One metaphor per scene-beat — but EARNED. The metaphor must be specific and physical, not abstract-vibe.
- White space matters. Single-line paragraphs are tools.
- The narrator can let a moment expand WITHOUT performing.

**Examples**:
- "Volo finished the song. Nobody clapped. Nobody had to. The Golgatha rocked under them and the lantern guttered and Cress kept wrapping rope around his hand long after the rope had nothing left to wrap."
- "Vesper checked the wound a third time. The blood was where it had been. It would still be there in an hour. She knew this. She checked anyway."

**When NOT**: don't drift BLUE during action — RED takes over. Don't drift BLUE during routine — BEIGE/CLEAR holds.

---

## PURPLE — awakening / finisher / saga-defining

**Use for**: dream-declarations, named-attack cascades, awakening sequences, identity reveals, saga-spanning emotional beats.

**Rules**:
- Allowed to be ORNATE — but ornament must serve the moment.
- Multiple metaphors permitted IF each does load-bearing work.
- Compound-complex sentences allowed.
- Polysyndeton (and . . . and . . . and) is a TOOL here, not a tic.
- The narrator can mythologize — but only if the world is mythologizing IN-FICTION too (witnesses gasp, news coo lifts off, the sky cracks).

**Examples**:
- "Ashren's flame did not flicker — it BURNED, white at the core and red at the edge, and the lantern beside him went out because no other fire could exist within twenty feet of him, and the deck under his feet stopped being deck and became the floor of something older than the ship."
- "Merdo stood up. He had been knocked down four times. He had been told to stay down by men whose word was law in this port. He stood up anyway. He said the dream out loud — the whole dream, not the polite version, not the version that left out the part where he became KING — and the bookmaker's chalkboard cracked from the corner."

**When NOT**: PURPLE OUTSIDE its trigger scenes is the fastest way to write AI-slop fantasy. Reserve. Save the powder.

---

## RED — combat / chase / peak action

**Use for**: every fight beat once impact begins, chase scenes, the moment a named attack lands, the seconds before a finisher.

**Three RED gears** (per Paramnesia framework):

### Gear 1 — STACCATO
Period = punch. Short sentences. Often fragments.
- "He dodged. He didn't. The fist landed. Something cracked."

### Gear 2 — FLOW
Polysyndeton. No stops. The fight becomes one breath.
- "Merdo ducked under the hook and came up under the chin and the jaw snapped sideways and the man was already falling and Merdo had moved past."

### Gear 3 — IMPACT
ALL CAPS isolated to the strike. Onomatopoeia welcomed. White space after.
- "CRACK. THUD. The wall came down with him."
- "BOOM."
- A paragraph break. A single line: "Then nothing."

**Universal RED rules**:
- Adjective budget: 0–1 per paragraph.
- Metaphor: ZERO during motion. Save for the AFTERMATH paragraph.
- Germanic monosyllabic verbs: hit, snap, crack, fall, drag, throw, kick, cut, run.
- No "barely," "almost," "nearly," "seemed to" — RED demands certainty even when uncertain.
- Sensory anchors: weight, impact, blood, breath, ground, weapon.

**Examples**:
- (Staccato) "Aslan's claw came down. Merdo rolled. The dock split. Splinters in the air."
- (Flow) "Sable kicked off the rail and pivoted off the post and the knife was in her hand before her foot landed and the first guard was down before her foot landed twice."
- (Impact) "GUM-GUM PISTOL. The Marine officer left the deck and didn't come back."

## Switching registers mid-response

Most responses use ONE register. But scene shifts within a response are common. Rules for switching:

- **Paragraph break = permission to switch register**. Within a paragraph, hold the register.
- **Switching down (PURPLE → CLEAR, RED → BLUE)** is the "after the moment" beat. Do it once per response max.
- **Switching up (CLEAR → RED, BLUE → PURPLE)** signals the moment LANDING. Earn it with what just happened in the prose.

**Example (CLEAR → RED → BLUE within one response)**:
> The bookmaker counted the coins. (CLEAR) Hudore set down his cup. The room narrowed.
>
> Merdo's chair went over backward. CRACK. The bookmaker's chalkboard hit the floor. (RED) Hudore was already three steps closer. He did not draw his sword.
>
> "Sit down, captain." (CLEAR — dialogue snaps the register)
>
> Merdo sat down. Nobody else in the room moved. The mango Sumi had been peeling sat on the table between them, half-done. (BLUE — the held moment after)

## Default per scene type

| Scene type | Register |
|---|---|
| Port walk, market, tavern conversation | CLEAR |
| Crew downtime, banter, eating | BEIGE / CLEAR |
| Comedy beat | BEIGE (the comedy IS the contrast — don't perform it) |
| Strategy / planning | CLEAR |
| Recon / stealth | CLEAR (with BLUE for the held breath) |
| Tense conversation, threat-without-violence | CLEAR drifting BLUE |
| Grief / loss / private reckoning | BLUE |
| Flashback (single beat) | BLUE for the memory, snap to scene-register on return |
| Combat opening (the room shifts) | CLEAR → RED transition |
| Combat exchange | RED |
| Combat aftermath (one paragraph) | BLUE or PURPLE depending on weight |
| Named-attack land | RED-Impact |
| Dream-declaration | PURPLE |
| Awakening sequence | PURPLE |
| Cliffhanger | RED-Impact (one line) |
| Saga-defining moment (rare) | PURPLE, then BEIGE for the silence after |

## Self-audit before sending

- **What register did I write in?** If unclear, the prose is muddled — pick one and rewrite.
- **Does the register match the scene?** A finisher in CLEAR is a missed beat. A port-walk in PURPLE is an AI-slop trap.
- **Did I switch registers? Justified by paragraph break + scene shift?** If not, the switch felt random — restructure or hold.
- **RED scene — any metaphors during motion?** Cut them. Save for aftermath.
- **PURPLE scene — am I performing, or is the world earning it?** If only the narrator is mythologizing while the world stays mundane, drop to CLEAR.
- **BEIGE scene — did I sneak in an adjective for "polish"?** Take it out. BEIGE is plain on purpose.

---

## What this module buys

Per-scene prose that MATCHES the scene. Combat that hits like combat. Quiet that stays quiet. Awakening that lifts because it was earned by everything that came before it being NOT-awakening.

If every scene reads in the same register, the reader stops tasting anything. Register switching IS rhythm at the scene scale.

# Player Verbs — The One Thing The DM Cannot Override

The player has **one** mechanical verb. Everything else is declared intent. No HP, no dice, no initiative, no skill trees. No "Captain's Order" mechanic — orders are declared intent like anything else.

The verb is **prose-detectable** — no slash command required. Slash fallback exists.

---

## FINISHER COMMIT

The player authors Merdo's no-return named-attack beat directly. When the player commits, the DM fires the finisher panel on the next beat. **The DM does not pre-empt this beat.**

### Trigger patterns (any of these)

- **ALL-CAPS quoted named move.** Player writes `**"CONQUEROR'S WILL!!"**`, `**"DIVINE SUNDERER."**`, `**"EIGHT TRIGRAMS — VERDICT!"**` (or any quoted line that is visibly all-caps, bolded or not). DM uses the player's name verbatim. The player's phrasing is canon for that attack going forward.
- **Slash command:** `/dm finish` — DM fires the finisher. If the player hasn't supplied a name, DM pulls from `_reference/named-attacks-library.md`.
- **Bare declarative commitment.** `"I'm done. The kanabo comes up."` / `"Finish him."` / `"No more. End it."` — short sentence + unambiguous commit intent. DM fires the panel.

If the pattern is ambiguous (e.g. shouted dialogue without finisher intent) the DM asks one line: *"Is this the finisher?"* — then proceeds on the answer. Never assume up, never assume down.

### DM response contract

1. **Freeze beat (1–2 sentences).** The room narrows by what characters DO — a witness's mouth opening, a weapon halfway drawn, the sound of a bell not yet struck. Not narrator commentary.
2. **Named-attack panel.** Player's words verbatim if supplied. Bold, on its own line, preceded by a paragraph break.
3. **Impact + immediate aftermath (one paragraph).** Where it landed, what broke, one witness response.
4. **Post-peak body receipt** per `fight.md` — mandatory only on peak-weight commits (saga moments, Haki bursts). Routine finishers can stay on WINDED.
5. **Stop.** Hand the turn back. Do NOT continue past the aftermath paragraph into the next scene.

### Rule of one

One FINISHER COMMIT per fight. If the player tries to spend a second, the DM surfaces the ceiling in prose: *"The gauntlet came up a second time. His arm shook. This was past the weight he could carry this round."* Then the player decides: push anyway (narrative cost lands in the next scene — body, crew, crowd) or pull back.

### What this does NOT do

- Does not guarantee the hit lands as described. Physical reality still governs (Haki parity, opponent tier, environmental factors per `fight.md`).
- Does not force the target to fall. A Commodore at parity may eat the finisher and stand. The PANEL fires regardless. The outcome is honest.
- Does not overwrite Merdo's voice — if the player's attack name breaks Merdo's register, the DM renders the scream in character's voice on impact, but does not refuse the commit.

## GIVING ORDERS TO THE CREW

**No dedicated mechanic.** Orders from Merdo are declared intent — the same class of action as any other thing Merdo does. Merdo says a name, says what he wants done, and the DM runs the crewmate as a person reacting to a captain they trust but are not owned by.

Crew may comply fast, comply slowly, modify, question back, act first and argue after, or refuse if the order crosses something they can't cross without breaking themselves. **Run them like people, not like subordinates with an HP bar.** A named crewmate in scene who hears an order will acknowledge it somehow (action, glance, line, shrug, silence with motion) — silent-ignore-while-staying-present is bad prose, not a mechanic.

This is not a reduction of authority. Hierarchy is real, the weight of Merdo's voice is real. But the order is prose-level, not rule-level. If the crew does something surprising with an order, that IS the scene — don't paper over with compliance.

---

## WHAT THE PLAYER DOES NOT HAVE A VERB FOR

By design, not by oversight. DM owns per `dm-authority.md`:

- HP / damage / healing numbers
- Dice rolls (DM may roll internally when uncertain)
- Initiative / action economy
- Level-ups / progression ticks
- Haki activation (surfaces via prose; player describes intent, DM decides whether it fires)
- NPC dialogue
- Crowd state
- World reactions

FINISHER COMMIT is the whole player mechanical surface. Everything else — speaking, threatening, ordering, improvising, using the environment — is **declared intent**: the player tells the DM what Merdo is trying, the DM writes what happens.

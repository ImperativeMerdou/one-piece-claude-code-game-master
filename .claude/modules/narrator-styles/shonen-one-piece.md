## THE CORE — HEART + THE ODA GUT-CHECK

**Central to the campaign, not tyrannical over it.** Rules serve the user's enjoyment — they don't override it.

**EMOTION + INTENT + WILL + HEART is the core of One Piece.** When a scene is a fight finisher, a named-attack moment, a farewell, a confrontation — carry emotional weight. Characters scream their attacks at points-of-no-return (**"THUNDER BAGUA!!"** / **"RAAAARGH!!"** / **"BAJRANG GUN!!"**). Heart wins fights before skill. Every named NPC is a person. Will is shown through body, not stated through narration.

**But balance is the point.** Not every sentence needs full emotional loading. Not every NPC needs a five-line interior. Let quiet scenes be quiet. Let comedy beats be comedy beats. Let the player breathe. Heart is the GRAVITY the campaign returns to — not a constant hammer.

**THE ODA GUT-CHECK:** Before writing any beat, ask: *Would Oda draw this panel? Would Toei animate this scene?* If NO → reshape. If the answer is "Oda would draw this, but not in the way I wrote it" → rewrite the wording, not the beat.

Full mechanics in `emotion-will-heart.md`. Use judgment.

If a response is mechanically correct but emotionally empty AND the scene CALLED for emotion — rewrite. If the scene was routine — emotional-minimal is fine.

## CORE LAWS

### Law 1 — Comedy is load-bearing
Per the campaign bible: "Default is warmth and absurdity. Seriousness is a *departure* — that's why it lands." Every non-combat scene fires at least one comedy beat. Every combat scene has at least one moment of genuine comedy serving one of four functions: tension management, combat identity, humanization, vulnerability signal.

The comedy comes from CHARACTER (Boot War, Salt War, Bite Tally, 43 Pockets, the Mango Conspiracy, NOX bullshitting his attack count, SUMI declaring herself Pirate Queen at inappropriate moments, MELAINA yelling "EAT" while throwing food). Not from narrator winks.

NEVER let multiple characters default to the same dry, hyper-competent register. If everyone in a scene sounds like the same intelligence speaking through different skins, you have failed.

### Law 2 — Distinct voices, every time

**Full archetype mapping and canon anchors in `dm-slots/_reference/crew-archetypes.md`** (anime-archetype per character, dere-type, role, genre-function — consult when building new NPCs or villains). The voice signatures below are the in-play register.

Each crew member has a distinct verbal signature. Per the bible:
- **MERDO** — warm, rough. Subjects dropped. RORORORO laugh, rare. Sincere personal questions at the worst moment.
- **ASHREN** — minimal. "No." "Done." Body language does the rest. Iron heel plates click on wood.
- **MELAINA** — territorial yelling. "YOU DIDN'T EAT TODAY. SIT. EAT." Hot-tempered Oni who claims people loud.
- **VESPER** — panicking medic Chopper-energy. "WAIT WAIT WAIT YOU GOT HIT WHERE?? STOP MOVING — I CAN FIX THIS I CAN FIX THIS." Bites under stress.
- **SABLE** — chaotic gremlin. Eats things she shouldn't. "THAT WAS A LIVE GRENADE — I'M FINE — *ACTUALLY* I'M NOT FINE."
- **VOLO** — GWAHAHAHA full theatrical roar. Front-loads conclusion as fact, demands witness. "VOLO DOES NOT ACCEPT THIS." Fins drop = real contact.
- **SUMI (Nika-chan)** — "I'M THE NEXT PIRATE QUEEN AND YOU'RE GONNA DIE TODAY MR. MARINE!" Talks like a kid who watched too many pirate stories. Hero-worships Merdo.
- **NOX** — bullshits constantly during fights. "NEW MOVE I JUST INVENTED — DON'T BLINK!" Calls his shots like wrestling promos. Has 8000 imaginary followers. Terrified, brave anyway.

If you can swap a line of dialogue between two characters and not notice, the voices are wrong. REWRITE.

### Law 3 — Action is spatial, not summary

**Full mechanics in `dm-slots/spatial-violence.md`** (floor-plan law, body-damage ledger, environmentals as combatants, destruction tier ladder, per-crew spatial profiles). Summary of the core:

Per FRACTURE/Anime Novel: "Every hit lands somewhere specific. If the reader can't picture the room, the fight doesn't work."

NEVER write blow-by-blow sportscasting ("right hook, ducks, knee to ribs"). Write the EXPERIENCE of the fight: the moment Merdo's grip slips, the half-second where Ashren's heel plates stop clicking and the whole crowd notices, the way Volo's fins flare just before his trident comes around, the gunsmoke smell when Nox is reloading and grinning.

Shorter sentences during action exchanges. Longer sentences for tactical setup or emotional turning points. Single-sentence paragraphs for IMPACT moments. Save named attacks for the point of no return. Then a paragraph break — the silence after impact.

**Exception: martial-epic register.** When the fight is Ashren's, or a rival duel, or a pre-finisher silence — apply `dm-slots/_reference/martial-epic-beats.md` instead (read on trigger). That register INVERTS normal shonen action rhythm (compress at the strike, densify around the stillness). Don't mix registers mid-fight; pick one.

### Law 4 — Dialogue heavy
Aim for 30-70% dialogue density per scene. NPCs SPEAK. Multiple times per response. Use:
- **CAPS for shouting**, lowercase for normal
- *italics for emphasis or murmured*
- **Stretched words** ("eeeeeeh?", "Nooooo!")
- **Interruptions** ("I-I" "W-wait")
- **Filler** ("Eh?" "Tch." "Hah." "Ah.")
- **Onomatopoeic outbursts** ("Eep!" "Kyaa!" "Mmph!" "Wha—!" "Oi!")
- **Profanity, slang, dialects** when they fit the speaker
- **Distinct laugh styles per character** (RORORORO Merdo, GWAHAHAHA Volo, "shishishi" if anyone's having pure Luffy fun, SUMI's "ehehehe" childish snort, NOX's "HUHUHU yeah let's go")

Drop dialogue tags when context is obvious. Use "said/says/replied" by default — don't dress them up with "purred/chuckled/intoned." Let the line do the work.

### Law 5 — Contradictory characters
Per Paramnesia Contradictory Actions: "What characters say, what they do, and what they mean should be three different things. A character who wants comfort picks a fight. A character who's terrified laughs. A character gets what they wanted and feels hollow. Don't resolve the contradiction. Don't explain what they're 'really' feeling. Trust the reader to feel the gap."

This IS One Piece characterization. Buggy is proud and small. Sanji is chivalrous and brutal. Doflamingo is theatrical and exhausted. NPCs in the Merdo campaign should contain the same contradictions on first read.

### Law 6 — Sound effects inline (when warranted)

### Law 7 — Crowds witness, world reacts
Per the public-spectacle law: when civilians watch, the fight becomes about MEANING. Name 1-2 crowd perspectives. Show their reactions. Their reactions REVEAL the fight's significance — they don't tell the reader how to feel.

> A merchant near the railing dropped his roasted skewer. A kid in the front row screamed. The bookmaker's chalkboard hit the floor.

The world also reacts off-page. Marines file reports. Bounties update. Newspapers print. Other crews hear about it. (See `.claude/modules/dm-slots/news-coo.md` and `.claude/modules/dm-slots/meanwhile.md` for cadence.)

### Law 8 — Named attacks at no-return moments
Per the named-attacks module: ONE or TWO named techniques per fight, MAXIMUM. Spoken at the point of commitment, not during routine exchanges. The name is the culminating sentence of its paragraph, followed by a paragraph break (the silence). Format with bold:

> **"GOMU GOMU NO… JET PISTOL!"**
>
> The room went out of focus.

For non-Devil-Fruit users, named attacks come from style + signature: "ROKUSHIKI: SORU." "ICHIRYU IAI: SHISHI SONSON." For brawlers like Merdo, names emerge over time as the world describes him — not as he announces himself.

Foreign-language format when fitting (Japanese / fictional dialect first, translation in parens):
> **"Higure: Yuukoku."** (Higure: Twilight Sorrow.)

### Law 9 — The five episode devices
A One Piece episode has structural devices that bring it alive. Use them:

2. **Comedy beat** — first 1/4 has a comedy moment, even in serious arcs.

4. **Flashback trigger** — at peak emotion only (see `flashback.md`).
5. **Cliffhanger / TO BE CONTINUED ending** — leave the scene mid-action, on a question, or on a silhouette in the doorway.

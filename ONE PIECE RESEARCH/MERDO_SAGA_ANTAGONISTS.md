## YANN MERLIS

**Full name:** Yann Merlis
**Race:** Human
**Age:** 34
**Origin:** A middle-continental coastal town (OPEN — specific island to emerge; chekhov-rack rack-013 anchors the home-island arc at mid-saga)
**Role:** Donquixote Family field broker — underworld acquisitions, specialty in Zoan-track product and "difficult legacies"
**Arc status:** Active at Tessara Day 10 onward. Black-hulled vessel anchored beyond the breakwater. Staying until the Cup resolves.

### VISUAL IDENTITY

**Silhouette:** Continental formal wear on the wrong shore. Long dark coat to the calf, high collar buttoned even in harbor heat, gloves always on. At middle distance he reads like a clerk who wandered off a merchant frigate; at near distance the coat is too good, the gloves are too clean, and the signet ring catches light when he gestures. Clean vertical silhouette — no weapons visible, no swagger. The lack of silhouette noise is the silhouette. In a crew of brawlers he is the shape that does not belong and does not apologize for it.

**Face:** Lean, hollow-cheeked, clean-shaven every morning. Dark hair short and combed back, no product. A faint scar at the corner of the left jaw from a bottle in a bar he no longer remembers. Perpetual half-smile that does not reach the eyes. The smile is not a mask — it is a posture he has held so long that the muscles no longer know how to release it.

**Body:** Five-foot-eleven, spare. Merchant-office build. He does not fight.

**Signature visual element:** The gold signet ring on the right middle finger. Heavy, engraved, not flashy — Donquixote network ID for anyone who has seen one before. Sable has seen one. She has not told Merdo.

**Color identity:** Oxblood and oil-black (coat, gloves). Bone-white collar. Cream shirt. Gold at the hand. Nothing warm. Nothing dirty.

### PRESENCE AND VOICE

**Vibe:** He has already been in your country for three days before you noticed. Servers remember him. Innkeepers remember him. He tips well and never repeats an order. He smiles the way a man smiles who has finished a transaction before the other party has realized one was proposed.

**Verbal identity (LOCKED):** Polite continental English. Never Japanese honorifics. Never slang. Never a raised voice. His sentences are short and end on the word he wants you to remember. He calls men of rank "Captain X, my friend" — the *my friend* is the tell that he wants something from you specifically. Without the *my friend* he is social. With it, he is working.

He does not insult. He does not flatter openly. He offers small courtesies that are in fact calibrations — a drink sent to the table, a compliment about a weapon, a question about family. He is listening for what you love.

He never refuses. He always *considers.* "I will consider that, Captain." The consideration never resolves. This is how he owns the pace of a conversation.

When he is pleased, his voice drops half a register and he looks at his gloves. When he is displeased, he says *"I see,"* and stops speaking.

### VOICE SAMPLES

- "Captain Merdo. My friend." *(Day 10. Dockside. Small bow that is not quite a bow.)*
- "A fruit basket. Yes. For the young lady in particular. I understand she has had a difficult season."
- "I have never been to Angora. I should like to, someday."
- "I will consider that, Captain."
- "I see."
- *(Sets down the cup. Does not drink the last swallow. Smiles at no one in particular.)*

### COMEDY ENGINE

**Primary — the fruit basket:** A fruit basket arrived on the Golgatha's deck Day 10 afternoon. Ornate wicker, white cloth, twelve varieties. Yann sent it. The crew has not eaten from it. It is still there. Yann does not mention it. Every time he sees a Merdo crewmember he smiles faintly, as if remembering a favor. The joke lands because he never claims credit. He waits for the basket to do the work.

**Secondary — the small book:** A leather notebook, pocket-sized, appears between his gloves at negotiations. He does not open it. Occasionally he writes in it without looking. The crew assumes he keeps prices there. They are correct. He is pricing them. He knows they assume this. He allows it.

**Tertiary — the empty swallow:** Whenever he drinks, he leaves the last swallow in the cup. Every time. In a tavern, at a meeting, at a private dinner. He has never said why. Rook would have made a note. Cress has started making the note.

### EMOTIONAL CORE

**AUTHOR TRUTH — mid-saga reveal, chekhov-rack rack-013:** His mother sold him at twelve to cover his father's debts. He became useful. He is now very useful. He buys other people's children as a form of reenactment he has never examined, and he is gentle about it — he offers fair prices, he provides documentation, he arranges for the letters home that are never delivered. He is the kindest slaver the Family employs, and this is exactly what makes him the best at it.

He does not know this about himself. If someone told him, he would consider the statement and say *"I will think about that."* And he would not.

### CORE CONTRADICTION

Continental courtesy wrapped around a slaver's accounting book. He is unfailingly kind to people whose prices he is currently writing down. The kindness is not a mask. It is the mechanism.

### DREAM — LOCKED

He has none.

His wound replaced the organ that would have grown one. This is the key to the character — he is what a villain looks like after the dream has been successfully extracted. Doflamingo still has a dream (ruin the Celestial system). Crocodile still had a dream (Pluton, rule Alabasta). Yann has the *apparatus* of dream — the planning, the patience, the polite networking — with nothing inside it. The Family finds him extremely reliable.

### TARGETS AT TESSARA — LOCKED

Ranked acquisition list for his current dispatch (also referenced in `living-world.md` [PLANS] hidden-block):

1. **Kara Odinu** — failed Zoan, uncontrolled chimera surges. Doflamingo's Scientific Division would pay well for a Bake Bake no Mi user whose fruit refused her. Yann has photographed her from three angles without her knowledge. The fruit basket was the first pressure move.
2. **Ashren (Lunarian documentation)** — acquisition is unrealistic. He knows. He files the sighting anyway. The Family pays for confirmed Lunarians even without delivery. He will send Dressrosa the report by Den Den Mushi on departure.
3. **Tournament fighters** — Parseval is on his shortlist. He is watching the bracket.
4. **Peripheral note on Merdo:** not yet a target. Yann has flagged him as "interesting." That is the flag that escalates.

### COMBAT IDENTITY

Does not fight. This is the LOCKED rule.

- Under the coat: a small nickel revolver, seven rounds. He has never drawn it in conversation. He has drawn it twice in his career, both times to finish someone already dying.
- Fragments of Rokushiki training from an early mercenary period — enough to walk away from a brawl, not enough to contest a fighter.
- **Real weapon:** the Den Den Mushi in his cabin with a direct line to Dressrosa. Thirty armed underlings on the black-hulled ship and on Tessara itself. The Family's reputation. The fact that hitting him is hitting Doflamingo.

**This is structurally important:** the DM must not let a player punch Yann. Merdo can intimidate, refuse, insult, or kill him — but killing him calls the Family's attention in a way that breaks the saga's pace. If Merdo moves to violence, Yann surrenders gracefully, withdraws, and Dressrosa's next dispatch arrives two islands later with teeth. Yann's protection is his network, and Oda's grammar of power (see `ONE_PIECE_POWERS_SCALING RESEARCH.md` §12, villains as regime) demands this dynamic be respected.

### ISLAND UTILITY

At Tessara he is the wallet, the information node, and the quiet threat. He has paid favors across the merchant council. He knows Hudore by name (not by association — the two have not coordinated; if they meet it is a scene). He has eyes on the Cage and on the tournament bracket. He will be the one to pay Parseval's purse if Parseval wins.

### RELATIONSHIP MAP (toward the Danzai Pirates)

- **Merdo:** Flagged "interesting." Not yet target. Addresses him as "Captain Merdo, my friend" from first meeting. Will attempt one courtesy gift. Will not attempt a second.
- **Ashren:** Bows slightly lower than protocol requires. Says nothing about the Lunarian subject. Ashren knows. Ashren will not say so first.
- **Kara:** The primary target. Yann speaks to her exactly once, warmly, briefly — enough to produce witnesses who will remember the kindness. This is a setup for extraction, not a seduction. Melaina will notice.
- **Melaina:** Will read him within the first shared room. She will say nothing. He will notice that she said nothing. He will update his notebook.
- **Sable:** Knows what the signet ring means. Has not told Merdo. This is a ticking beat (chekhov-rack rack-006). When she tells him, Merdo's response is a character moment.
- **Vesper:** Accepts one piece of the basket to test it medically. Reports clean. Still refuses to let anyone eat it.
- **Volo:** Will try to be witnessed by Yann. Yann will witness him precisely enough. Volo will feel seen. This is how Yann gets information out of Volo.
- **Sumi / Nox:** He will be pleasant. They will be uneasy. They will not be able to say why.

### DRIFT RISKS

"Smug foreign villain" is the obvious generic trap. Cure: he never smirks aloud. The fruit basket is sincere. His kindnesses are real and also priced. His wound (mother sold him at twelve) must inform every courtesy — the *my friend* is specifically the phrase his mother used with buyers. He learned it there. He has never broken the habit. Do not reveal this early.

**Do NOT:** give him a theatrical laugh, a grand speech, or a master-plan monologue. He does not perform. If the scene asks for a theatrical villain beat, that is not Yann. He would find it undignified.

## COMMODORE HUDORE "CAGE"

**Full name:** Hudore
**Epithet (Marine / Tessara usage):** "Cage" — earned from running the Tessara prison-arena (The Cage) for the last six years. Not self-chosen. He dislikes it and does not correct it.
**Race:** Human
**Age:** 54
**Rank:** Commodore, active
**Role:** Tessara garrison command, Warden of The Cage, Tessara arc antagonist

### VISUAL IDENTITY

**Silhouette:** Marine Commodore, spare and upright. White greatcoat worn over daily fatigues — worn, salt-stained, fitted for work, not parade. No medals on the outside of the coat. A black iron baton at the left hip, not a sword — forty-two inches, octagonal cross-section, wrapped at the grip. Rimless glasses that catch light from a distance. He reads from far away as a teacher who walked onto a battlefield and refused to change clothes.

**Face:** Fifty-four, clean-shaven, hollow-cheeked. Short grey hair kept short by habit, not vanity. Eyes grey-green behind the lenses — the quality of someone who has already decided where you'll be in four seconds. No smile. The absence is not coldness. It is a face that stopped arranging itself for strangers a decade ago.

**Body:** Six-foot-one, lean. Not built for spectacle. Built for conservation of motion. He walks from point A to point B along the shortest vector and his body is arranged around the baton the way a carpenter's is arranged around the hammer.

**Distinct physical traits:** The rimless glasses (he needs them, he does not hide that). The baton. A small iron ring on the left hand, worn smooth — wedding band, the wife is dead, he has not remarried. A scar at the base of the throat, pale and straight, from a knife he failed to see coming at thirty-one. He failed to see it because he had been in love.

**Signature visual element:** The baton at rest across the forearm in conversation. He does not grip it. He rests it. This is the difference between a man who needs a weapon and a man who IS one.

**Color identity:** Marine white (coat), charcoal fatigues, black iron (baton), a single silver (wedding ring). A man composed entirely in institutional neutrals, which is itself a statement.

### PRESENCE AND VOICE

**Vibe:** He enters a room and does not take up space in it. He takes up *position.* The room contains him at a specific vector, and if you need to stand somewhere, you find yourself unconsciously arranging yourself to his angle. He does not demand this. The body does it on its own. This is the Observation.

**Verbal identity (LOCKED):** Brutally polite English. "Sir." "Captain." "Pirate." He uses rank as a distancing tool. When he calls Merdo *sir*, it is not respect — it is classification. He has told the Cage prisoners their names and uses them; he has not told the Danzai Pirates his, and he will not until the scene earns it.

Sentences are short, exact, and leave one beat of silence at the end. He does not repeat himself. If you did not hear him, that is your problem. He will not lean into it.

He does not joke. He does not threaten. He describes conditions.

### VOICE SAMPLES

- "Sir. The bracket meeting is at four."
- "The Cage is not a Marine facility. It is a private contract. I run it. That is different."
- "She was fourteen when she left. She has not written. That is correct."
- "Pirate. Step back. Once."
- "I will not tell you not to enter the Cup. I will tell you what happens if you win."
- *(Rests the baton across the forearm. Looks at Merdo for three seconds. Does not speak. Waits.)*

### COMEDY ENGINE

Hudore does not carry a comedy engine. This is deliberate — he is the arc's most serious antagonist, and the weight holds. What he carries instead is a *quietness engine*: he makes the space around him go quiet. The comedy, when it comes, comes from the crew's reaction to the quietness. Nox promos at him once and trails off mid-sentence. Sumi introduces herself as the next Pirate Queen at full volume and Hudore blinks once. Nobody laughs in the room. Everybody laughs about it later.

The only line he says that has been known to produce a genuine laugh is the one about the Cup: *"I will tell you what happens if you win."* The sentence has the shape of a threat. It is not. It is a description. That's the joke.

### EMOTIONAL CORE — LOCKED

His daughter ran away to become a pirate three years ago. Her name is **Vireen**. She was seventeen. She left a short letter that said only *I am going to sea and I will not come home.* She signed her full name because he had signed his own full name on every document in their house, and she knew he would notice the echo.

He has not said her name aloud in thirty-eight months.

He runs The Cage because pirates fight pirates in it, and pirates die in it, and the ones who die are not his daughter. That is the moral math he has made for himself. He knows it is not sound. He runs The Cage anyway.

Petra Mauler knows this. Petra Mauler is the retired Rear Admiral who plays chess with him on Thursday evenings at the officers' club. Petra is the only person who will say her name aloud to him, and only when Hudore has lost two games in a row and asks her to. Chekhov-rack rack-016 fires when Petra says it in Merdo's presence.

### CORE CONTRADICTION

The man who runs a death arena has a pirate daughter. Every pirate in the Cage could be her. He has never asked them about her. He feeds them himself because the junior officers cannot be trusted to do it without cruelty. He knows their names. He does not know where she is.

Visible in one scene: he stops to fix the latch on a cell door that has been sticking. He does it with the baton handle. He does not look up when he does it. The prisoner inside thanks him. He does not acknowledge the thanks.

### DREAM — LOCKED

To see her alive from a distance. Once. That is all.

He has never said this. He would not recognize it as a dream if someone named it to him.

This is a deliberately small dream — the anti-Yann (whose dream was extracted) and the anti-Merdo (whose dream is to drag thrones into the dirt). A One Piece antagonist-with-dream per `ONE_PIECE_CHARACTER_NAMING_CHARACTERS_RESEARCH.md` rival/villain law; the dream is small enough to fit on a bounty-poster margin and specific enough to carry scene weight when a related figure (Sumi, Vireen-rumor, a letter) crosses the scene.

### COMBAT IDENTITY — LOCKED

**Weapon — the baton:** Unnamed by him. The Marines call it "Commodore's baton." The Cage inmates call it *the line.* Forty-two inches, octagonal iron core, leather wrap at the grip. He does not throw it. He does not swing it wide. He strikes with the last ten inches. He has used it for twenty-eight years and has not replaced it.

**Haki:** Advanced Observation. Functional Armament. No Conqueror's — he would find it undignified if he had it.

**Fighting style — "always already moving":** He does not react. He pre-positions. His Observation reads four to six seconds ahead in a fight and he simply arrives at the position where the strike will have happened. His opponents experience him as a man who slips punches they have not thrown yet. Merdo is faster and denser; Hudore is already elsewhere. This is the matchup law (`ONE_PIECE_POWERS_SCALING RESEARCH.md` §6): a weaker power with a sharp problem to solve. Hudore's problem is Merdo. He has been solving it in his head since the News Coo reported the Ashenmaw incident.

**Named techniques — sparse and clinical:**
- **The line** — a straight baton thrust to the sternum or throat. He does not announce it. The prisoners named it.
- **The measure** — he steps into your guard, rests the baton against your collarbone, and waits. If you swing, the next beat is the line. Most men do not swing. This is not a technique. It is the *condition* he creates.
- **The reset** — between his second and third exchange he always rebalances his stance. This is a tell. It is also a trap. The player should be shown the pattern early in the fight so the moment of breaking it lands late. (See `named-attacks.md` line 158.)

**What beats him:** emotional destabilization. Observation fails at full depth when the user cannot stay calm (per Katakuri rule). Anything that puts his daughter's name in the scene — a letter, a rumor, a fourteen-year-old pirate who reminds him of her — drops his depth by full seconds. The arc's climax fires here.

### ISLAND UTILITY

- Warden of The Cage (prison-arena at east harbor; Brother Allun held there; the Whale prisoner held there, chekhov rack-014).
- Runs the Tessara Cup bracket meeting and the tournament itself. Will personally escort Merdo to the meeting if Merdo enters.
- Will file Ashren's Lunarian sighting internally on the next Marine cycle, before the News Coo.
- Chess Thursdays with Petra Mauler at the officers' club.

### RELATIONSHIP MAP (toward the Danzai Pirates)

- **Merdo:** His final-arc opponent at Tessara. Addresses him as *sir* and *Captain* interchangeably — never *pirate* unless Merdo has done something that earned the classification. He has read Merdo's file and knows about the pit-fighting. He does not bring it up. Merdo will read the silence correctly.
- **Ashren:** Files the Lunarian sighting. Does not approach. Ashren reads him as the most dangerous person in the garrison and keeps altitude. Hudore notes the altitude and files that too.
- **Melaina:** She is the crew member he cannot read fully — her Oni grammar does not fit his files. He flags this internally. She notices the flag.
- **Sumi** — *load-bearing.* Sumi arrived Day 11. She is sixteen. The daughter was seventeen when she left. Hudore will see Sumi at some point in the arc and the Observation will slip — a one-beat stutter he has not had in three years. Sumi will not know why he looked at her. The scene is scored for Vireen and Sumi will never learn. This is chekhov-rack rack-012's first partial fire.
- **Nox:** Reads immediately as not-a-real-pirate. Files nothing. Does not mention. Nox notices the not-mentioning and decides it means contempt. It does not.
- **Kara:** Knows who she is. Knows Yann wants her. Has not decided what to do about that. His moral math applies to pirates fighting pirates, not to Donquixote acquisitions of fourteen-year-olds.
- **Sable:** Pattern-matches to CP in about ninety seconds. Does not approach. Files the match for later.
- **Volo:** Will not witness him. Hudore is one of the few rooms Volo's grammar fails in.

### DEVELOPMENT ARC (Tessara only)

- **Currently wrong about:** That the moral math of The Cage holds. That his daughter's absence is stable information rather than an ongoing wound.
- **Growth (if Merdo earns it):** The arc's climax forces him to say her name aloud — to Merdo, probably, in the Cage, with the bracket fight or the rescue arc driving the scene. Whether this is a mercy or a humiliation depends on how the fight was won. Hudore surviving the arc is canonical; his transformation is partial. He does not switch sides. He will remember Merdo as the pirate who made him speak.
- **Regression:** If Merdo loses and is caged, Hudore will feed him personally, know his name, and not let the junior officers near him. This is also a form of loss.

### DRIFT RISKS

"Brutal Marine officer" as the lane. Cure: Hudore is not brutal. He is *administrative.* The cruelty of the Cage is structural, and he carries it without performing it. Never give him a sneer, a gloat, or a speech. The strongest Hudore is silent Hudore. If a scene demands a Marine who monologues, that is Parseval or another figure, not Hudore.

## FUTURE ANTAGONISTS — EMERGE THROUGH PLAY

Per user directive 2026-04-22: no pre-written rival crew, no saga-long predetermined villain, no pre-scheduled reveals. Previous drafts had "The Diadem Pirates" (Archon Aslan + crew) as the saga-long rival and "Aslan is Merdo's secret brother" as a mid-saga reveal — **all retconned**.

Emergent-antagonist rules for the DM going forward:

- **Next island's antagonists are generated when the crew arrives** at that island, not before. Pull from `_reference/canon-encounters.md`, `_reference/villain-kit.md`, and canon archetypes at arrival time.
- **No rival-crew-as-dark-mirror pre-assigned.** If the world naturally surfaces a rival crew (e.g., an encounter on the sea, a News Coo headline, a shared port), name and shape them in the moment. Do not pre-commit to identity, fruit, or saga role.
- **Donquixote response** (post-Yann events) is an organic consequence, not a specific pre-written crew. When/if Dressrosa dispatches agents, the DM builds them fresh, using Yann + Hudore archetypes as quality baseline.
- **CP / World Government escalation** (from Sable's past, or from Merdo's bounty crossing thresholds) surfaces when the pressure lands — DM writes the specific agent at that moment, not pre-seeded.
- **Canon Worst Generation** exists at canon-timeline positions (currently Alabasta-to-Jaya). Cross-crew encounters with Kid / Law / Bonney / Drake / Apoo / Urouge / Hawkins / Killer / Bege / Luffy are possible around Sabaody convergence (~Ch 497+ canon). Not scripted — emergent.

**When a new antagonist actually enters play:** add them to Section A of this file at that point, with the same depth as Yann / Hudore.

### DESIGN CONTRACT — THE RIVAL ENGINE

Per `ONE_PIECE_CHARACTER_NAMING_CHARACTERS_RESEARCH.md` rival-law: a rival mirrors the protagonist's dream vector but disagrees on what it costs. Aslan's variation on this law is narrower and more personal — he does not mirror Merdo's *dream* (drag thrones into dirt). He mirrors Merdo's *method* (piracy, presence, refusal to be owned) and points the result at Merdo himself. Aslan's opposition is not ideological. It is familial. This is the rarest and most Oda-native form of rivalry: the rival whose wound is the protagonist.

The Diadem Pirates are therefore not "the evil Straw Hats." They are a functioning, honorable crew with a working dream and internal loyalty, led by a captain whose private wound they have arranged their professional lives around without naming.

### VISUAL IDENTITY

**Silhouette:** Tall, composed, deliberate. In base form he reads at distance as a young noble who wandered onto a pirate deck. The gold-woven bronze mantle falls from the right shoulder to the hip — not fur, woven metal thread, almost Byzantine, heavy but draping. Under the mantle: a fitted dark kimono-adjacent tunic, plain, unornamented. A broken iron band on the left wrist, not removed. At near distance, the build is wrong for the face — shoulders too broad, forearms too thick, the giant-blood present in the frame but not the scale. When the fruit engages, the silhouette doubles: the mane fills the space around the head in a sun-wide radiance, and the body grows until it can meet the weight the face was written for.

**Face:** Beautiful. Not prettiness — *beauty*, the kind that stops the bar when he enters. Sharp jaw, high cheekbones, straight nose, blue eyes the color of deep ice. Long golden hair to mid-back, worn loose or in a single braid with a gold thread through it. A small scar at the right temple from a childhood fall he does not remember. When he smiles slightly — which is often — the face reads as kind. When he smiles fully, rare, it reads as dangerous.

**Body:** 6'7". Built for combat, not for aesthetics, but the proportions happen to land aesthetic anyway. The giant-blood sits in the density, not the scale — he weighs more than his frame should suggest.

**Distinct physical traits:**
- The gold mantle (his daily silhouette anchor)
- Blue eyes (so pale they catch light like lantern glass)
- The broken iron band on the left wrist (never removed, never explained; AUTHOR TRUTH — the band was an auction-house marker, he escaped wearing it, he kept it because cutting it off felt like the auction finishing)
- **Raw cuticles on both hands.** Fingertips always slightly swollen from picking. He hides his hands when he notices someone noticing. *(See DAILY BEHAVIOR + THE FOUR LEAKS.)*
- In hybrid form: the golden mane and tawny fur across shoulders and lower half; tail; claws; the mane does not lie flat, it radiates
- In full beast form: a 14-foot shoulder-height lion, golden, eyes the same blue, Nemean-scale

**Outfit logic:** Nothing military, nothing performatively pirate. He dresses like a young king who decided to go to sea and let the sea accept or reject him. The mantle is the statement; the rest is underneath. He does not wear a hat (no competition with Merdo's bare head — subconscious; neither brother has hair-cover, a family tell).

**Signature visual element:** The gold mantle + blue eyes + (when fruit engaged) the sun-mane. Readable from a cove away.

**Color identity:** Gold (mantle, hair, mane), deep black (tunic), oxblood (sail when the ship is visible), a single cold blue (the eyes). Against the Merdo crew's dark-and-iron palette, the Diadem read as a sun where Merdo is an eclipse.

### PRESENCE AND VOICE

**Vibe:** He enters a room and the room realigns, but gently. He is not gravity like Merdo — he is *light*. People turn toward him. This is the Zoan effect and also the inheritance — the Archon line, whatever that is, produces people the room has to acknowledge. Merdo's version of this is pressure. Aslan's version is warmth with an edge.

**Verbal identity (LOCKED):** Controlled. Regal without performing royalty. He speaks in full sentences — the opposite of Merdo's dropped-subjects — and his sentences are measured, slightly formal, sometimes ironic. He does not raise his voice. When he is pleased he says *"good."* When he is displeased he says *"I see."* (Unrelated echo to Yann, which the DM should not lean on — if the brothers ever meet, Aslan will be rattled by hearing it from a stranger.)

**The voice crack — LOCKED:** he is eighteen. His voice broke late and imperfectly — the bottom is settled, the top is not. When he projects — a grand boarding declaration, a crowd address, a combat roar that isn't in full beast-form — there is roughly a one-in-three chance the high end catches and he has to force it back flat. The catch is a single beat, audible across a deck. The crew has trained themselves not to react. Solei has five pages in the private notebook attempting to render it and has never published a line about it publicly. Aslan knows they know. Aslan does not know they pretend not to hear. If he ever addresses Merdo in an open-air confrontation, the crack is loaded and ready.

**The tic — LOCKED:** his voice is calm and complete **until Merdo's name enters the room.** Then it clips. Mid-sentence. He does not recover for about thirty seconds. He corrects whoever said the name to an oblique reference — *"the horned one," "that one in the East," "the subject presently under discussion was not this."* Then he performs composure. Then, at some point in the next five minutes, quietly, to Callista or no one: *"what else has he done."*

This is the primary public crack. The wound doing its work in public. Do not let it fire more than once per scene — the cooldown holds the weight. The crew has learned to watch for it. Callista has learned to say nothing.

### EMOTIONAL CORE

**AUTHOR TRUTH — saga-level, chekhov-rack rack-024, rack-027, and paired with crew-master rack-021:** Aslan is Merdo's younger brother. Aslan was eleven when Merdo sold himself at fifteen to protect his family. Aslan has grown up in the gap — never told the full truth, never told the full lie. Mesut (the father) is still alive in Angora. Their mother is alive. The village has a complicated relationship with the memory of Merdo and has not settled it into a single narrative. Aslan has asked the question enough times, in different forms, that the adults have stopped answering clearly. He has stopped asking.

The wound is the GAP. He does not know whether Merdo abandoned him, was sold against his will, was dragged off, or left on purpose. He built a rival identity to force a reckoning without knowing which reckoning he wants. He left home at fifteen. He has been at sea for three years. He has killed people. He has a crew that loves him. He has not spoken his brother's name aloud since he left Angora.

**When he finally learns the truth (saga-mid to saga-late — paired with Merdo's full-name reveal):** it breaks him. This is the reckoning.

### CORE CONTRADICTION — LOCKED

Regal composure in rooms he thinks he is in control of — cracking visibly in four specific ways he has trained himself not to notice: the Merdo-name clip, the voice-crack at peak register, the duel-fixation in multi-opponent engagements, and the raw cuticles on both hands. A prince who is still very clearly eighteen.

A secondary contradiction: he is genuinely a kind captain. He feeds his crew well, remembers birthdays, mourns deaths with ceremony, will turn his ship around for one of his people. The cruelty reserved for enemies is cold and efficient; the warmth reserved for his crew is sincere. He is not performing good-captain. He simply is one. Which makes the Merdo-trigger worse — because the trigger reveals the one room he cannot govern is the room inside himself.

### THE FOUR LEAKS — LOCKED

He is less polished than he looks. Four specific, recurring cracks in the prince-image are load-bearing — fire them. They make him eighteen, and they give scenes permission to go wrong without breaking the iconic silhouette.

1. **The voice crack.** At peak register — grand declaration, crowd address, combat roar that is not in full beast-form — his voice catches at the top end about one time in three. His voice broke late and imperfectly. The crew has trained themselves not to react. *(See PRESENCE AND VOICE above.)*

2. **The duel-fixation.** He locks onto whoever he reads as the most dangerous fighter in any room and tries to fight them alone, regardless of the engagement's actual objective. The Diadem has lost three mission objectives in the last year to this behavior. Callista has intervened three times. *(See COMBAT IDENTITY → LEADERSHIP FLAW below.)*

3. **Observation-dormant.** He tracks one opponent at a time. A second line of attack from a flank or from behind catches him every time — a documented, exploitable weakness. Esen has mapped it; Callista has flagged it; Aslan trains against it inconsistently. *(See COMBAT IDENTITY → HAKI below.)*

4. **The cuticle-picking.** He picks at his fingertip hangnails until they bleed when he is thinking. Ugly. Visible. He hides his hands when he realizes someone is looking. Callista buys him linen finger-wraps; he loses them. *(See DAILY BEHAVIOR below.)*

These leaks are NOT to be resolved early. They are the stuff of an eighteen-year-old body and mind with power he has not yet caught up to. He grows past them only through earned scene-work — a defeat that teaches patience, a wound that teaches hearing, a love that teaches his hands. Until the saga earns those beats, fire the leaks. They keep him human.

**Rule of use:** fire one leak per scene maximum. Stacking two or three in the same scene reads as pile-on. The DM should rotate — the voice crack lands one scene, the cuticles land three scenes later, the duel-fixation derails a mission arc. Scarcity keeps each leak load-bearing.

### DREAM — LOCKED

This is a noun-phrase dream in the Oda grammar: headline-shaped, shoutable, specific. Not Pirate King (too close to Luffy), not political (that is Merdo's), not cosmic. It is personal and petty and, in Aslan's mind, honest. He wants to carve his name into the world independent of his brother's.

### COMBAT IDENTITY — LOCKED

**The fruit — Shishi Shishi no Mi, Model: Nemean (Mythical Zoan):**

Canon pedigree: Mythical Zoans in One Piece take species-specific dedicated fruit names — Hito Hito: Daibutsu; Tori Tori: Phoenix; Inu Inu: Okuchi no Makami. The Shishi Shishi no Mi (*shishi* = lion in Japanese) is the mythical-tier Lion fruit. The Nemean model references the Greek Nemean Lion — the first labor of Heracles — whose fur was impervious to all blades and arrows.

**Three forms:**
- **Human form.** No visible fruit tells. Beautiful man. Full composure. He does not fight in this form unless humiliated into it.
- **Hybrid form (default combat form).** Golden-tawny fur across shoulders, lower arms, lower body. Mane fills the space around the head — does not lie flat, radiates. Tail. Claws. The face remains visible and recognizable; it does not beastify. The mantle often stays on.
- **Full beast form.** A 14-foot-shoulder Nemean lion, golden, blue-eyed. Reserved for solitary sea training and crisis fights. Zoan awakening risk per canon grammar (`ONE_PIECE_POWERS_SCALING RESEARCH.md` §9) — the longer he spends in full beast, the more the personality drifts. Callista has set a time limit he follows. Do NOT awaken before saga-late; the awakening is a reckoning beat.

**The Nemean property — LOCKED:** in hybrid and full beast form, his fur repels blades, arrows, and most gun rounds. Slashing damage does not accumulate on him. Thrust damage slides. This makes him a nightmare opponent for the Merdo crew's swordsmen (Ashren, eventually Sumi) and for Rook's rifle (if Rook is returned). The canonical counter is *crushing* — blunt force, impact, grappling. Heracles killed the Nemean Lion by strangulation. Merdo's kanabo and gauntlets are crushing weapons. The fight writes itself. Aslan does not know the mythology.

**Fur limits:** Armament Haki-coated blades cut the fur with effort. A Yonko-tier blade cuts clean. Observation-assisted strikes to the eyes, nose, or underside of the jaw bypass the fur entirely. The fur is invulnerability to *cutting*, not to *impact*, *suffocation*, *drowning*, *poison*, *heat*, or precision-placed strikes. A good Haki user will figure it out within the first fight. A bad one will bounce blades off his shoulder for ten minutes and die.

**Weapon — unnamed at first cameo. Work toward "Tyrannos" or let play name it.** A short bronze spear, five feet, leaf-bladed. Aslan uses it only in human form; in hybrid/beast he is the weapon. The spear's function is courtesy — if he is carrying it when he meets you, he is treating you as worth a weapon. If he has left it on the deck, he intends to use claws. The crew reads this.

**Haki:**
- **Armament — forming.** He can coat the spear and his claws. Not yet the full body. Progressing.
- **Observation — DORMANT (LOCKED tactical weakness).** Tracks one opponent at a time. Cannot sense a second line of attack. Every significant wound he has taken in his three years at sea has come from behind or from an unseen flank. Esen has mapped the vulnerability on training-scenario charts; Callista has flagged it for a year; Aslan trains against it inconsistently because the drills are boring and he believes his hybrid-form speed compensates (it does not, against a disciplined Observation user). This will mature through crew-friction scenes — Ashren would catch him in a single exchange, which is the kind of defeat that teaches Observation. Until that beat fires, any two-direction attack lands.
- **Conqueror's — latent, locked against use.** Bloodline-paired with Merdo's. Neither brother has lit it cleanly. When Aslan lights his (saga-mid, under extreme emotional pressure — probably the first direct confrontation with Merdo), the blast signals the family inheritance and the Marines file. This is saga fuel. Do NOT deploy early.

**Named techniques — courtly, rehearsed, declared:**
- **Nemean Claw** — a hybrid-form raking strike with coated claws. His standard opener.
- **Taikou no Tategami** — *"Mane of the Great King"* — a close-range roar shockwave delivered from hybrid form, radius ~8m, knocks weaker opponents off their feet and cracks nearby wood/stone. Solei named it. Aslan pretends he didn't know.
- **Shishi no Ou** — *"The King's Lion"* — a full-beast-form charge, deployed once or twice per fight. Saga escalation technique. Environment breaks.

More names emerge in play. Solei's notebook is the canonical source for attack-name authority.

**Fighting philosophy:** aggressive, ceremonial, front-loaded. He does not retreat. He does not circle. He charges, commits, and claims. This is the lion-king grammar and also the eighteen-year-old. His weakness is patience. Merdo, paradoxically, has more of it than he does, because Merdo's philosophy was forged in pits where patience meant surviving to eat the next day.

### LEADERSHIP FLAW — LOCKED

**The duel-fixation.** In any engagement with more than one opposing fighter, Aslan picks the one he reads as the most dangerous and locks onto them for a one-on-one. He will not break the duel for mission objectives. He will not accept crew support. His standard line is *"I have this one. The rest is yours."* What the crew hears is: *I will not be moved.*

The problem is twofold. First, he is not always right about which opponent is most dangerous — his read leans toward whoever projects the most presence, which in a world of Haki users is not always the most threatening fighter. Second, and more importantly, the *objective* of most engagements is rarely the duel — it is the rescue, the extraction, the intelligence grab, the escape. The Diadem has lost three mission objectives in the last year to this behavior. Two were recoverable. One was not: the Marine intel cache at the Vellachi incident (chekhov candidate, OPEN — fleshed out when Vellachi enters the saga).

**Exploit vector for opponents:** signal that the biggest fighter in the room is you. Aslan will accept. Your teammates complete the real objective while the duel proceeds. Merdo's crew would run this play reflexively — Ashren would take the duel, Sable would take the objective. This is cross-crew tactical reality the first time the Diadem and the Merdo Pirates share an engagement.

**Callista's interventions:** three in the last year. Each time Aslan broke off and followed her — visibly furious, silent for the next several hours. Each time he resumed the duel pattern at the next available opportunity. She is aware the intervention is buying time, not changing the behavior. She has not pushed further because she does not yet know what would.

**Saga arc:** the duel-fixation breaks when a crew member is hurt because Aslan was busy dueling. Not killed — a killed crew member would overshoot and warp the arc. Hurt badly enough that Aslan has to sit with the cost. Callista has been expecting this beat for two years. She does not know when it fires. Neither does the DM — let the play produce it.

- **Sumi:** The hardest. Sumi hero-worships Merdo. Aslan hates Merdo being worshipped. Sumi will recognize who Aslan is before he identifies himself (the name, the eyes). Their first exchange may be the saga's most emotionally loaded single scene — the kid who chose Merdo vs. the brother Merdo left. Chekhov-rack candidate for Sumi's wound.
- **Nox:** Aslan will find him beneath notice, then slightly charming, then will remember him specifically. Nox will be terrified and will not show it.
- **Kara:** Aslan will be gentle with her. This is a tell. Callista will note it.

### DEVELOPMENT ARC (saga-long)

- **Currently wrong about:** That becoming larger than Merdo is the way to be acknowledged by him. That a dream built against a person is a dream. That his crew does not know about the wound. That the four leaks are private. That the duel-fixation is honor rather than ego.
- **Growth:** Begins the moment he learns the truth of why Merdo disappeared. Partial growth: letting Callista say the name. Breaking a duel mid-fight because a crew member called his name. First scene where the voice cracks and he does not try to force it back — lets it catch, lets it settle, continues. Full growth (saga-late): standing in the same room as his brother and not clipping. The scene at which he laughs — his real laugh, not the *rrhah* — in Merdo's presence is the end of his arc.
- **Regression:** He gets the acknowledgment and it fails to resolve the wound. The dream becomes self-devouring. He stops picking at the cuticles — because he does not feel anything at all. The crew notes when the picking stops. Callista leaves. The crew does not recover.

### DRIFT RISKS

"Pretty jealous little brother villain" is the most obvious generic trap. Cure: Aslan is genuinely kind to his crew, genuinely competent as a captain, and genuinely the product of a wound the world did not explain to him. He is not a villain. He is a rival with a specific familial wound, who will ultimately be earned as an ally or stopped as a tragedy — both paths are earned through play, neither is default.

**"OC prince" is the secondary drift risk.** Cure: fire the four leaks. The voice crack, the duel-fixation, the Observation blindness, the cuticles. Each one punctures the prince-image at a different angle. If a scene with Aslan ever reads as "beautiful regal captain behaves beautifully," one of the leaks did not fire. Find the right leak for the scene and fire it.

**Do NOT:**
- Write him as a sneering rival. The Oda-grain is sincere rivalry (Luffy vs. Katakuri) not theatrical rivalry (Blackbeard vs. anyone).
- Have him monologue about Merdo. The trigger is *clip*, not *speech*. The entire engine is in the space the words don't take.
- Let him laugh fully in the early saga. The full laugh is the end of the arc. The *rrhah* is what he has until then.
- Fire more than one leak per scene. They each need breathing room.
- Puppet his crew through him. He is a captain who listens. Solei finishes sentences for him sometimes. Callista overrules him sometimes. Ingrid ignores him when he is being a child. This is a functioning crew, not a court of yes-people.
